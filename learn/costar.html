The COSTAR Method

A comprehensive framework that emphasizes audience awareness and output format. Ideal for professional content creation.

Think of these as a checklist, not a template. You don't write "Objective: ..." in your prompt. Instead, make sure your natural request includes your objective. The method ensures you don't forget important elements.

Why COSTAR?

The framework that puts your audience first

COSTAR excels when audience matters. Unlike CRISP, which focuses on the task, COSTAR ensures your output is tailored to who will receive it.

Professional Communications

Emails, reports, and presentations that need to connect with specific recipients.

Marketing & Sales

Content that must persuade and resonate with target demographics.

User Documentation

Guides and help content written for specific user groups.

Published Content

Blog posts, articles, and content that will be shared publicly.

COSTAR vs. CRISP

CRISP uses "Parameters" for output constraints. COSTAR splits this into "Audience" and "Response" for more nuanced control. Choose COSTAR when the recipient significantly affects how you should write.

C - Context

Provide the background the AI needs to understand your situation

Include relevant details about your business, project, or circumstances. The more context you provide, the more tailored and relevant the output will be.

Strong Context Example

"I'm organizing our neighborhood's annual summer potluck. Last year we had some issues with too many desserts and not enough main dishes. This year I want to coordinate better so we have a balanced meal."

O - Objective

State your goal clearly and the action you want

What outcome do you want from this content? What should the reader think, feel, or do after engaging with it?

Inform

"Help readers understand the new policy changes"

Persuade

"Encourage neighbors to bring a variety of dishes"

Instruct

"Guide users through the onboarding process"

Engage

"Build excitement about our upcoming product launch"

S - Style

Define the writing approach and voice

Style refers to the writing approach. You can reference known styles or describe the characteristics you want.

Reference Styles

"Write like The Economist" or "In the style of Harvard Business Review"

Characteristic Styles

"Concise and data-driven" or "Narrative and story-based"

T - Tone

The emotional quality of your writing

Tone is how your content "feels" to the reader. Consider the spectrum of options for your context.

Formal
Casual
Serious
Playful
Reserved
Enthusiastic
Authoritative
Collaborative
Tone Example

"Use an empathetic and reassuring tone. We're delivering news about service changes, so acknowledge potential concerns while focusing on benefits."

A - Audience

Who will read this? Their characteristics shape everything.

Define your audience in detail. The better you describe them, the more the AI can tailor content to their needs, expectations, and level of understanding.

Knowledge Level

Technical experts vs. general audience

Role/Position

Executives vs. end users vs. developers

Relationship

Existing customers vs. prospects vs. partners

Concerns

What questions or objections might they have?

Audience Definition Example

"The audience is my neighbors - a mix of families with kids, retirees, and young professionals. They're busy people who want easy recipes. Some have dietary restrictions we need to consider."

R - Response

Specify the format and structure of your output

Define exactly what form the output should take. Be specific about format, length, and required elements.

Format

Email, report, blog post, script, outline

Length

Word count, page count, time to read

Structure

Headers, bullet points, numbered lists

Elements

Include summary, CTA, examples, data

1 Start

Write a press release.

↓
2 + Context

We're a Series B startup that just closed a $50M funding round led by a top-tier VC. We plan to use the funds to expand our engineering team and enter the European market. Write a press release.

↓
3 + Objective

We're a Series B startup that closed a $50M round from a top VC for engineering expansion and European market entry. Create a press release that builds credibility, attracts potential customers and talent, and positions us as a market leader.

↓
4 + Style & Tone

We closed a $50M Series B for expansion. Create a press release that builds credibility and attracts customers and talent. Use a professional, confident tone that's newsworthy without being boastful. Write in standard press release format with a strong headline and dateline.

↓
5 + Audience

We closed a $50M Series B. Create a press release to build credibility and attract talent. Professional, confident tone in standard press release format. Primary audience is tech journalists and industry analysts. Secondary audience is potential enterprise customers and job candidates.

↓
6 + Response

We closed a $50M Series B. Create a press release for tech journalists, analysts, enterprise customers, and job candidates. Professional, confident tone. Format: Headline, subhead, dateline, 3-4 paragraphs, quotes from CEO and lead investor, boilerplate about company. Keep under 500 words. Include contact info placeholder.

1 Start

Write a policy announcement.

↓
2 + Context

We're transitioning from fully remote to a hybrid model requiring 3 days in-office starting next quarter. Some employees are concerned, and we need to communicate this carefully. Write a policy announcement.

↓
3 + Objective

We're moving to a hybrid model (3 days in-office) next quarter, and some employees are concerned. Create an announcement that explains the reasoning, acknowledges concerns, and maintains employee trust while being clear about expectations.

↓
4 + Style & Tone

We're moving to hybrid (3 days in-office). Create an announcement that explains reasoning and maintains trust. Tone should be empathetic but clear. Acknowledge this is a significant change. Be honest about the "why" without being defensive.

↓
5 + Audience

We're moving to hybrid. Create an announcement that explains reasoning and maintains trust. Empathetic but clear tone. Audience is all 300 employees, including parents, caregivers, people with long commutes, and those who relocated during full-remote. Managers will receive a separate FAQ.

↓
6 + Response

Create hybrid policy announcement for all employees including parents, caregivers, and those with long commutes. Empathetic but clear tone. Format: CEO letter style, 400-500 words. Include: Why we're making this change, what the policy is, timeline, flexibility options, how to raise concerns, and next steps. End with appreciation.

1 Start

Create a pitch deck outline.

↓
2 + Context

We sell an AI-powered customer service platform. We have a demo meeting with a large retail chain that's frustrated with their current ticketing system's slow response times. Create a pitch deck outline.

↓
3 + Objective

We sell AI customer service to a large retail chain frustrated with slow response times. Create a pitch deck that positions us as the solution to their specific pain point, differentiates us from competitors, and gets them to agree to a pilot program.

↓
4 + Style & Tone

AI customer service pitch to retail chain with slow response time issues. Create deck that positions us as solution and gets pilot agreement. Consultative, not salesy. Lead with their problem, not our product. Use concrete numbers and case studies from similar retailers.

↓
5 + Audience

AI customer service pitch to retail chain. Consultative tone, lead with their problem, use case studies. Decision makers are VP of Customer Experience and IT Director. VP cares about customer satisfaction scores, IT cares about integration complexity and security.

↓
6 + Response

AI customer service pitch for VP Customer Experience (cares about CSAT) and IT Director (cares about integration/security). Consultative tone, use case studies. Format: 10-12 slides. Structure: Problem → Cost of Problem → Solution Overview → How It Works → Case Study → Integration/Security → Pilot Proposal → Q&A. Include speaker notes.

1 Start

Write an incident report.

↓
2 + Context

Our payment processing system went down for 45 minutes during peak hours due to a database connection pool exhaustion. Customers couldn't complete purchases. The issue is now resolved. Write an incident report.

↓
3 + Objective

Payment system was down 45 minutes during peak hours due to database issues, now resolved. Create an incident report that documents what happened, explains root cause, outlines remediation steps, and prevents similar incidents.

↓
4 + Style & Tone

Payment system was down 45 minutes. Create incident report documenting what happened, root cause, and prevention. Factual, objective tone. Be specific about timeline and technical details. Take accountability without being defensive. Focus on learning, not blame.

↓
5 + Audience

Payment system was down 45 minutes. Create incident report with root cause and prevention. Factual, objective, focus on learning. Primary audience is leadership and compliance. Secondary audience is the engineering team. Leadership needs business impact, engineers need technical details.

↓
6 + Response

Payment outage incident report for leadership/compliance and engineering. Factual tone, focus on learning. Format: Executive Summary (3 sentences), Timeline (minute-by-minute), Root Cause Analysis, Business Impact (revenue, customers affected), Immediate Fix, Long-term Prevention (with owners and deadlines), Lessons Learned.

1 Start

Write an executive summary.

↓
2 + Context

We completed a cost-benefit analysis for switching from on-premise servers to cloud infrastructure. The analysis shows 30% cost savings over 3 years but requires $500K upfront migration investment. Write an executive summary.

↓
3 + Objective

Cost-benefit analysis shows 30% savings over 3 years for cloud migration but needs $500K upfront. Create an executive summary that clearly presents the recommendation, supports the investment ask, and addresses likely concerns about the upfront cost.

↓
4 + Style & Tone

Cloud migration analysis: 30% savings, $500K upfront. Create executive summary with clear recommendation addressing upfront cost concerns. Confident but not overselling. Data-driven and specific. Present risks honestly alongside benefits. Lead with the bottom line.

↓
5 + Audience

Cloud migration executive summary. Data-driven, present risks honestly, lead with bottom line. Audience is the CFO and CEO who will approve the budget. They're financially sophisticated but not technical. They'll ask about payback period and risk.

↓
6 + Response

Cloud migration executive summary for CFO and CEO who will approve budget. Data-driven, present risks, lead with bottom line. Format: 1 page max. Start with recommendation and key number. Include: Recommendation, Investment Required, 3-Year ROI, Payback Period, Key Risks with Mitigations, Immediate Next Steps. Use a comparison table.

Audience-First Thinking

COSTAR puts your audience at the center. Notice how knowing the readers (executives, engineers, customers) shapes how you write—even when that information isn't explicitly labeled.
