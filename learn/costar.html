The COSTAR Framework

Know your audience. Shape your message. Get results. COSTAR puts audience awareness at the center of every prompt.

The Key Insight

Why Audience Changes Everything

The same information communicated to executives, engineers, or customers requires completely different approaches—not just different words, but different structure, depth, and emphasis.

Calibrated Complexity Match vocabulary and depth to your reader's expertise
Addressed Concerns Anticipate what your audience worries about most
Resonant Appeals Frame benefits in terms that matter to them
Framework Components

The Six Elements

Each element shapes how your content reaches its intended audience

C

Context

Background information

Provide the situation, background, and relevant details that frame your request.

Example

"Our company just acquired a smaller competitor. We need to communicate this before the press release."

O

Objective

What you want to achieve

Define your primary goal and any secondary outcomes you're hoping for.

Example

"Write an announcement that explains the acquisition and addresses job security concerns."

S

Style

Writing style or voice

Specify the writing approach, format conventions, or brand voice to follow.

Example

"Write in the CEO's voice—direct, transparent, and optimistic."

T

Tone

Emotional quality

Define the emotional register—the feeling you want readers to have.

Example

"Confident but not dismissive. Empathetic about uncertainty while emphasizing stability."

A

Audience

Who will receive this
Key Element

Describe who will read this—their knowledge level, concerns, and what they care about.

Example

"All employees—mix of tenures, roles, and likely anxiety levels about job security."

R

Response

Format of the output

Specify the structure, length, and format you need the output to take.

Example

"Email format: Subject line, opening hook, 3 key points with headers, FAQ section."

Best Fit

When to Use COSTAR

COSTAR excels when audience awareness is critical to success

Stakeholder Communications

Messages that need to resonate with specific groups or personas.

Executive Briefings

High-stakes communication requiring precise tone and framing.

Marketing Content

Copy that speaks directly to your target market's needs and desires.

Internal Announcements

Company-wide communications that address diverse concerns.

Customer Support

Responses tailored to customer knowledge and emotional state.

Training Materials

Content calibrated to learner expertise and learning goals.

Alternatives

Consider Other Frameworks When...

Audience isn't critical

CRISP Framework

Simpler, faster approach for everyday tasks where audience is less critical.
You need exact format control

CRISPE Framework

Uses examples to show the AI exactly what format you want.
You need step-by-step reasoning

ReAct Framework

For complex problems requiring reasoning, action, and tool use.
