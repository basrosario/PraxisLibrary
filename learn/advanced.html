Chain-of-Thought Prompting

Encourage explicit step-by-step reasoning

üß† Why This Works

When you ask AI to "think step by step," you're activating a different processing mode. Instead of jumping straight to an answer, the model generates intermediate reasoning tokens that it then uses as context for subsequent steps. This dramatically reduces errors on multi-step problems because each step constrains the next.

40% accuracy boost On math and logic problems with chain-of-thought prompting
5 words to trigger "Let's think step by step" activates reasoning mode
Easy to verify Visible reasoning lets you catch errors mid-process

Without CoT

"What's 15% of 847?"

Answer: 127.05 (no work shown, can't verify)

vs

With CoT

"What's 15% of 847? Think through this step by step."

10% of 847 = 84.7. 5% = half of that = 42.35. So 15% = 84.7 + 42.35 = 127.05

Trigger Phrases

"Let's think step by step" ‚Ä¢ "Walk through this step by step" ‚Ä¢ "Show your reasoning" ‚Ä¢ "Before giving your final answer, work through the logic" ‚Ä¢ "Explain your thought process as you solve this"

Few-Shot Learning

Teach through examples, not explanations

üìñ Why This Works

Examples are more powerful than descriptions because they implicitly communicate countless details you might forget to specify. Format, length, tone, level of detail, edge case handling‚Äîall of these get transmitted through good examples. The AI pattern-matches your examples and extends that pattern to new inputs.

2-5 examples ideal Enough to establish pattern, not so many it's wasteful
50% better format match Compared to describing format in words
Implicit communication Transmits style, tone, detail level automatically
Task

Convert technical feature descriptions into user-friendly bullet points.

Example 1

Input: "256-bit AES encryption for data at rest and in transit"
Output: "Bank-level security protects your data everywhere"

Example 2

Input: "OAuth 2.0 integration with SAML support"
Output: "Single sign-on with your existing company login"

Your Input

Now the AI understands: simplify jargon, focus on benefit, keep it short. Just provide your technical feature and get a user-friendly translation.

Best Practices

Show diversity: Cover different cases the AI might encounter. Match real data: Your examples should resemble actual inputs. Include edge cases: Show how to handle tricky situations.

üé≠ Why This Works

Different roles activate different patterns of thinking in the AI. A "devil's advocate" role surfaces objections and weaknesses. A "teacher" role produces clearer explanations. A "senior engineer" role goes deeper on technical details. You're not giving the AI new knowledge‚Äîyou're directing how it applies what it already knows.

Constrained Output Formats

Define exact structure for programmatic use

üìã Why This Works

When you need to parse AI output with code, inconsistent formatting breaks everything. By defining exact output structure and explicitly requesting "no additional text," you get reliable, machine-readable responses. This is essential for automation, APIs, and any workflow where AI output feeds into other systems.

JSON Format

{
  "summary": "...",
  "key_points": ["..."],
  "sentiment": "positive|negative|neutral",
  "confidence": 0.0-1.0
}

or

Structured Text

TITLE: ...
CATEGORY: ...
PRIORITY: High/Medium/Low
ACTION ITEMS:
- Item 1
- Item 2

Enforcement Tip

End your prompt with: "Return your analysis in exactly this format, with no additional text or explanation." This prevents the AI from adding conversational fluff that breaks parsing.

Self-Consistency Checking

Have AI verify and improve its own work

‚úì Why This Works

AI can catch errors when prompted to review its own output. This works because generation and evaluation are different processes‚Äîthe AI might miss something while generating but catch it when specifically looking for problems. For critical tasks, adding a self-check step significantly improves reliability.

Prompt Chaining

Break complex tasks into connected steps

‚õìÔ∏è Why This Works

Complex tasks work better as a series of focused steps rather than one giant prompt. Each step gets the AI's full attention, you can verify intermediate results before proceeding, and when something goes wrong, you know exactly which step failed. Think of it as assembly-line production for AI tasks.

1

Research

"List all factors to consider for [topic]"
Output: A comprehensive list of factors and considerations.

2

Analyze

"Given these factors: [output 1], analyze the implications"
Output: Analysis connecting the factors to outcomes.

3

Synthesize

"Based on this analysis: [output 2], recommend a course of action"
Output: Justified recommendations tied to analysis.

4

Refine

Optionally: "Review these recommendations and add implementation steps"
Output: Actionable final deliverable.

Benefits

Focused attention: Each step gets full context. Verifiable: Check outputs before proceeding. Debuggable: Know exactly where issues occur. Reusable: Good chains work for similar tasks.

Combining Techniques

The real power comes from mixing methods

Chain-of-Thought + Role

"You are a senior financial analyst. Walk through your reasoning step by step to evaluate this investment opportunity."

Few-Shot + Constrained Output

"Here are two examples of properly formatted responses: [examples]. Return your analysis in exactly this JSON format."

Chaining + Self-Check

"Step 1: Research. Step 2: Analyze. Step 3: Recommend. Step 4: Review your recommendation for any errors."

All Together

Expert role + explicit reasoning + format examples + structured output + verification = maximum reliability for complex tasks.
