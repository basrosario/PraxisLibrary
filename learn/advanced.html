Technique 1

Chain-of-Thought Prompting

When you ask AI to "think step by step," you're activating a different processing mode. Instead of jumping straight to an answer, the model generates intermediate reasoning tokens that it then uses as context for subsequent steps.

This dramatically reduces errors on multi-step problems because each step constrains the next. The visible reasoning also lets you catch errors mid-process.

Higher Accuracy
5 Words to Trigger
Example
Prompt

"What's 15% of 847? Think through this step by step."

Response

10% of 847 = 84.7
5% = half of that = 42.35
15% = 84.7 + 42.35 = 127.05

Trigger Phrases

"Let's think step by step" / "Walk through this step by step" / "Show your reasoning" / "Before giving your final answer, work through the logic" / "Explain your thought process as you solve this"

Technique 2

Few-Shot Learning

Examples are more powerful than descriptions because they implicitly communicate countless details you might forget to specify. Format, length, tone, level of detail, edge case handling—all transmitted through good examples.

The AI pattern-matches your examples and extends that pattern to new inputs. A few clear examples is usually ideal—enough to establish pattern without wasting tokens.

Optimal Example Count
Better Format Match
Few-Shot Pattern
Example 1

In: "256-bit AES encryption"
Out: "Bank-level security protects your data"

Example 2

In: "OAuth 2.0 with SAML"
Out: "Single sign-on with your company login"

Your Input

Now AI understands the pattern: simplify jargon, focus on benefit, keep it short.

Best Practices

Show diversity: Cover different cases the AI might encounter. Match real data: Your examples should resemble actual inputs. Include edge cases: Show how to handle tricky situations.

Role Prompting

Assign personas to activate different thinking modes

Technique 3

Why Roles Work

Different roles activate different patterns of thinking in the AI. A "devil's advocate" role surfaces objections. A "teacher" role produces clearer explanations. A "senior engineer" role goes deeper on technical details.

You're not giving the AI new knowledge—you're directing how it applies what it already knows.

Limitations

Roles influence style and approach, but don't give the AI knowledge it doesn't have. A "legal expert" role doesn't make the AI a lawyer. Always verify important information with authoritative sources.

The Critic

"Act as a devil's advocate and find weaknesses in this plan."

Best for stress-testing ideas

The Expert

"You are a senior data scientist with 15 years of experience."

Best for technical depth

The Teacher

"Explain this as if teaching a bright high school student."

Best for accessible explanations

The Editor

"Review this text as a strict editor for The New York Times."

Best for quality improvement

Constrained Output Formats

Define exact structure for programmatic use

Technique 4

When Structure Matters

When you need to parse AI output with code, inconsistent formatting breaks everything. By defining exact output structure and explicitly requesting "no additional text," you get reliable, machine-readable responses.

This is essential for automation, APIs, and any workflow where AI output feeds into other systems.

Enforcement Tip

End your prompt with: "Return your analysis in exactly this format, with no additional text or explanation." This prevents conversational fluff that breaks parsing.

JSON Format

{ "summary": "...", "key_points": ["..."], "sentiment": "positive", "confidence": 0.92 }
OR

Structured Text

TITLE: ... CATEGORY: ... PRIORITY: High/Med/Low ACTION ITEMS: - Item 1 - Item 2

Self-Consistency Checking

Have AI verify and improve its own work

Technique 5

Built-In Quality Control

AI can catch errors when prompted to review its own output. This works because generation and evaluation are different processes—the AI might miss something while generating but catch it when specifically looking for problems.

For critical tasks, adding a self-check step significantly improves reliability.

Review and Revise

"After generating your response, review it for accuracy. Correct any errors."

Multiple Approaches

"Solve this using two different methods, then compare answers to verify."

Confidence Rating

"Rate your confidence in each claim (High/Medium/Low) and explain uncertainties."

Prompt Chaining

Break complex tasks into connected steps

Technique 6

Assembly-Line AI

Complex tasks work better as a series of focused steps rather than one giant prompt. Each step gets the AI's full attention, you can verify intermediate results, and when something goes wrong, you know exactly which step failed.

Think of it as assembly-line production for AI tasks.

Benefits

Focused attention: Each step gets full context. Verifiable: Check outputs before proceeding. Debuggable: Know exactly where issues occur. Reusable: Good chains work for similar tasks.

1

Research

"List all factors to consider for [topic]"

2

Analyze

"Given these factors, analyze the implications"

3

Synthesize

"Based on analysis, recommend a course of action"

4

Refine

"Review recommendations and add implementation steps"

Combining Techniques

The real power comes from mixing methods

Chain-of-Thought + Role

"You are a senior financial analyst. Walk through your reasoning step by step to evaluate this investment opportunity."

Few-Shot + Constrained Output

"Here are two examples of properly formatted responses: [examples]. Return your analysis in exactly this JSON format."

Maximum Reliability

Expert role + explicit reasoning + format examples + structured output + verification = the most reliable results for complex tasks.
