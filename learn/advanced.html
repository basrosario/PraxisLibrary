Chain-of-Thought Prompting

Encourage the AI to show its reasoning step by step

Chain-of-Thought (CoT) prompting encourages the AI to break down complex reasoning into explicit steps. This dramatically improves performance on tasks requiring logic, math, or multi-step analysis.

Simple Trigger

Add "Let's think step by step" or "Walk through this step by step" to your prompt.

Structured Trigger

"Before giving your final answer, show your reasoning process. Consider each factor, then conclude."

Without CoT

"What's 15% of 847?" → Answer: 127.05 (no work shown)

With CoT

"What's 15% of 847? Think through this step by step." → 10% of 847 = 84.7. 5% = 42.35. So 15% = 127.05

When to Use CoT

Use Chain-of-Thought for math problems, logical reasoning, complex analysis, planning, and any task where showing work helps verify correctness.

Few-Shot Learning

Show the AI what you want through examples

Few-shot learning means providing examples in your prompt to show the AI exactly what you want. Instead of describing the format, you demonstrate it.

Exact Format

Examples implicitly communicate the precise structure you expect.

Level of Detail

Show how much depth and specificity you want in responses.

Style & Tone

Demonstrate the voice and approach through real examples.

Edge Cases

Show how to handle tricky situations by including them.

Few-Shot Example Template

Task: Convert feature descriptions into user-friendly bullet points.

Example 1:
Input: "256-bit AES encryption for data at rest and in transit"
Output: "Bank-level security protects your data everywhere"

Example 2:
Input: "OAuth 2.0 integration with SAML support"
Output: "Single sign-on with your existing company login"

Now convert: [Your input here]

Use 2-5 Examples

More examples help with complex patterns, but diminishing returns set in quickly.

Show Diversity

Include examples that cover different cases the AI might encounter.

Match Real Data

Your examples should be representative of actual inputs the AI will see.

Role Prompting

Assign personas to activate different modes of thinking

Assigning the AI a specific role or persona changes how it approaches problems. Different roles activate different "modes" of thinking and can dramatically change the output.

The Critic

"Act as a devil's advocate and find weaknesses in this plan"

Use for: Stress-testing ideas

The Expert

"You are a senior data scientist with 15 years of experience"

Use for: Technical depth

The Teacher

"Explain this as if teaching a bright high school student"

Use for: Accessible explanations

The Editor

"Review this text as a strict editor for The New York Times"

Use for: Quality improvement
Role Prompting Limitations

Roles influence style and approach, but don't give the AI knowledge it doesn't have. A "legal expert" role doesn't make the AI a lawyer. Always verify important information.

Constrained Output Formats

Define exact output structure for programmatic use

When you need structured data, explicitly define the output format. This is essential for programmatic use of AI outputs and ensures consistency.

JSON Output

{ "summary": "...", "key_points": ["..."], "sentiment": "positive|negative|neutral", "confidence": 0.0-1.0 }

Structured Text

TITLE: ... CATEGORY: ... PRIORITY: High/Medium/Low ACTION ITEMS: - Item 1 - Item 2
Format Enforcement Prompt

"Analyze this feedback. Return your analysis in exactly this JSON format, with no additional text: {\"sentiment\": \"positive/negative/neutral\", \"main_issue\": \"...\", \"suggested_action\": \"...\", \"urgency\": 1-5}"

Self-Consistency Checking

Ask the AI to verify its own work

Self-consistency checking catches errors and improves reliability, especially for complex tasks. The AI can review, revise, and validate its own output.

Review and Revise

"After generating your response, review it for accuracy and completeness. If you find any errors, correct them."

Multiple Approaches

"Solve this problem using two different methods, then compare answers to verify."

Confidence Assessment

"Rate your confidence in each claim (High/Medium/Low) and explain any uncertainties."

Prompt Chaining

Break complex tasks into a series of connected prompts

Complex tasks work better when broken into a series of prompts, where each step's output feeds into the next. This allows for verification at each stage.

1

Research

"List all factors to consider for [topic]"

2

Analyze

"Given these factors: [output 1], analyze the implications"

3

Synthesize

"Based on this analysis: [output 2], recommend a course of action"

Focused Attention

Each step gets the AI's full focus without context switching.

Verify Intermediate Steps

You can check each output before proceeding to the next step.

Easier Debugging

When something goes wrong, you know exactly which step failed.

Reusable Chains

Well-designed chains can be reused for similar tasks.

Keep Practicing

Advanced techniques require practice. Start with one technique, master it, then add more to your toolkit.
