What Makes CRISPE Different

CRISP plus one powerful addition

CRISPE extends CRISP by adding Example (few-shot learning). Showing the AI what you want through samples dramatically improves output quality and consistency.

E - Example

Show the AI what you want through sample input/output pairs. This is called few-shot prompting and dramatically improves output quality.

"Example: For a recipe, format like this: 'Monday: Veggie Tacos - Prep: 15 min - Hidden veggies: bell peppers, zucchini - Kid appeal: crunchy shells, fun to build'"
When to Use CRISPE

Use CRISPE when showing examples of your desired output format will help the AI understand exactly what you need. Examples are especially powerful for consistent formatting, specific writing styles, or complex structures.

C - Context

Give the AI the background it needs

Context provides the situation, background, and relevant details that help the AI understand your world. Without context, responses are generic. With context, they're tailored to your specific needs.

Your Situation

"I'm renovating my kitchen for the first time"

The Problem

"I have a small galley kitchen that feels cramped and dated"

Constraints

"I have a $15,000 budget and need to keep the same layout"

Strong Context Example

"I'm a first-time homeowner trying to improve my garden. I have a shady backyard with clay soil in the Pacific Northwest. I work full-time and can only spend a few hours on weekends gardening."

R - Role

Tell the AI who it is

The Role component transforms the AI from a generic assistant into a specific expert. Use "Act as..." or "You are..." to assign a persona that brings specialized knowledge and perspective to your task.

Professional Role

"Act as an interior designer" or "You are an experienced gardener"

Experience Level

"...with 20 years of experience" or "...who has helped hundreds of families"

Specialization

"...specializing in small spaces" or "...focused on low-maintenance gardens"

Approach

"...known for budget-friendly solutions" or "...who prioritizes practicality"

Strong Role Example

"Act as a friendly nutritionist who specializes in family meal planning. You're known for creating healthy meals that even picky eaters will enjoy, using common grocery store ingredients."

Role Best Practices
  • Be specific: "Pediatric sleep consultant" beats "parenting expert"
  • Add relevant experience that matches your problem
  • Include perspective or approach when it matters

I - Instruction

The main ask—what you want done

The Instruction is your core request. Use clear action verbs and be specific about the deliverable. This is what you want the AI to actually produce.

Creating

Write, Create, Generate, Design, Draft, Develop

Analyzing

Analyze, Review, Evaluate, Audit, Assess, Critique

Transforming

Rewrite, Simplify, Expand, Translate, Summarize, Convert

Strategizing

Plan, Recommend, Propose, Outline, Prioritize, Organize

Clear Instruction Example

"Create a weekly meal plan for a family of four, including breakfast, lunch, and dinner, with a grocery shopping list organized by store section."

S - Specifics

Define format, length, tone, and scope

Specifics tell the AI exactly how you want the output shaped. This includes format, length, tone, and scope. The more specific you are, the closer the output matches your needs.

Format

Bullet points, numbered list, table, paragraph, JSON

Length

50 words, 3 paragraphs, one page, 5 bullet points

Tone

Formal, casual, urgent, friendly, professional

Scope

High-level overview, detailed analysis, executive summary

Specifics Example

"Keep recipes under 30 minutes of prep time. Use a friendly, encouraging tone. Include nutritional info per serving."

P - Parameters

Rules and boundaries for the output

Parameters set the constraints, requirements, and boundaries for the response. These are the measurable, specific rules the AI must follow.

Length

"Maximum 500 words", "Keep each point to 2-3 sentences", "One page summary"

Format

"Numbered list", "Table format", "Use headers for each section"

Requirements

"Include specific metrics", "Reference industry benchmarks", "Add implementation steps"

Restrictions

"No solutions requiring new hires", "Budget under $5,000", "Must be mobile-compatible"

Parameters Example

"Keep the plan to 5 action items maximum. Each item should include: the tactic, expected impact (high/medium/low), implementation effort, and a 1-sentence rationale. No recommendations that require hiring or major tech changes."

E - Example

Show what you want through sample input/output

Examples demonstrate exactly what you're looking for through sample input/output pairs. This technique, called few-shot prompting, is one of the most powerful ways to get consistent, high-quality results.

Format Examples

Show the exact structure you want the output to follow

Example output format:
[Tactic]: Simplify form fields
[Impact]: High
[Effort]: Low
[Why]: Reduces friction at highest drop-off point

Tone Examples

Demonstrate the voice and style you're looking for

Good: "Consider A/B testing your CTA placement"
Not: "You should definitely move your CTA!"

Input/Output Pairs

Show how to transform specific inputs into desired outputs

Input: "8-step checkout process"
Output: "Consolidate to 3 steps: Cart Review → Shipping + Payment → Confirmation"
Few-Shot Prompting Power

Even one good example dramatically improves AI output. Two or three examples create even more consistency. Use examples when you need specific formats, consistent tone, or when words alone don't capture what you want.

1 Start

Create a content calendar.

↓
2 + Context

We're a B2B software company launching a new analytics feature. Our audience is data-savvy marketing managers at mid-size companies. Create a content calendar.

↓
3 + Role

We're a B2B software company launching a new analytics feature for data-savvy marketing managers. Approach this as a content strategist who has driven successful product launches at SaaS companies. Create a content calendar.

↓
4 + Instruction

We're a B2B software company launching a new analytics feature. Approach as a content strategist with SaaS launch experience. Create a 4-week content calendar covering blog posts, LinkedIn content, email sequences, and one webinar.

↓
5 + Specifics

We're a B2B software company launching an analytics feature. Approach as a content strategist. Create a 4-week content calendar with blog posts, LinkedIn content, email sequences, and one webinar. Use a professional but approachable tone. Focus on pain points and outcomes, not features.

↓
6 + Parameters

We're a B2B software company launching an analytics feature. Approach as a content strategist. Create a 4-week calendar with blog posts, LinkedIn, emails, and a webinar. Professional but approachable tone, focus on pain points. 2 blog posts per week max. LinkedIn posts should be under 200 words. Email sequence is 5 emails. Avoid jargon.

↓
7 + Example

We're a B2B software company launching an analytics feature. Approach as a content strategist. Create a 4-week calendar with blog posts, LinkedIn, emails, and a webinar. Professional tone, focus on pain points. 2 blogs/week max, LinkedIn under 200 words, 5-email sequence, avoid jargon. Format each entry like: "Week 1, Tuesday | Blog | Title: [hook] | Goal: awareness | CTA: read case study"

The Power of Examples

Notice how adding the example at the end shows the AI exactly what format you want. This is few-shot learning in action—one example dramatically improves consistency.
