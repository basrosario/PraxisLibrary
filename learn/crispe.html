What Makes CRISPE Different

Two powerful additions for richer AI interactions

CRISPE extends the basic prompting elements by adding Role (persona assignment) and Example (few-shot learning). These additions create more consistent, expert-level responses.

R - Role

Tell the AI who it is. Assign a specific persona, expertise, or identity that shapes how it approaches your task.

"Act as a senior UX researcher with 10 years of experience in mobile app usability"

E - Example

Show the AI what you want through sample input/output pairs. This is called few-shot prompting and dramatically improves output quality.

"Example: Input: 'Our checkout has 8 steps' Output: 'Reduce to 3 steps: Cart → Details → Confirm'"
When to Use CRISPE

Use CRISPE when you need the AI to adopt a specific expert persona, or when showing examples of your desired output format will help the AI understand exactly what you need.

C - Context

Give the AI the background it needs

Context provides the situation, background, and relevant details that help the AI understand your world. Without context, responses are generic. With context, they're tailored to your specific needs.

Your Situation

"I'm a product manager at a fintech startup launching our first mobile app"

The Problem

"We're seeing 60% drop-off during onboarding"

Constraints

"We have a small dev team and need solutions implementable in 2 sprints"

Strong Context Example

"I run a small e-commerce business selling handmade ceramics. Our website gets about 5,000 visitors monthly, but only 2% convert to buyers. Most visitors leave after viewing just one product page."

R - Role

Tell the AI who it is

The Role component transforms the AI from a generic assistant into a specific expert. Use "Act as..." or "You are..." to assign a persona that brings specialized knowledge and perspective to your task.

Professional Role

"Act as a senior marketing strategist" or "You are an experienced copywriter"

Experience Level

"...with 15 years in B2B SaaS" or "...who has worked with Fortune 500 clients"

Specialization

"...specializing in conversion optimization" or "...focused on mobile-first design"

Approach

"...known for data-driven recommendations" or "...who prioritizes user research"

Strong Role Example

"Act as a senior conversion rate optimization specialist who has helped 50+ e-commerce businesses increase their conversion rates. You're known for identifying quick wins that don't require major development resources."

Role Best Practices
  • Be specific: "UX designer for mobile apps" beats "designer"
  • Add relevant experience that matches your problem
  • Include perspective or approach when it matters

I - Instruction

The main ask—what you want done

The Instruction is your core request. Use clear action verbs and be specific about the deliverable. This is what you want the AI to actually produce.

Creating

Write, Create, Generate, Design, Draft, Develop

Analyzing

Analyze, Review, Evaluate, Audit, Assess, Critique

Transforming

Rewrite, Simplify, Expand, Translate, Summarize, Convert

Strategizing

Plan, Recommend, Propose, Outline, Prioritize, Organize

Clear Instruction Example

"Create a 5-point action plan to reduce our onboarding drop-off rate, with specific tactics for each step of the current flow."

S - Specifics

Define format, length, tone, and scope

Specifics tell the AI exactly how you want the output shaped. This includes format, length, tone, and scope. The more specific you are, the closer the output matches your needs.

Format

Bullet points, numbered list, table, paragraph, JSON

Length

50 words, 3 paragraphs, one page, 5 bullet points

Tone

Formal, casual, urgent, friendly, professional

Scope

High-level overview, detailed analysis, executive summary

Specifics Example

"Write 5 bullet points, under 30 words each. Use a direct, actionable tone. Avoid jargon—this will be shared with non-technical stakeholders."

P - Parameters

Rules and boundaries for the output

Parameters set the constraints, requirements, and boundaries for the response. These are the measurable, specific rules the AI must follow.

Length

"Maximum 500 words", "Keep each point to 2-3 sentences", "One page summary"

Format

"Numbered list", "Table format", "Use headers for each section"

Requirements

"Include specific metrics", "Reference industry benchmarks", "Add implementation steps"

Restrictions

"No solutions requiring new hires", "Budget under $5,000", "Must be mobile-compatible"

Parameters Example

"Keep the plan to 5 action items maximum. Each item should include: the tactic, expected impact (high/medium/low), implementation effort, and a 1-sentence rationale. No recommendations that require hiring or major tech changes."

E - Example

Show what you want through sample input/output

Examples demonstrate exactly what you're looking for through sample input/output pairs. This technique, called few-shot prompting, is one of the most powerful ways to get consistent, high-quality results.

Format Examples

Show the exact structure you want the output to follow

Example output format:
[Tactic]: Simplify form fields
[Impact]: High
[Effort]: Low
[Why]: Reduces friction at highest drop-off point

Tone Examples

Demonstrate the voice and style you're looking for

Good: "Consider A/B testing your CTA placement"
Not: "You should definitely move your CTA!"

Input/Output Pairs

Show how to transform specific inputs into desired outputs

Input: "8-step checkout process"
Output: "Consolidate to 3 steps: Cart Review → Shipping + Payment → Confirmation"
Few-Shot Prompting Power

Even one good example dramatically improves AI output. Two or three examples create even more consistency. Use examples when you need specific formats, consistent tone, or when words alone don't capture what you want.

Complete CRISPE Example

See all six components working together

C - Context

I run a small e-commerce store selling handmade ceramics. We get 5,000 monthly visitors but only 2% convert. Most leave after viewing one product. We're a 2-person team with limited development resources.

R - Role

Act as a conversion rate optimization specialist who has helped 50+ small e-commerce businesses increase sales. You specialize in quick wins that don't require major development work.

I - Instruction

Create a prioritized list of 5 changes we can make to increase our conversion rate from 2% to 4%.

S - Specifics

Use bullet points. Keep each recommendation under 50 words. Direct and actionable tone. Focus on practical tactics, not theory.

P - Parameters

Exactly 5 recommendations. Each must be implementable within 1 week by a non-technical person. No recommendations requiring custom development or paid tools over $50/month.

E - Example

Format each recommendation like this:

1. [Tactic Name]
What to do: [Specific action]
Why it works: [One sentence]
How to implement: [2-3 steps]
Expected impact: [High/Medium/Low]
