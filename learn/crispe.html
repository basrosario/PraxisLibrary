The Example Element

Rather than describing what you want, show it. Examples unlock few-shot learning—one of the most powerful techniques in prompt engineering.

The Science

Few-Shot Learning

When you include examples in your prompt, the AI extracts patterns: format, tone, structure, level of detail, even subtle stylistic choices. This technique is called few-shot learning.

Pattern Recognition AI identifies format, structure, and style from your examples
Consistency Boost Examples dramatically improve output format consistency
Show Don't Tell One good example often beats paragraphs of description
More Consistent Format consistency improves dramatically with examples
Few Examples Needed Just a few examples unlock few-shot power

Framework Elements

The Six Components

Five from CRISP plus the game-changing Example

C

Context

Background information about your situation, constraints, and goals.

"I'm a content strategist at a B2B software company..."
R

Role

The persona or expertise you want the AI to adopt.

"Act as a senior content marketing expert..."
I

Instructions

The main task you want accomplished.

"Create a 3-month content calendar..."
S

Specifics

Format, length, tone, and scope requirements.

"Include publishing dates, target keywords..."
P

Parameters

Constraints, boundaries, and rules to follow.

"Maximum 12 pieces per month..."
E

Example

Sample input/output showing the exact format you want.

"Format like: Week 1: Blog post on [topic]..."

Deep Dive

Types of Examples

Different ways to show the AI what you want

Format Examples

Show the exact structure you want the output to follow.

Example: [Tactic]: Simplify form
[Impact]: High | [Effort]: Low
[Rationale]: Reduces friction
Best for: Reports, SOPs, structured data

Tone Examples

Demonstrate the voice and style you're looking for.

Example: Good: "Consider A/B testing..."
Not: "You should definitely..."
Best for: Brand voice, copywriting

Input/Output Pairs

Show how to transform specific inputs into desired outputs.

Example: Input: "8-step checkout"
Output: "Consolidate to 3 steps with progress indicator"
Best for: Transformations, rewrites

Template Examples

Provide a complete template for the AI to follow.

Example: Week 1: [Topic] Blog post
- 3 LinkedIn posts
- 1 email to nurture list
Best for: Calendars, plans, schedules
In Practice

Complete CRISPE Prompt

See all six components working together

Full CRISPE Prompt
C I'm a content strategist at a B2B software company launching a new product next quarter.
R Act as a senior content marketing expert with 10+ years in tech industry campaigns.
I Create a 3-month content calendar covering blog posts, social media, email sequences, and webinars.
S Include publishing dates, content themes, target keywords, and distribution channels for each piece.
P Focus on LinkedIn and email as primary channels. Keep total content to 12 pieces maximum per month.
E Format like: "Week 1: Blog post on [topic], 3 LinkedIn posts, 1 email to nurture list"
Context Role Instructions Specifics Parameters Example

The Example Makes It Concrete

Notice how the Example (E) at the end shows exactly what format you want. Without it, the AI might produce a calendar in any format. With it, you get consistent, usable output.

Decision Guide

CRISP vs CRISPE

When to add the Example element

Use CRISP When...

  • Format is flexible or standard
  • Task is straightforward
  • General output is sufficient
  • Speed matters more than precision
  • You're exploring possibilities
Learn CRISP

Use CRISPE When...

  • Exact format is critical
  • Creating reusable templates
  • Matching existing brand voice
  • Complex data structures needed
  • "Show don't tell" applies
