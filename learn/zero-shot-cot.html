Zero-Shot Chain-of-Thought

Five words that changed AI reasoning forever: "Let's think step by step."

AI for Everybody Built With UD/UDL Security A+ 100% Performance 100% AI Assisted Building Claude Code Community GitHub
The Breakthrough

A Simple Phrase Unlocks Reasoning

In 2022, researchers discovered something remarkable: adding "Let's think step by step" to prompts increased accuracy on math problems from 17.7% to 78.7%—with no examples needed.

The Key Insight

LLMs already know how to reason—they just need permission to show their work. The trigger phrase activates reasoning pathways that otherwise stay dormant.

Dramatic Improvement

Adding "Let's think step by step" can transform results—especially on math and reasoning tasks where showing work leads to correct answers.

See the Difference

Before vs. After

Watch how the same question produces dramatically different results

Without CoT Often Wrong
Prompt: A store has 23 apples. They sell 8 in the morning and receive a delivery of 15 more. Then they sell 12 in the afternoon. How many apples are left?
Response: 18 apples.
Skipped steps, got wrong answer
With Zero-Shot CoT Correct
Prompt: A store has 23 apples. They sell 8 in the morning and receive a delivery of 15 more. Then they sell 12 in the afternoon. How many apples are left? Let's think step by step.
Response:
1. Start: 23 apples
2. Sell 8: 23 - 8 = 15 apples
3. Receive 15: 15 + 15 = 30 apples
4. Sell 12: 30 - 12 = 18 apples
Shows work, verifiable, correct
Without CoT Guesses
Prompt: If all Bloops are Razzies, and all Razzies are Lazzies, are all Bloops definitely Lazzies?
Response: Yes.
Right answer, but no justification
With Zero-Shot CoT Reasoned
Prompt: If all Bloops are Razzies, and all Razzies are Lazzies, are all Bloops definitely Lazzies? Let's think step by step.
Response:
1. Given: All Bloops are Razzies
2. Given: All Razzies are Lazzies
3. If every Bloop is a Razzie, and every Razzie is a Lazzie...
4. Then every Bloop must also be a Lazzie (transitive property)
Yes, all Bloops are definitely Lazzies.
Clear logical chain, trustworthy
Without CoT Shallow
Prompt: Should a startup prioritize revenue growth or profitability in its first year?
Response: It depends on the business model and market conditions. Generally, revenue growth is prioritized.
Generic, lacks actionable insight
With Zero-Shot CoT Thorough
Prompt: Should a startup prioritize revenue growth or profitability in its first year? Let's think step by step.
Response:
1. Funding situation: VC-backed → growth; bootstrapped → profitability
2. Market type: Winner-take-all → growth; niche → profitability
3. Unit economics: Must be viable regardless of focus
4. Runway: Less than 12 months → profitability path needed
Decision framework: Choose growth only if funded with 18+ months runway and proven unit economics.
Structured, considers multiple factors

Decision Guide

When Zero-Shot CoT Excels

Math & Calculations

Multi-step arithmetic, word problems, percentages, conversions.

Logic Problems

Syllogisms, deduction, if-then reasoning, constraint satisfaction.

Complex Decisions

Weighing trade-offs, multi-factor analysis, strategic planning.

Code Debugging

Tracing execution, finding bugs, explaining code flow.

When CoT Might Not Help

Simple factual lookups, creative writing, or tasks where the model should be fast and intuitive rather than deliberative. Adding "think step by step" to "What is the capital of France?" just adds unnecessary tokens.

Advanced Zero-Shot CoT Patterns

Pattern

Think through this using the following structure:
1. What do I know?
2. What do I need to find?
3. What approach will I use?
4. Execute the approach
5. Verify the answer

Use For

When you want consistent, methodical responses across multiple similar problems.

Pattern

Let's think step by step. After reaching your answer, check your work by [specific verification method].

Example

"...After calculating the total, verify by working backwards from your answer to the original values."

Pattern

Think through this as a [domain expert] would, showing your reasoning at each step.

Examples

• "...as a financial analyst would..."
• "...as a software architect would..."
• "...as a doctor diagnosing symptoms would..."
