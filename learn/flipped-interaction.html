Foundation Framework

Flipped Interaction

Let the AI interview you first. Get personalized advice instead of generic responses by reversing who asks the questions.

AI for Everybody Built With UD/UDL Security A+ 100% Performance 100% AI Assisted Building Claude Code Community GitHub
The Core Insight

Real Experts Ask First

When you visit a doctor, they don't immediately prescribe medicine. A consultant doesn't start with solutions. A coach doesn't give advice on day one.

They ask questions. Because context changes everything.

Flipped Interaction activates this same expert pattern in AI. Instead of getting generic advice from a rushed response, you get personalized recommendations from a system that actually understands your situation.

Traditional

Why This Works

The psychology and mechanics behind the flip

Context Discovery

AI questions surface information you didn't know was relevant. The interview process reveals hidden constraints, preferences, and requirements that would never appear in a standard prompt.

Forced Depth

The AI can't give a shallow response because it hasn't gathered enough context yet. This structurally prevents the "here are 10 generic tips" problem that plagues standard interactions.

Expert Pattern

By mimicking the consultation process of real experts, you activate more sophisticated reasoning patterns in the AI. It shifts from "answer mode" to "diagnostic mode."

See the Difference

Same goal, dramatically different results

The Four-Step Flow

From goal to personalized guidance

1

State Your Goal

Tell the AI what you're trying to accomplish. Be clear about the end result you want, but don't try to provide all context upfront. That's the AI's job to discover.

Example

"I want to improve my content marketing strategy..."

2

Request the Interview

Ask the AI to interview you before giving advice. Specify the number of questions (5-7 works well) and optionally the areas you want covered.

Example

"...before giving recommendations, ask me 5-6 focused questions about my business, audience, and current efforts."

3

Answer Thoughtfully

Respond to each question with specifics about your situation. The more context you provide, the better the eventual advice. This is where the magic happens.

Key Insight

The AI's questions often reveal aspects of your situation you hadn't considered sharing.

4

Receive Tailored Advice

Now the AI has the context it needs. Its recommendations will directly address your constraints, goals, and specific situation instead of offering generic tips.

Result

Actionable, personalized guidance that acknowledges your real constraints.

How to Trigger the Flip

Phrases that activate interview mode

Direct Request

"Before you answer, ask me questions to understand my situation."

Clear and simple

Numbered Questions

"Ask me 5 clarifying questions before providing recommendations."

Controlled scope

Information Gathering

"What do you need to know about my situation to give me good advice?"

Open-ended

Expert Frame

"Interview me like a consultant would before proposing solutions."

Role-based
Pro Tip: Be Specific

Specify the number of questions and optionally the areas to cover. "Ask me 5 questions about my team structure, current tools, and main blockers" is more effective than just "ask me some questions."

When to Use Flipped Interaction

Best for personalized advice and complex situations

Perfect For

Personalized Advice

When generic tips won't cut it and you need recommendations for your specific situation.

Complex Problems

Multi-factor challenges where the right answer depends on many variables you might not think to share.

Unique Constraints

When your budget, timeline, team, or resources make standard solutions impractical.

Unknown Unknowns

Skip It When

Simple Factual Questions

"What's the capital of France" doesn't need an interview first.

Full Context Provided

When you've already specified everything the AI needs to know.

Speed Over Depth

When you need a quick answer and personalization isn't worth the extra time.

Variations of the Flip

Adapt the technique to different professional contexts

The Diagnostic

"Act as a consultant. Diagnose my problem before prescribing solutions."

Best for troubleshooting and problem-solving

The Intake Form

"Ask me the questions you'd put on an intake form for a new client."

Best for professional services scenarios

The Discovery Call

"Conduct a discovery call to understand my needs before proposing solutions."

Best for sales and solutions design

Requirements Gathering

"You're a business analyst. Gather requirements before drafting the specification."

Best for technical and product work

Flipped Interaction in Action

A complete example from prompt to personalized advice

Your Opening Prompt

"I need help with our content marketing strategy. Before giving recommendations, interview me about our business, audience, current efforts, and goals. Ask 5-6 focused questions."

AI's Questions

  1. What does your company do, and what's your main product or service?
  2. Who is your target audience, and what's their biggest pain point?
  3. What content are you currently producing, and on which channels?
  4. What's working well, and what's underperforming?
  5. What's your primary goal: brand awareness, lead generation, or retention?
  6. What resources (time, budget, team) do you have for content?

Your Answers

You share specifics: B2B SaaS for project management, targeting CTOs at mid-size tech companies, currently only doing LinkedIn posts, getting engagement but no leads, goal is lead generation, small team with limited time for content creation.

Tailored Strategy

Instead of generic "post consistently" advice, you get: specific content formats CTOs actually read (case studies, data-driven posts), a realistic bi-weekly cadence for a small team, a LinkedIn-to-gated-whitepaper strategy for capturing leads, and concrete first steps you can start this week.

The Difference

Without the interview, you'd get advice like "create valuable content and post regularly." After the interview, you get a strategy designed for your specific business model, audience, resource constraints, and goals. Same question, completely different value.

Where Flipped Interaction Fits
