Flipped Interaction

Let the AI interview you first. Get personalized advice instead of generic responses by reversing who asks the questions.

The Flip

Reverse who asks the questions

Traditional

You ask, AI answers immediately

‚Üí

Flipped

AI interviews you, then answers with context

üß† Why This Works

Real experts‚Äîdoctors, consultants, coaches‚Äînever give advice without first understanding your situation. They ask questions because context changes everything. When you flip the interaction, you activate this same expert pattern in the AI, forcing it to gather the specific information needed before jumping to solutions. The result: personalized advice instead of generic tips.

See the Difference

Same goal, dramatically different results

Traditional Approach

"Help me improve my team's productivity."

Result: Generic advice about time management, meetings, and tools that may not apply to your situation at all.

vs

Flipped Interaction

"I want to improve my team's productivity. Before giving advice, ask me 5-7 questions about my team, our challenges, and what we've already tried."

Result: Targeted recommendations based on your team size, remote/in-office setup, specific bottlenecks, and past attempts.

How It Works

The flipped interaction flow

1

State Your Goal

Tell the AI what you're trying to accomplish. Be clear about the end result you want, but don't try to provide all context upfront.

2

Request the Interview

Ask the AI to interview you before giving advice. Specify the number of questions (5-7 works well) and optionally the areas to cover.

3

Answer Thoughtfully

Respond to each question with specifics about your situation. The more context you provide, the better the eventual advice.

4

Receive Tailored Advice

Now the AI has the context it needs. Its recommendations will directly address your constraints, goals, and situation.

When to Use Flipped Interaction

Best for personalized advice and complex situations

üéØ

Personalized Advice

When generic tips won't cut it and you need recommendations for your specific situation.

üß©

Complex Problems

Multi-factor challenges where the right answer depends on many variables you might not think to share.

üöß

Unique Constraints

When your budget, timeline, team, or resources make standard solutions impractical.

Unknown Unknowns

When you're not sure what information is relevant‚Äîlet the AI figure out what to ask.

Skip It When

For simple factual questions, tasks with all context provided, or when speed matters more than personalization. A question like "what's the capital of France" doesn't need an interview first.

Flipped Interaction in Action

A complete example from prompt to personalized advice

Your Prompt

"I need help with our content marketing strategy. Before giving recommendations, interview me about our business, audience, current efforts, and goals. Ask 5-6 focused questions."

AI's Questions

1. What does your company do, and what's your main product or service?
2. Who is your target audience, and what's their biggest pain point?
3. What content are you currently producing, and on which channels?
4. What's working well, and what's underperforming?
5. What's your primary goal: brand awareness, lead generation, or retention?
6. What resources do you have for content?

Your Answers

You share specifics: B2B SaaS, targeting CTOs, currently only doing LinkedIn posts, getting engagement but no leads, goal is lead generation, small team with limited time.

Tailored Advice

Instead of generic "post consistently" advice, you get: specific content formats for CTOs, a realistic cadence for a small team, LinkedIn-to-gated-content strategy for lead capture, and measurable next steps.

‚úì The Difference

Without the interview, you'd get advice like "create valuable content and post regularly." After the interview, you get a strategy designed for your specific business model, audience, resource constraints, and goals. Same question, completely different value.
