From frameworks to flow: understanding how modern AI handles context and when structure helps versus hinders your prompts.

From Frameworks to Flow

How AI communication has evolved

2022 Rigid Frameworks Strict formats needed
2023-24 Flexible Prompting Natural language works
2025-26 Conversational AI Structure optional

Early AI systems required careful prompt engineering with specific formats. Today's models understand natural conversation remarkably well. So when does structure still help?

The answer: structure becomes valuable when your prompt is complex, when you need consistent output, or when you're working with large amounts of context.

Understanding Context Windows

How much can an AI "remember" in a single conversation?

ðŸ“Š What is a Context Window?

A context window is the total amount of text an AI can process at onceâ€”your prompt plus its response. Think of it as the AI's "working memory." Modern models have large context windows (100K+ tokens), but more context isn't always better. Relevant, well-organized context outperforms massive dumps of information.

Practical Insight

Don't worry about hitting context limits for most tasks. Focus on including relevant information rather than all information. A well-organized 500-word prompt often outperforms a 5,000-word info dump.

Keep It Simple

Three Structuring Approaches

From natural to highly organized

Natural Paragraphs

I'm planning a surprise birthday party for my mom who's turning 60. She loves gardening and Italian food. We have about 20 guests coming and a budget of $500. Can you help me plan the menu and decorations?

Labeled Sections

Event: Surprise 60th birthday party

Guest of honor: My mom (loves gardening, Italian food)

Guests: 20 people

Budget: $500

Need help with: Menu and decorations

XML Tags

<event>Surprise 60th birthday</event>

<honoree>

<interests>gardening, Italian food</interests>

</honoree>

<constraints>

<guests>20</guests>

<budget>$500</budget>

</constraints>

<request>Plan menu and decorations</request>

All Three Work

For this simple request, all three approaches produce excellent results. The natural paragraph is fastest. Labels help ensure completeness. XML becomes valuable for complex, technical, or programmatic use cases.

Preview: XML Tags

A glimpse at advanced structuring techniques

XML (eXtensible Markup Language) tags provide the most explicit structure. They're not required for most prompts, but become powerful when you need:

Clear Boundaries

Unambiguous separation between instructions, context, and examples.

Nested Information

Hierarchical data that labels alone can't cleanly represent.

Programmatic Use

Building prompts dynamically in code or templates.

Complex Instructions

Multi-step processes with conditional logic or references.

When to Use What

A quick decision guide

Situation Approach Why
Quick question or simple task Natural language Fast and effective
Multi-part request Labels or framework Ensures completeness
Consistent output format needed Labels + example Shows expected structure
Technical/complex instructions XML tags Maximum clarity
Template for repeated use Labels or XML Easy to fill in
Creative brainstorming Natural language Don't constrain creativity
