Why Use CRISP?

A lean framework designed for efficiency and clarity

CRISP is designed for efficiency and clarity. It's the ideal starting point for anyone learning structured prompting, and remains useful even as you master more advanced techniques.

Quick & Focused

Get actionable outputs for everyday tasks without extensive setup.

Easy to Remember

A simple 5-letter framework you can memorize and apply immediately.

Consistent Results

Structure leads to predictability. Same approach, reliable outcomes.

Great Foundation

Master CRISP first, then graduate to CRISPE or COSTAR for complex needs.

CRISP vs. Other Methods

CRISP is leaner than COSTAR or CRISPE. Use it when you don't need to specify audience separately or add AI personality. For more complex needs, explore COSTAR or CRISPE.

Better Use Cases for CRISP

Great for quick emails and general tasks. Better suited for flexible content than exact structure replication. Use CRISP when specific structured output is not required. For tasks needing precise format replication, CRISPE is a better fit.

C - Context

Set the stage with background information

Context tells the AI about your situation, background, and relevant details that will shape the response. The more relevant context you provide, the more tailored the output.

Who You Are

"I'm a parent planning my child's 10th birthday party"

The Situation

"The party is in two weeks with 15 kids attending"

Relevant Background

"My child loves dinosaurs and we have a backyard"

Effective Context Example

"I'm helping my elderly mother downsize from her home of 40 years. She's moving to a smaller apartment and we need to decide what to keep, donate, or pass on to family."

R - Role

Define the persona for the AI to adopt

The role tells the AI who to be. By assigning a persona, you activate domain-specific knowledge and get responses shaped by that expertise.

Expert Roles

Nutritionist, Personal trainer, Financial advisor

Professional Roles

Teacher, Chef, Travel agent, Interior designer

Creative Roles

Storyteller, Party planner, Gift idea expert

Advisory Roles

Life coach, Parenting expert, Career counselor

Effective Role Example

"Act as an experienced travel agent who specializes in family-friendly vacations"

I - Instructions

Provide specific guidelines for how to complete the task

Instructions are the specific guidelines for how to complete the task. Think of these as the "rules" for the output. What should be included? Excluded? How should it be organized?

What to Include

"Include estimated costs, time needed, and a shopping list"

What to Avoid

"Avoid recipes with nuts due to allergies"

How to Structure

"Organize by morning, afternoon, and evening activities"

Priority/Focus

"Focus on options that work for all ages"

S - Specifics

Define format, length, tone, and scope of the output

Specifics tell the AI exactly how you want the output shaped. This includes format, length, tone, and scope. The more specific you are, the closer the output matches your needs.

Format

Bullet points, numbered list, table, paragraph, JSON

Length

50 words, 3 paragraphs, one page, 5 bullet points

Tone

Formal, casual, urgent, friendly, professional

Scope

High-level overview, detailed analysis, executive summary

Specifics Example

"Write 3 bullet points, under 50 words each, in a professional but approachable tone. Focus on key benefits only."

P - Parameters

Set constraints and specifications that bound the output

Parameters are the constraints and specifications that give the output its final shape. These are the measurable, concrete boundaries for the response.

Length

"300 words", "2 paragraphs", "one page maximum"

Format

"Bullet points", "numbered list", "table format"

Sections

"5 sections with headers", "intro/body/conclusion"

Special Requirements

"Include citations", "add examples for each point"

Complete CRISP Example

See all five components working together

I'm a project manager at a tech startup preparing for our quarterly planning meeting. Act as a strategic planning consultant with experience in agile methodologies. Create a quarterly planning template that covers goals, milestones, resource allocation, and risk assessment. Format as a structured document with clear headings, bullet points, and a timeline table. Keep it to 2 pages maximum. Focus on actionable items. Avoid jargon.

No labels needed! Each element flows naturally into the next. The AI understands from context what you need.
