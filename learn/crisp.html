The CRISP Method

A structured approach for quick, focused AI prompting. Perfect for everyday tasks that need clear, actionable results.

C

Context

Background information about your situation

R

Request

What you want the AI to do

I

Instructions

Specific guidelines for the task

S

Style

Tone, format, and voice

P

Parameters

Constraints like length, format

Why Use CRISP?

A lean framework designed for efficiency and clarity

CRISP is designed for efficiency and clarity. It's the ideal starting point for anyone learning structured prompting, and remains useful even as you master more advanced techniques.

Quick & Focused

Get actionable outputs for everyday tasks without extensive setup.

Easy to Remember

A simple 5-letter framework you can memorize and apply immediately.

Consistent Results

Structure leads to predictability. Same approach, reliable outcomes.

Great Foundation

Master CRISP first, then graduate to CRISPE or COSTAR for complex needs.

CRISP vs. Other Methods

CRISP is leaner than COSTAR or CRISPE. Use it when you don't need to specify audience separately or add AI personality. For more complex needs, explore COSTAR or CRISPE.

C - Context

Set the stage with background information

Context tells the AI about your situation, background, and relevant details that will shape the response. The more relevant context you provide, the more tailored the output.

Who You Are

"I'm a marketing manager at a B2B software company"

The Situation

"We're launching a new feature next month"

Relevant Background

"Our audience is mid-size businesses in healthcare"

Strong Context Example

"I'm a freelance web developer creating a portfolio website. I specialize in React and Node.js, and my target clients are small businesses and startups."

R - Request

State exactly what you want with a clear action verb

The request is your core ask. Use a clear action verb and be specific about the deliverable. Start with what you want to end up with.

Creating

Write, Create, Generate, Design, Draft, Compose

Analyzing

Analyze, Compare, Evaluate, Review, Assess, Examine

Transforming

Summarize, Simplify, Expand, Translate, Rewrite, Convert

Explaining

Explain, Describe, Define, Clarify, Break down, Outline

Clear Request Example

"Write a project proposal for building a customer feedback portal"

I - Instructions

Provide specific guidelines for how to complete the task

Instructions are the specific guidelines for how to complete the task. Think of these as the "rules" for the output. What should be included? Excluded? How should it be organized?

What to Include

"Include a timeline, budget estimate, and risk assessment"

What to Avoid

"Don't use technical jargon or acronyms"

How to Structure

"Organize into three sections: Problem, Solution, Benefits"

Priority/Focus

"Focus primarily on cost savings and ROI"

S - Style

Define the voice, tone, and feel of the output

Style shapes how the content sounds and feels. Consider your audience and purpose when defining style. The same information can be presented in vastly different ways.

Formal

Professional, polished, suitable for executives and official communications

Conversational

Friendly, approachable, like talking to a trusted colleague

Technical

Precise, detailed, uses domain-specific terminology

Persuasive

Compelling, benefit-focused, calls the reader to action

Style Specification Example

"Write in a confident but not aggressive tone. Be direct and use active voice. The style should feel like a trusted advisor, not a salesperson."

P - Parameters

Set constraints and specifications that bound the output

Parameters are the constraints and specifications that give the output its final shape. These are the measurable, concrete boundaries for the response.

Length

"300 words", "2 paragraphs", "one page maximum"

Format

"Bullet points", "numbered list", "table format"

Sections

"5 sections with headers", "intro/body/conclusion"

Special Requirements

"Include citations", "add examples for each point"

Complete CRISP Example

See all five components working together

C - Context

I'm a product manager at a SaaS company. We're preparing for our quarterly business review with leadership.

R - Request

Create an executive summary of our Q3 product achievements.

I - Instructions

Include key metrics (user growth, feature adoption, NPS). Highlight the top 3 launches and their business impact. Mention any challenges and how we addressed them.

S - Style

Professional and confident. Focus on outcomes, not activities. Use data to support claims.

P - Parameters

One page maximum. Use headers for each section. Include a brief "Looking Ahead" section at the end.
