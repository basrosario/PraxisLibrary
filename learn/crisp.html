Why This Framework

Structure Reduces Mental Load

CRISP emerged as one of the first widely-adopted structured frameworks for prompt engineering, providing a memorable checklist that helps include essential elements.

Cognitive Research Backed Structured approaches free mental resources for what matters
Easy to Remember Acronym-based design makes learning and recall effortless

Deep Dive

The Five Elements

Click each element to explore what it means and how to use it effectively

C

Context

Set the stage

Background information that helps AI understand your situation, constraints, and goals. Think of it as the "who, what, where, when, why" of your request.

Who You Are "I'm a first-time manager at a startup..."
Your Situation "We're launching a product in 3 weeks..."
Background "We've already tried X and Y..."
R

Role

Define the persona

Assign a specific expertise or perspective for the AI to adopt. Roles activate domain-specific knowledge patterns and shape vocabulary, priorities, and approach.

Expert Role "Act as a nutritionist who specializes in plant-based diets..."
Creative Role "Act as an interior designer with a minimalist aesthetic..."
Professional Role "Act as a career counselor for mid-career professionals..."
I

Instructions

Provide direction

The "what" and "how" of your request. Clear instructions eliminate guesswork by specifying exactly what to do, include, avoid, and how to organize the response.

What to Include "Include estimated costs and a timeline..."
What to Avoid "Avoid recipes with nuts due to allergies..."
How to Structure "Organize by morning, afternoon, evening..."
S

Specifics

Shape the output

Quality controls that define format, length, and tone. Without specifics, AI defaults to its own judgment. These ensure the response matches how you need it delivered.

Format "Bullet points, numbered list, table, JSON..."
Length "50 words, 3 paragraphs, one page..."
Tone "Formal, casual, urgent, friendly..."
P

Parameters

Set boundaries

Guardrails that keep responses on track. Constraints often improve creativity by forcing more thoughtful, precise outputs. They transform open-ended prompts into targeted requests.

Length Limits "300 words max, 2 paragraphs..."
Exclusions "No technical jargon, avoid cliches..."
Requirements "Include citations, add examples..."
See The Difference

CRISP in Action

Compare a vague prompt with a structured CRISP prompt

Before CRISP

"Help me write an email about our new policy."

Result: Generic email that doesn't match your needs. Multiple revision rounds required.
With CRISP

I'm the HR director at a 200-person tech company. We just approved a new hybrid work policy. Act as an internal communications specialist. Write an announcement email to all employees explaining the new policy—when it starts, the schedule options, and how to request their preferences. Keep it warm but professional, under 300 words, with bullet points for the key dates.

Result: Polished, on-brand email ready to send with minimal edits.
C R I S P
Flexibility

Labels vs Natural Language

Both approaches work equally well—use what feels natural to you

With Labels

Context: I'm launching a new product next month and need help with the announcement.

Role: Act as a marketing communications specialist.

Instruction: Write an internal announcement email to get the sales team excited.

Specifics: Enthusiastic but professional tone, highlight key selling points.

Parameters: 200 words max, include 3 bullet points of key features.

Great for learning Easy to double-check
OR
Natural Language

I'm launching a new product next month and need to get our sales team excited about it. Can you help me write an internal announcement email? I'd like it to be enthusiastic but still professional—we want them pumped but not over the top. Focus on the key selling points they'll need to know. Keep it around 200 words and include 3 bullet points covering the main features.

Feels conversational Faster to write

Both Get the Same Results

The AI understands both formats equally well. Labels help when learning or double-checking that you've covered all elements. Natural conversation flows faster once you're comfortable with the framework.

Best Fit

When to Use CRISP

CRISP excels for everyday tasks that need clarity without complexity

Email Drafting

Professional correspondence, announcements, follow-ups, and replies.

Content Writing

Blog posts, social media, marketing copy, and descriptions.

Planning Tasks

Meeting agendas, project outlines, checklists, and schedules.

Communication

Presentations, talking points, scripts, and messaging.

Research Summaries

Topic overviews, comparisons, and brief analyses.

Quick Tasks

Everyday prompts where you need speed with quality.

Alternatives

Consider Other Frameworks When...

You need exact output format

CRISPE Framework

Adds Persona and Examples for when you need to show the AI exactly what you're looking for.
You're writing for a specific audience

COSTAR Framework

Adds explicit Audience and Style considerations for targeted communication.
You need multi-step reasoning

ReAct Framework

For complex problems requiring reasoning, action, and tool use in iterative cycles.
