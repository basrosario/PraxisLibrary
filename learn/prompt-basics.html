What is a Prompt?

Understanding your primary tool for AI communication

A prompt is the input you give to an AI system to get a response. It's your way of communicating what you want the AI to do.

Think of it like giving instructions to a highly capable assistant who:

Vast Knowledge

Has broad knowledge but no context about your specific situation.

Literal Interpretation

Takes instructions literally and precisely as given.

Variable Output

Can produce different outputs based on how you phrase your request.

No Mind Reading

Doesn't know what you're thinking unless you tell it.

Basic Prompt

"Write about marketing"

Enhanced Prompt

"Write a 200-word introduction for a blog post about email marketing best practices for small e-commerce businesses. Use a friendly, conversational tone."

Key Takeaway

The quality of your output is directly tied to the quality of your input. Vague prompts produce vague results; specific prompts produce specific results.

The Anatomy of a Good Prompt

Essential elements every effective prompt contains

1

Task/Action

What do you want the AI to do? (Write, analyze, explain, create, compare, summarize...)

2

Context

Background information that helps the AI understand your situation.

3

Specifics

Details about format, length, style, audience, or constraints.

4

Examples

Sample inputs/outputs that show what you're looking for.

Write a newsletter.

Write a newsletter announcing our company's new remote work policy to all employees. Keep it under 200 words, use a friendly but professional tone, and include the key dates. Structure it with a catchy subject line, brief intro, 3 bullet points for key changes, and a closing with next steps.

Common Prompting Mistakes

Learn what to avoid for better results

Being Too Vague

Instead of

"Help me with my presentation"

Try

"Create an outline for a 10-minute presentation about Q3 sales results for our executive team"

Assuming Context

Instead of

"Make it better"

Try

"Improve this email by making it more concise and adding a clear call-to-action"

Overloading

Instead of

"Write a blog post, create social media posts, draft an email, and make a video script"

Try

Break into separate prompts, one task at a time

No Format Guidance

Instead of

"Explain machine learning"

Try

"Explain machine learning in 3 paragraphs for a high school student, using everyday analogies"

The Iteration Mindset

Great prompts are rarely written on the first try

The best prompt engineers use an iterative approach. Each interaction teaches you something about how to get better results.

1

Start Simple

Begin with a basic prompt to see what the AI produces.

2

Analyze the Output

What's good? What's missing? What's wrong?

3

Refine Your Prompt

Add constraints, context, or examples to address gaps.

4

Repeat

Continue until you get the results you need.

Key Takeaway

Don't expect perfection on the first try. Treat prompting as a conversation, not a one-shot request.

Putting It All Together

Your action plan for better prompts

1

Always include a clear action verb

Tell the AI exactly what to do

2

Provide relevant context

Who, what, why, for whom?

3

Specify the format

Length, structure, style, tone

4

Give examples when helpful

Show, don't just tell

5

Iterate and refine

Build on what works
