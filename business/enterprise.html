Strategic Planning & Analysis

Enterprise-level strategic thinking and decision support.

Executive Strategy BriefCOSTAR
[Context] You are a senior strategy consultant preparing a brief for C-suite executives at a [industry] enterprise.
[Objective] Synthesize complex strategic information into an executive-ready brief that supports decision-making.
[Style] McKinsey-style executive communication—structured, concise, insight-driven.
[Tone] Professional, confident, objective.
[Audience] C-suite executives with limited time but need to make informed decisions.
[Response] Create an executive brief on [topic/decision] including: (1) Executive Summary - decision required, recommended action, key rationale; (2) Situation Analysis - current state, market/competitive context, internal capabilities; (3) Options Evaluated - Option A/B/C with pros, cons, resources; (4) Recommendation & Rationale - preferred option, success factors, risk mitigation; (5) Implementation Roadmap - phased timeline with key actions; (6) Investment & Returns - resources, expected outcomes, success metrics; (7) Decision Required - what approval is needed and timeline.
[Strategic Context] Topic: [Strategic decision/issue]. Background: [Context]. Key stakeholders: [Approvers/involved]. Constraints: [Budget, timeline, political]. Data available: [Information we have].
[Constraints] Keep to 2 pages maximum. Use data to support assertions. Acknowledge uncertainties. Provide clear recommendation. Consider board/investor perspective.
Market Intelligence SynthesizerCRISPE
[Context] You are a market intelligence analyst synthesizing competitive and market data for strategic planning.
[Role] Senior competitive intelligence analyst for enterprise strategy team.
[Instruction] I need to synthesize multiple sources of market intelligence into actionable insights for [strategic initiative/decision]. Based on the information I provide, create a market intelligence synthesis covering: (1) Market Overview - size, growth, segments, trends, regulatory factors; (2) Competitive Landscape - key players, positioning, strategic moves, emerging threats; (3) Customer Dynamics - buyer behaviors, unmet needs, decision factors; (4) Strategic Implications - opportunities, threats, capabilities needed; (5) Intelligence Priorities - what to monitor, gaps to fill, early warning indicators.
[Style] Analytical, objective, focused on "so what" not just "what."
[Parameters] Identify 3 non-obvious insights that emerge from connecting different data points.
[Example] Non-obvious insight: "Competitor X's hiring pattern in ML engineering combined with their recent patent filings suggests they're pivoting to predictive analytics—a direct threat to our core offering."
[Intelligence Inputs] [Paste or describe data points, research findings, competitor news].
[Constraints] Distinguish between facts and inferences. Note confidence levels. Identify contradictory signals. Focus on actionable insights.

Governance & Compliance

Maintain control while enabling innovation.

AI Governance Framework DeveloperCOSTAR
[Context] You are helping an enterprise develop a comprehensive AI governance framework that enables responsible AI adoption while managing risk.
[Objective] Create a governance framework that balances innovation enablement with appropriate oversight and risk management.
[Style] Formal policy framework suitable for board presentation and regulatory review.
[Tone] Professional, thorough, risk-aware but not innovation-blocking.
[Audience] Board, executive leadership, legal/compliance, business units.
[Response] Develop a governance framework including: (1) Governance Structure - steering committee, RACI, escalation, decision rights; (2) Risk Classification - tiers, criteria, approval requirements, review frequency; (3) Use Case Approval - intake, evaluation, workflow, documentation; (4) Operational Controls - data handling, validation, oversight, monitoring; (5) Vendor/Tool Management - approved lists, assessment, contracts, monitoring; (6) Training and Enablement - role-based requirements, certification, awareness, support; (7) Metrics and Reporting - KRIs, adoption metrics, incidents, board reporting.
[Enterprise Context] Industry: [Industry and regulations]. AI maturity: [Current state]. Risk appetite: [Conservative/moderate/aggressive]. Existing governance: [Current frameworks].
[Constraints] Align with existing ERM. Consider industry regulations. Balance control with usability. Plan for evolution. Include clear accountability.
Regulatory Impact AssessmentCRISPE
[Context] You are a regulatory affairs specialist assessing the compliance implications of AI deployment.
[Role] Senior regulatory analyst for enterprise compliance.
[Instruction] We're planning to deploy [AI use case] and need to understand regulatory implications. Conduct an assessment covering: (1) Applicable Regulations - what applies, which jurisdictions, industry-specific requirements; (2) Compliance Requirements - what we must do, documentation needed, disclosures; (3) Risk Assessment - compliance risks, consequences, how to demonstrate compliance; (4) Gap Analysis - current capabilities, gaps, what to implement; (5) Recommendations - controls, documentation, monitoring, timeline.
[Style] Thorough, conservative on risk, practical on implementation.
[Parameters] Identify emerging regulations we should prepare for proactively.
[Example] "EU AI Act classifies this as high-risk, requiring conformity assessment, human oversight documentation, and incident reporting mechanisms."
[Use Case Details] AI application: [Planned use]. Data involved: [What data]. Users: [Internal/external]. Jurisdictions: [Operating locations]. Industry: [Sector context].
[Constraints] This is preliminary analysis, not legal advice. Flag areas requiring legal review. Consider current and emerging regulations. Include practical implementation guidance.

Change Management at Scale

Rolling out AI across thousands of employees.

Enterprise Change Plan DeveloperCOSTAR
[Context] You are an organizational change management expert helping plan the enterprise-wide rollout of [AI initiative].
[Objective] Develop a comprehensive change management plan that ensures successful adoption across the organization.
[Style] Prosci/ADKAR-informed change management approach.
[Tone] Practical, people-focused, realistic about challenges.
[Audience] Change management team, project leadership, executive sponsors.
[Response] Create a change plan including: (1) Impact Assessment - who affected, what changes, magnitude, expected resistance; (2) Stakeholder Analysis - groups, concerns, influence mapping, engagement strategy; (3) Communication Plan - messages by audience, channels, timing, feedback; (4) Training & Enablement - needs by role, delivery, schedule, support; (5) Resistance Management - anticipated points, root causes, mitigation, escalation; (6) Sponsorship Roadmap - exec activities, middle management, champions, visible actions; (7) Sustainment - reinforcement, celebration, improvement, adoption tracking.
[Change Initiative] What's changing: [AI initiative]. Scope: [People, functions, locations]. Timeline: [Rollout schedule]. Culture: [How org handles change]. Past experience: [What worked/failed].
[Constraints] Account for geographic/cultural differences. Consider union/works council if applicable. Align with existing change portfolio. Resource realistically. Build in feedback mechanisms.

Process Optimization

Identify and capture AI efficiency opportunities.

Process Automation Opportunity IdentifierCRISPE
[Context] You are a process improvement consultant specializing in AI-driven automation opportunities.
[Role] Business process analyst for enterprise transformation.
[Instruction] I need to identify which processes in [function/department] would benefit most from AI augmentation. Analyze the processes I describe and provide: (1) Process Assessment - current state, pain points, volume/frequency, automation level; (2) AI Opportunity Identification - AI-suitable tasks, AI type, expected impact, complexity; (3) Prioritization Matrix - impact vs effort, risk, dependencies, sequence; (4) Business Case Outline - current cost, expected benefits, investment, payback; (5) Implementation Considerations - integration, change management, risks, metrics.
[Style] Analytical, ROI-focused, realistic about implementation challenges.
[Parameters] Identify one "quick win" that could demonstrate value within 30 days.
[Example] Quick win: "Automate weekly report compilation from 3 source systems—currently takes 4 hours/week, AI could reduce to 15 minutes with human review."
[Process Description] Function: [Department]. Processes: [Current state]. Pain points: [Known issues]. Volume: [How often, how many]. Current tools: [Systems used].
[Constraints] Focus on high-impact, lower-risk first. Consider system integration. Account for human oversight. Build in change management.

Enterprise AI Principles

Responsible Enterprise AI Use
  • Never input confidential data into public AI tools without approved guardrails
  • Follow your governance framework—shortcuts create enterprise risk
  • Verify AI outputs before using in external communications or decisions
  • Document AI use in audit-sensitive processes
  • Escalate uncertainty—when in doubt, check with legal/compliance
  • Share learnings—help build organizational AI capability
