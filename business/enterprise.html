Enterprise AI Prompts

Size: 1000+ Employees Focus: Scale & Governance Challenge: Complexity Management

Enterprise organizations face unique AI challenges: regulatory compliance, governance frameworks, cross-functional coordination, and managing change at scale. These prompts address the specific needs of large organizations while maintaining the rigor your stakeholders expect.

Enterprise-Grade AI Adoption

The challenge isn't just using AI—it's deploying it safely, consistently, and effectively across thousands of employees while maintaining compliance and governance.

Strategic Planning & Analysis

Enterprise-level strategic thinking and decision support.

Executive Strategy Brief COSTAR
Context: You are a senior strategy consultant preparing a brief for C-suite executives at a [industry] enterprise.

Objective: Synthesize complex strategic information into an executive-ready brief that supports decision-making.

Style: McKinsey-style executive communication—structured, concise, insight-driven.

Tone: Professional, confident, objective.

Audience: C-suite executives with limited time but need to make informed decisions.

Response: Create an executive brief on [topic/decision] including: 1. **Executive Summary** (1 paragraph) - The decision required - Recommended action - Key supporting rationale 2. **Situation Analysis** (bullet points) - Current state - Market/competitive context - Internal capabilities/constraints 3. **Options Evaluated** - Option A: [Description, pros, cons, resource requirements] - Option B: [Description, pros, cons, resource requirements] - Option C: [Description, pros, cons, resource requirements] 4. **Recommendation & Rationale** - Preferred option and why - Key success factors - Risk mitigation approach 5. **Implementation Roadmap** - Phase 1: [Timeline, key actions] - Phase 2: [Timeline, key actions] - Phase 3: [Timeline, key actions] 6. **Investment & Returns** - Resource requirements - Expected outcomes - Success metrics 7. **Decision Required** - Clear statement of what approval is needed - Timeline for decision [Strategic Context] Topic: [The strategic decision or issue] Background: [Relevant context] Key stakeholders: [Who needs to approve/be involved] Constraints: [Budget, timeline, political considerations] Data available: [What information do we have?] [Constraints] - Keep to 2 pages maximum - Use data to support assertions - Acknowledge uncertainties - Provide clear recommendation (don't fence-sit) - Consider board/investor perspective
Market Intelligence Synthesizer CRISPE
Capacity: You are a market intelligence analyst synthesizing competitive and market data for strategic planning.

Role: Senior competitive intelligence analyst for enterprise strategy team.

Insight: I need to synthesize multiple sources of market intelligence into actionable insights for [strategic initiative/decision]. I'll provide various data points and need them woven into a coherent analysis.

Statement: Based on the information I provide, create a market intelligence synthesis that covers: 1. **Market Overview** - Size, growth trajectory, key segments - Major trends shaping the market - Regulatory/macroeconomic factors 2. **Competitive Landscape** - Key players and their positioning - Recent strategic moves by competitors - Emerging threats (new entrants, substitutes) 3. **Customer Dynamics** - Shifting buyer behaviors/preferences - Unmet needs and opportunities - Decision-making factors 4. **Implications for Our Strategy** - Opportunities to capture - Threats to defend against - Capabilities we need to develop 5. **Recommended Intelligence Priorities** - What we need to monitor - Information gaps to fill - Early warning indicators

Personality: Analytical, objective, focused on "so what" not just "what."

Experiment: Identify 3 non-obvious insights that emerge from connecting different data points. [Intelligence Inputs] [Paste or describe the various data points, research findings, competitor news, etc.] [Constraints] - Distinguish between facts and inferences - Note confidence levels for key conclusions - Identify contradictory signals if present - Focus on actionable insights, not just information

Governance & Compliance

Maintain control while enabling innovation.

AI Governance Framework Developer COSTAR
Context: You are helping an enterprise develop a comprehensive AI governance framework that enables responsible AI adoption while managing risk.

Objective: Create a governance framework that balances innovation enablement with appropriate oversight and risk management.

Style: Formal policy framework suitable for board presentation and regulatory review.

Tone: Professional, thorough, risk-aware but not innovation-blocking.

Audience: Board, executive leadership, legal/compliance, business units.

Response: Develop a governance framework including: 1. **Governance Structure** - AI Steering Committee composition and charter - Roles and responsibilities (RACI matrix) - Escalation pathways - Decision rights framework 2. **Risk Classification Framework** - Risk tiers (Low/Medium/High/Critical) - Classification criteria - Approval requirements by tier - Review frequency by tier 3. **Use Case Approval Process** - Intake and assessment process - Evaluation criteria - Approval workflow - Documentation requirements 4. **Operational Controls** - Data handling requirements - Model validation standards - Human oversight requirements - Monitoring and auditing 5. **Vendor/Tool Management** - Approved tools list management - Vendor assessment criteria - Contract requirements - Ongoing monitoring 6. **Training and Enablement** - Role-based training requirements - Certification programs - Awareness campaigns - Support resources 7. **Metrics and Reporting** - Key risk indicators - Adoption metrics - Incident tracking - Board reporting cadence [Enterprise Context] Industry: [Industry and key regulatory requirements] Current AI maturity: [Where are we now?] Risk appetite: [Conservative, moderate, aggressive] Existing governance: [What frameworks already exist?] [Constraints] - Must align with existing enterprise risk management - Consider industry-specific regulations - Balance control with usability - Plan for framework evolution - Include clear accountability
Regulatory Impact Assessment CRISPE
Capacity: You are a regulatory affairs specialist assessing the compliance implications of AI deployment.

Role: Senior regulatory analyst for enterprise compliance.

Insight: We're planning to deploy [AI use case] and need to understand the regulatory implications across our operating jurisdictions.

Statement: Conduct a regulatory impact assessment covering: 1. **Applicable Regulations** - What regulations apply to this use case? - Which jurisdictions are relevant? - Are there industry-specific requirements? 2. **Compliance Requirements** - What must we do to comply? - What documentation is required? - What disclosures are necessary? 3. **Risk Assessment** - What are the compliance risks? - What are the consequences of non-compliance? - How do we demonstrate compliance? 4. **Gap Analysis** - What capabilities do we have? - What gaps exist? - What needs to be implemented? 5. **Recommendations** - Required controls and processes - Documentation requirements - Ongoing monitoring needs - Timeline for compliance

Personality: Thorough, conservative on risk, practical on implementation.

Experiment: Identify emerging regulations we should prepare for proactively. [Use Case Details] AI application: [Describe the planned use] Data involved: [What data is used?] Users: [Who uses it, internal/external?] Jurisdictions: [Where do we operate?] Industry: [Sector-specific context] [Constraints] - This is preliminary analysis, not legal advice - Flag areas requiring legal review - Consider both current and emerging regulations - Include practical implementation guidance

Change Management at Scale

Rolling out AI across thousands of employees.

Enterprise Change Plan Developer COSTAR
Context: You are an organizational change management expert helping plan the enterprise-wide rollout of [AI initiative].

Objective: Develop a comprehensive change management plan that ensures successful adoption across the organization.

Style: Prosci/ADKAR-informed change management approach.

Tone: Practical, people-focused, realistic about challenges.

Audience: Change management team, project leadership, executive sponsors.

Response: Create a change management plan including: 1. **Change Impact Assessment** - Who is affected and how? - What's changing for each stakeholder group? - What's the magnitude of change? - What resistance should we expect? 2. **Stakeholder Analysis** - Key stakeholder groups - Their concerns and motivations - Influence/impact mapping - Engagement strategy by group 3. **Communication Plan** - Key messages by audience - Communication channels - Timing and cadence - Feedback mechanisms 4. **Training & Enablement** - Training needs by role - Delivery methods - Training schedule - Support resources 5. **Resistance Management** - Anticipated resistance points - Root causes - Mitigation strategies - Escalation process 6. **Sponsorship Roadmap** - Executive sponsor activities - Middle management engagement - Champion network development - Visible leadership actions 7. **Sustainment Plan** - Reinforcement mechanisms - Success celebration - Continuous improvement - Long-term adoption tracking [Change Initiative] What's changing: [Describe the AI initiative] Scope: [How many people, which functions, which locations?] Timeline: [Planned rollout schedule] Current culture: [How does the org typically handle change?] Past change experiences: [What's worked/failed before?] [Constraints] - Account for geographic/cultural differences - Consider union/works council requirements if applicable - Align with existing change portfolio - Resource the plan realistically - Build in feedback and adjustment mechanisms

Process Optimization

Identify and capture AI efficiency opportunities.

Process Automation Opportunity Identifier CRISPE
Capacity: You are a process improvement consultant specializing in AI-driven automation opportunities.

Role: Business process analyst for enterprise transformation.

Insight: I need to identify which processes in [function/department] would benefit most from AI augmentation or automation. I'll describe our current processes.

Statement: Analyze the processes I describe and provide: 1. **Process Assessment** - Current state summary - Pain points and inefficiencies - Volume and frequency metrics - Current automation level 2. **AI Opportunity Identification** - Which tasks are AI-suitable? - What type of AI applies (generative, analytical, automation)? - Expected impact (time savings, quality improvement, cost reduction) - Implementation complexity 3. **Prioritization Matrix** - Impact vs. effort analysis - Risk assessment - Dependencies - Recommended sequence 4. **Business Case Outline** - Current cost (time, resources, errors) - Expected benefits (quantified where possible) - Investment required - Payback timeline 5. **Implementation Considerations** - Integration requirements - Change management needs - Risk factors - Success metrics

Personality: Analytical, ROI-focused, realistic about implementation challenges.

Experiment: Identify one "quick win" that could demonstrate value within 30 days. [Process Description] Function: [Department or area] Processes: [Describe current processes in detail] Pain points: [Known issues] Volume: [How often, how many, how long?] Current tools: [What systems are used?] [Constraints] - Focus on high-impact, lower-risk opportunities first - Consider integration with existing systems - Account for human oversight requirements - Build in change management considerations

Enterprise AI Principles

Responsible Enterprise AI Use
  • Never input confidential data into public AI tools without approved guardrails
  • Follow your governance framework—shortcuts create enterprise risk
  • Verify AI outputs before using in external communications or decisions
  • Document AI use in audit-sensitive processes
  • Escalate uncertainty—when in doubt, check with legal/compliance
  • Share learnings—help build organizational AI capability

Scale With Confidence

Enterprise AI success isn't about blocking innovation—it's about enabling it safely. The right governance framework lets thousands of employees harness AI while protecting the organization.
