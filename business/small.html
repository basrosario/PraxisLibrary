Small Business Prompts

Size: 10-99 Employees Focus: Maximize Limited Resources Challenge: Compete with Bigger Players

Small businesses don't have marketing departments, HR teams, or data analysts. But with AI, you can get enterprise-quality output without enterprise costs. These prompts help you punch above your weight and compete with companies ten times your size.

Your Secret Weapon

Big companies have bureaucracy. You have speed. AI lets you move even faster while maintaining quality that used to require teams of specialists.

Marketing & Customer Acquisition

Get marketing results without marketing headcount.

Social Media Content Creator COSTAR
Context: You are a social media marketing specialist helping a small [type of business] create engaging content for their audience.

Objective: Generate a week's worth of social media content that builds brand awareness, engages followers, and drives business results.

Style: Match the brand voice I describe—authentic, not corporate or salesy.

Tone: [Specify: Professional, friendly, witty, educational, inspiring, etc.]

Audience: [Describe your ideal customer]

Response: Create a content calendar for [platform: Instagram/Facebook/LinkedIn/etc.]: **For each post, provide:** - Post copy (platform-appropriate length) - Suggested image/visual description - Relevant hashtags - Best posting time - Engagement prompt (call-to-action or question) **Content Mix:** - 2 educational/valuable posts (tips, how-tos, insights) - 2 engagement posts (questions, polls, conversation starters) - 1 promotional post (product/service highlight) - 1 behind-the-scenes/personality post - 1 social proof post (testimonial, results, milestone) [Business Context] Business: [What you do] Target audience: [Who are your customers?] Brand voice: [How should you sound?] Key messages: [What do you want people to know/remember?] Current promotions: [Anything to highlight?] [Constraints] - Keep it authentic—not corporate-speak - Each post should standalone but build brand consistency - Include variety in content types - Make them easy to execute (realistic visuals) - Focus on value to the audience, not just selling
Email Marketing Campaign CRISPE
Capacity: You are an email marketing specialist who creates campaigns that get opened, read, and acted upon.

Role: Direct response copywriter for small business email marketing.

Insight: I need to create an email [campaign type: welcome series, promotional, newsletter, re-engagement] for my [business type]. My email list is [describe your subscribers].

Statement: Create a [X]-email sequence that achieves [goal]: **For each email, provide:** 1. Subject line (+ 2 alternatives to test) 2. Preview text 3. Email body copy 4. Call-to-action 5. Suggested send timing (relative to previous) **Campaign structure:** - Email 1: [Purpose] - Email 2: [Purpose] - Email 3: [Purpose] [etc.]

Personality: Conversational, valuable, not pushy—like a helpful friend who happens to sell something.

Experiment: Include one "pattern interrupt" email that's different from the others to boost engagement. [Campaign Context] Business: [What you sell] Goal: [What action do you want?] Offer: [Any specific promotion or value prop?] Audience: [Who's receiving these?] Brand voice: [How should you sound?] [Constraints] - Subject lines under 50 characters - Emails should be scannable (short paragraphs, bullet points) - Each email should deliver value even if they don't buy - Include personalization opportunities [First Name], etc. - Mobile-friendly formatting
Local SEO Content Generator CRISP
Context: I run a [type of business] in [city/region] and want to improve my local search visibility. I need content that helps me rank for local searches.

Request: Create SEO-optimized content for my local business.

Instructions: Create the following: 1. **Homepage copy** (~300 words) - Incorporate location naturally - Highlight key services - Include local trust signals 2. **Service page template** (for [main service]) - Location + service keyword optimization - Local benefits emphasized - FAQ section with local angle 3. **Google Business Profile content** - Optimized business description - Response templates for reviews (positive and negative) - Q&A content to add 4. **Local blog post ideas** (5 topics) - Topics that incorporate local + service keywords - Brief outline for each

Style: Natural, helpful, locally relevant—not keyword-stuffed.

Parameters: - Target keywords: [list your target search terms] - Service area: [cities/neighborhoods you serve] - Differentiators: [what makes you unique locally] [Constraints] - Write for humans first, search engines second - Include location naturally, not forced - Focus on local trust (years in business, community involvement, etc.) - Include clear calls-to-action

Customer Service & Communication

Deliver great service without a call center.

Customer Response Template Library COSTAR
Context: You are helping a small business create professional, on-brand response templates for common customer situations.

Objective: Create a library of customizable response templates that save time while maintaining personal touch.

Style: Professional but warm—like a real person wrote it, not a bot.

Tone: [Match brand voice: Friendly, professional, casual, etc.]

Audience: Customers reaching out via email/message.

Response: Create templates for these scenarios: **Inquiry Responses:** - General inquiry / "Tell me more" - Pricing question - Availability question - Custom request **Order/Service:** - Order confirmation - Appointment confirmation - Shipping update - Delivery confirmation **Problem Resolution:** - Initial response to complaint - Apology for mistake (minor) - Apology for mistake (significant) - Resolution confirmation - Can't fulfill request **Follow-up:** - Thank you post-purchase - Feedback request - Review request - Re-engagement (lapsed customer) **For each template:** - Subject line (if email) - Body text with [CUSTOMIZATION BRACKETS] - Sign-off [Business Context] Business type: [What you do] Brand voice: [How you sound] Common issues: [What do customers typically ask/complain about?] [Constraints] - Templates should be 80% ready to send, 20% customization - Sound human, not robotic - Include empathy where appropriate - Make customization points obvious with [BRACKETS] - Keep them concise—respect customer time
Difficult Customer Conversation Helper CRISPE
Capacity: You are a customer service expert helping navigate difficult conversations while maintaining relationships.

Role: Customer experience coach for small businesses.

Insight: I have a challenging customer situation and I'm not sure how to respond. I want to resolve the issue while maintaining the relationship (if possible) and protecting my business.

Statement: Help me craft a response to this situation: 1. **Situation Analysis** - What's the customer really upset about? - What's within my control to fix? - What's reasonable to offer? 2. **Response Strategy** - Recommended approach - What to say and not say - What to offer (if anything) 3. **Draft Response** - Full written response I can customize - Alternative versions if my first approach doesn't work 4. **Prevention** - How could this be avoided in the future? - Process or policy changes to consider

Personality: Calm, professional, firm but fair.

Experiment: Prepare me for the worst-case response and how to handle escalation. [Situation] What happened: [Describe the situation] Customer's complaint: [What are they saying?] What I know: [Context—whose fault, what's already been done] Customer value: [New customer, regular, VIP, etc.] What I'm willing to offer: [What can you give to resolve?] [Constraints] - Protect my business while being fair - Don't be a pushover but don't be defensive - Focus on resolution, not who's right - Maintain possibility of future relationship - Know when to fire a customer (if warranted)

Proposal & Quote Generator COSTAR
Context: You are helping a small business create professional proposals that win business.

Objective: Generate a compelling proposal for [project/service] that clearly communicates value and makes it easy to say yes.

Style: Professional, client-focused, easy to scan.

Tone: Confident, helpful, focused on their outcomes.

Audience: [Describe the prospective client]

Response: Create a proposal including: 1. **Executive Summary** - What they need (show you understand) - What we'll deliver - Key benefits/outcomes - Investment overview 2. **Understanding Your Needs** - Restate their situation/challenges - Goals they're trying to achieve - Why this matters to them 3. **Our Solution** - What we'll do (scope of work) - How we'll do it (approach/methodology) - Deliverables list - Timeline 4. **Investment** - Pricing (clear breakdown) - What's included - Payment terms - Options if applicable 5. **Why Us** - Relevant experience - What makes us different - Social proof (brief) 6. **Next Steps** - Clear call-to-action - How to proceed - Validity period [Project Details] Client: [Who is this for?] Project: [What do they need?] Scope: [What you'll do] Pricing: [Your costs] Timeline: [Expected duration] Our strengths: [Why should they choose you?] [Constraints] - Focus on their outcomes, not our features - Make pricing clear, not confusing - Keep it concise—respect their time - Make it easy to say yes - Include clear next steps
Business Planning Assistant CRISPE
Capacity: You are a business advisor helping small business owners think through important decisions and plans.

Role: Strategic thinking partner for small business owners.

Insight: I'm trying to [plan for next year / decide on a big investment / evaluate a new opportunity / solve a business problem]. I need help thinking it through systematically.

Statement: Help me work through this decision/plan: 1. **Clarify the Goal** - What am I actually trying to achieve? - How will I know if it worked? - What's the timeline? 2. **Assess Current State** - Where am I now? - What assets/resources do I have? - What constraints exist? 3. **Explore Options** - What are the possible paths? - Pros/cons of each - Resource requirements 4. **Risk Assessment** - What could go wrong? - What's the downside? - How to mitigate risks? 5. **Decision Framework** - Key criteria for deciding - What matters most? - Recommendation 6. **Action Plan** - Specific next steps - Timeline - How to track progress

Personality: Thoughtful, practical, honest about risks but encouraging about possibilities.

Experiment: Play devil's advocate—challenge my assumptions and point out what I might be missing. [My Situation] Decision/Plan: [What are you working on?] Context: [Relevant background] Options I'm considering: [What are you thinking?] Concerns: [What worries you?] Resources: [What do you have to work with?] [Constraints] - Be realistic about small business resources - Help me think, don't just tell me what to do - Consider personal factors (lifestyle, risk tolerance, values) - Focus on practical, actionable outcomes

Thank You Note

Small Business AI Principles

Getting Maximum Value from AI
  • Start with time-wasters. Identify what eats your time but doesn't need your brain. That's where AI helps most.
  • Keep your voice. AI should sound like you, not like a robot. Edit ruthlessly to maintain authenticity.
  • Templates are gold. Create once, customize always. AI helps you build the template, you make it personal each time.
  • Don't over-automate relationships. AI for efficiency, humans for connection. Know the difference.
  • Verify important outputs. AI makes mistakes. Double-check anything customer-facing or high-stakes.
  • Your expertise matters. AI is fast but you know your customers, market, and business. Stay in the driver's seat.

You Have More Than You Think

Small businesses built this economy. You know your customers by name, you can make decisions in minutes, and you actually care about quality. AI doesn't replace those advantages—it amplifies them. Use it to do more of what only you can do.
