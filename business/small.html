Marketing & Customer Acquisition

Get marketing results without marketing headcount.

Social Media Content CreatorCOSTAR
[Context] You are a social media marketing specialist helping a small [type of business] create engaging content for their audience.
[Objective] Generate a week's worth of social media content that builds brand awareness, engages followers, and drives business results.
[Style] Match the brand voice I describe—authentic, not corporate or salesy.
[Tone] [Specify: Professional, friendly, witty, educational, inspiring, etc.]
[Audience] [Describe your ideal customer].
[Response] Create a content calendar for [platform]. For each post provide: copy, image description, hashtags, posting time, engagement prompt. Content mix: 2 educational posts (tips/how-tos), 2 engagement posts (questions/polls), 1 promotional post, 1 behind-the-scenes post, 1 social proof post (testimonial/results).
[Business Context] Business: [What you do]. Target audience: [Who are your customers]. Brand voice: [How you sound]. Key messages: [What to communicate]. Current promotions: [Anything to highlight].
[Constraints] Keep it authentic—not corporate-speak. Each post standalone but consistent. Include variety. Make visuals easy to execute. Focus on value, not just selling.
Email Marketing CampaignCRISPE
[Context] You are an email marketing specialist who creates campaigns that get opened, read, and acted upon.
[Role] Direct response copywriter for small business email marketing.
[Instruction] I need to create an email [campaign type: welcome series, promotional, newsletter, re-engagement] for my [business type]. Create a [X]-email sequence achieving [goal]. For each email provide: subject line (+ 2 alternatives), preview text, body copy, call-to-action, send timing. Include one "pattern interrupt" email that's different from the others.
[Style] Conversational, valuable, not pushy—like a helpful friend who happens to sell something.
[Parameters] Subject lines under 50 characters. Emails scannable (short paragraphs, bullets). Each email delivers value even without purchase. Include personalization [First Name].
[Example] Subject: "Quick question for you" | Preview: "No really, I want to know..." | Body: Opens with genuine question, pivots to value, soft CTA.
[Campaign Context] Business: [What you sell]. Goal: [What action]. Offer: [Promotion/value prop]. Audience: [Who's receiving]. Brand voice: [How you sound].
[Constraints] Mobile-friendly formatting. Value in every email. Scannable structure.
Local SEO Content GeneratorCRISP
[Context] I run a [type of business] in [city/region] and want to improve my local search visibility. I need content that helps me rank for local searches.
[Role] Local SEO content specialist helping small businesses get found online.
[Instruction] Create SEO-optimized content including: (1) Homepage copy (~300 words) - location naturally incorporated, key services, local trust signals; (2) Service page template for [main service] - location + service keywords, local benefits, FAQ with local angle; (3) Google Business Profile content - optimized description, review response templates (positive/negative), Q&A content; (4) Local blog post ideas (5 topics) - local + service keywords with brief outlines.
[Style] Natural, helpful, locally relevant—not keyword-stuffed.
[Parameters] Target keywords: [list terms]. Service area: [cities/neighborhoods]. Differentiators: [what makes you unique locally].
[Constraints] Write for humans first, search engines second. Include location naturally. Focus on local trust (years in business, community involvement). Include clear calls-to-action.

Customer Service & Communication

Deliver great service without a call center.

Customer Response Template LibraryCOSTAR
[Context] You are helping a small business create professional, on-brand response templates for common customer situations.
[Objective] Create a library of customizable response templates that save time while maintaining personal touch.
[Style] Professional but warm—like a real person wrote it, not a bot.
[Tone] [Match brand voice: Friendly, professional, casual, etc.]
[Audience] Customers reaching out via email/message.
[Response] Create templates for: Inquiries (general, pricing, availability, custom request), Order/Service (order confirmation, appointment confirmation, shipping update, delivery confirmation), Problem Resolution (initial complaint response, apology minor/significant, resolution confirmation, can't fulfill), Follow-up (thank you, feedback request, review request, re-engagement). For each template: subject line, body with [CUSTOMIZATION BRACKETS], sign-off.
[Business Context] Business type: [What you do]. Brand voice: [How you sound]. Common issues: [What customers ask/complain about].
[Constraints] 80% ready to send, 20% customization. Sound human, not robotic. Include empathy. Use [BRACKETS] for customization. Keep concise.
Difficult Customer Conversation HelperCRISPE
[Context] You are a customer service expert helping navigate difficult conversations while maintaining relationships.
[Role] Customer experience coach for small businesses.
[Instruction] I have a challenging customer situation. Help me craft a response covering: (1) Situation Analysis - what they're really upset about, what's in my control, what's reasonable to offer; (2) Response Strategy - approach, what to say/not say, what to offer; (3) Draft Response - full response I can customize, alternatives if first approach fails; (4) Prevention - how to avoid in future, process/policy changes.
[Style] Calm, professional, firm but fair.
[Parameters] Prepare me for worst-case response and how to handle escalation.
[Example] "I hear that you're frustrated about [issue]. That's not the experience we want you to have. Here's what I can do: [solution]. Would that work for you?"
[Situation] What happened: [Describe]. Customer's complaint: [What they're saying]. Context: [Whose fault, what's been done]. Customer value: [New/regular/VIP]. What I'll offer: [Resolution options].
[Constraints] Protect business while being fair. Don't be pushover or defensive. Focus on resolution, not blame. Maintain relationship possibility. Know when to fire a customer.

Proposal & Quote GeneratorCOSTAR
[Context] You are helping a small business create professional proposals that win business.
[Objective] Generate a compelling proposal for [project/service] that clearly communicates value and makes it easy to say yes.
[Style] Professional, client-focused, easy to scan.
[Tone] Confident, helpful, focused on their outcomes.
[Audience] [Describe the prospective client].
[Response] Create a proposal including: (1) Executive Summary - their need, our delivery, key benefits, investment overview; (2) Understanding Your Needs - their situation/challenges, goals, why it matters; (3) Our Solution - scope of work, approach, deliverables, timeline; (4) Investment - pricing breakdown, what's included, payment terms, options; (5) Why Us - experience, differentiators, social proof; (6) Next Steps - clear CTA, how to proceed, validity period.
[Project Details] Client: [Who]. Project: [What they need]. Scope: [What you'll do]. Pricing: [Costs]. Timeline: [Duration]. Strengths: [Why choose you].
[Constraints] Focus on outcomes, not features. Clear pricing. Concise. Easy to say yes. Clear next steps.
Business Planning AssistantCRISPE
[Context] You are a business advisor helping small business owners think through important decisions and plans.
[Role] Strategic thinking partner for small business owners.
[Instruction] I'm trying to [plan for next year / decide on investment / evaluate opportunity / solve problem]. Help me work through it: (1) Clarify the Goal - what I'm trying to achieve, success metrics, timeline; (2) Assess Current State - where I am, resources, constraints; (3) Explore Options - possible paths, pros/cons, resource requirements; (4) Risk Assessment - what could go wrong, downside, mitigation; (5) Decision Framework - key criteria, priorities, recommendation; (6) Action Plan - next steps, timeline, progress tracking.
[Style] Thoughtful, practical, honest about risks but encouraging about possibilities.
[Parameters] Play devil's advocate—challenge my assumptions and point out what I might be missing.
[Example] "Before expanding to a second location, have you tested: Can your current systems handle 2x volume? Who will manage location 2? What's your break-even timeline?"
[My Situation] Decision/Plan: [What you're working on]. Context: [Background]. Options: [What you're thinking]. Concerns: [Worries]. Resources: [What you have].
[Constraints] Be realistic about small business resources. Help me think, don't just tell me what to do. Consider personal factors. Focus on practical outcomes.

Small Business AI Principles

Getting Maximum Value from AI
  • Start with time-wasters. Identify what eats your time but doesn't need your brain. That's where AI helps most.
  • Keep your voice. AI should sound like you, not like a robot. Edit ruthlessly to maintain authenticity.
  • Templates are gold. Create once, customize always. AI helps you build the template, you make it personal each time.
  • Don't over-automate relationships. AI for efficiency, humans for connection. Know the difference.
  • Verify important outputs. AI makes mistakes. Double-check anything customer-facing or high-stakes.
  • Your expertise matters. AI is fast but you know your customers, market, and business. Stay in the driver's seat.

You Have More Than You Think

Small businesses built this economy. You know your customers by name, you can make decisions in minutes, and you actually care about quality. AI doesn't replace those advantages—it amplifies them. Use it to do more of what only you can do.
