Operations & Scaling

Build processes that grow with you.

Process Documentation BuilderCOSTAR
[Context] You are helping a growing business document their processes so they can be trained consistently and scaled effectively.
[Objective] Create comprehensive process documentation that enables consistent execution and easy onboarding of new team members.
[Style] Clear, step-by-step documentation with visual elements where helpful.
[Tone] Professional but accessible, assuming someone new to the role is reading.
[Audience] New employees, team members covering for each other, future process improvers.
[Response] Create a Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) for [process name]: (1) Process Overview - purpose, owner, frequency, inputs, outputs; (2) Step-by-Step Instructions - numbered steps, decision points, examples, mistakes to avoid; (3) Tools & Access Required - systems, logins, resources; (4) Quality Checkpoints - verification, error checks, escalation; (5) Troubleshooting - common problems, who to contact, FAQs; (6) Revision History - version, date, changes.
[Process to Document] Process name: [What is this?]. Current state: [How it works today]. Who does it: [Role/team]. Tools used: [Systems]. Known issues: [What goes wrong].
[Constraints] Write for someone with no prior knowledge. Include actual examples. Flag confusing steps. Make it searchable and scannable.
Bottleneck IdentifierCRISPE
[Context] You are an operations consultant specializing in helping growing businesses identify and eliminate bottlenecks.
[Role] Process improvement analyst for scale-up businesses.
[Instruction] Our team is struggling to keep up with demand. Analyze our workflow and help us identify: (1) Bottleneck Analysis - where work accumulates, constraint limiting throughput, capacity/capability/process issue; (2) Root Cause Investigation - why it exists, what created it, real bottleneck or symptom; (3) Quick Relief Options - this week's actions, temporary workarounds, quick tweaks; (4) Systemic Solutions - permanent fixes, process redesign, automation opportunities, investments needed; (5) Prevention - how to avoid as we grow, early warning indicators, scalability considerations.
[Style] Practical, focused on quick wins and sustainable solutions.
[Parameters] Apply Theory of Constraints thinking—if we fix this bottleneck, where will the new one appear?
[Example] "The bottleneck is approval signoff. Work backs up for 48-72 hours waiting for one person. Quick fix: delegate approval for items under $X. Systemic: implement tiered approval authority."
[Workflow Description] Process: [End-to-end workflow]. Symptoms: [What you're experiencing]. Volume: [How much work, what changed]. Team: [Who's involved, capacity]. Tools: [Systems used].
[Constraints] Solutions must be realistic for our size. Consider immediate and long-term fixes. Account for human element. Help prioritize what to fix first.

People & Culture

Build and retain the team you need to scale.

Role Definition DeveloperCOSTAR
[Context] You are an HR consultant helping a growing company define roles clearly as they scale and specialize.
[Objective] Create clear role definitions that help with hiring, performance management, and career development.
[Style] Professional HR documentation that's also useful for actual humans.
[Tone] Clear, specific, motivating—not corporate jargon.
[Audience] Hiring managers, candidates, current role holders, HR.
[Response] Create a comprehensive role definition for [position]: (1) Role Summary - what it does, why it matters, where it fits; (2) Key Responsibilities (5-8 items) - duties, time allocation, success indicators; (3) Required Skills & Experience - must-have, nice-to-have, experience level; (4) Success Metrics - performance measures, key results, quality indicators; (5) Growth Path - next roles, skills to develop, timeline; (6) Working Relationships - reports to, direct reports, collaborators; (7) Compensation Context - level, market positioning.
[Role Context] Position: [Title]. Department: [Location]. Current state: [New/existing]. Why needed: [Problem solved]. Similar roles: [Comparables].
[Constraints] Be specific enough to evaluate candidates. Be flexible for growth. Focus on outcomes. Avoid gendered language. Make it realistic—not a unicorn hunt.
Onboarding Program DesignerCRISPE
[Context] You are an organizational development specialist creating onboarding programs that accelerate new hire productivity.
[Role] Employee experience designer for growing companies.
[Instruction] We're hiring more people and our onboarding is ad-hoc. Design an onboarding program for [role type] covering: Week 1 Foundation - Day 1 experience, setup, introductions, culture; Weeks 2-4 Learning - training, shadowing, quick wins, check-ins; Months 2-3 Contributing - responsibility increase, mentorship, feedback, first projects; 90-Day Checkpoint - assessment criteria, conversation guide, decision framework, development plan. For each phase include: activities, timeline, resources, who's responsible, success indicators.
[Style] Practical, employee-centric, focused on time-to-productivity.
[Parameters] Include a "new hire feedback survey" to continuously improve the program.
[Example] "Day 1: 9am laptop/access setup, 10am CEO welcome video, 11am buddy lunch, 1pm team introductions, 3pm first task walkthrough with manager."
[Company Context] Company size: [Employees]. Role type: [What role]. Current onboarding: [What we do now]. Common struggles: [What goes wrong]. Culture: [What new hires should understand].
[Constraints] Manageable for managers. Include remote/hybrid. Flexible for different learning speeds. Focus on relationship building.

Sales & Growth

Scale revenue without scaling chaos.

Sales Process OptimizerCOSTAR
[Context] You are a sales operations consultant helping a growing company build a scalable, repeatable sales process.
[Objective] Analyze our current sales approach and help us build a process that can scale with more reps.
[Style] Practical sales methodology with clear stages and actions.
[Tone] Results-focused, realistic, seller-friendly.
[Audience] Sales leadership, sales reps, revenue operations.
[Response] Based on our current approach, help us define: (1) Sales Process Stages - name, definition, entry/exit criteria, activities, timeframe; (2) Qualification Framework - qualified opportunity criteria, disqualification signals, required info per stage; (3) Sales Playbook Elements - key questions, objection responses, competitive positioning, value props by persona; (4) Metrics & Reporting - conversion rates, velocity, leading indicators, rep performance; (5) Tool & Technology - CRM config, enablement needs, automation opportunities.
[Current Sales Context] Product/Service: [What we sell]. Target customer: [Who]. Average deal size: [Value]. Current process: [How sales works]. Pain points: [What's not working]. Team size: [Reps].
[Constraints] Process must work for our deal complexity. Keep simple enough reps will follow. Build in coaching touchpoints. Focus on buyer journey.
Customer Success FrameworkCRISPE
[Context] You are a customer success strategist helping companies retain and grow their customer base.
[Role] Customer success program designer for B2B companies.
[Instruction] We've grown our customer base but retention and expansion could be better. Help us build a customer success framework: (1) Customer Journey Mapping - lifecycle stages, critical moments, risk signals, expansion opportunities; (2) Success Metrics Definition - health score components, leading/lagging indicators, at-risk triggers, expansion signals; (3) Engagement Model - touch cadence by segment, QBR structure, proactive triggers, escalation paths; (4) Playbooks - onboarding, adoption, at-risk intervention, expansion, renewal; (5) Team Structure - CSM ratios by segment, responsibilities, handoff processes.
[Style] Customer-obsessed, data-driven, practical.
[Parameters] Design a "customer advisory board" structure to deepen relationships with top accounts.
[Example] "Health Score = Product Usage (40%) + Engagement (30%) + Support Tickets (15%) + Expansion Signals (15%). Red flag: Usage drops >20% over 2 weeks."
[Customer Context] Business model: [SaaS, services]. Customer segments: [How you segment]. Current retention: [Rate]. Team size: [CS resources]. Pain points: [What's not working].
[Constraints] Scalable with limited CS resources. Balance high-touch and tech-touch. Focus on outcomes. Include customer perspective.

Scale-Up Principles

Building for Scale
  • Document before you forget. The process in your head needs to be on paper before you hire the next person.
  • Automate the repetitive. If you do it more than twice a week, find a way to automate or templatize it.
  • Hire ahead of the curve. By the time you desperately need someone, you're already behind.
  • Communicate more than you think you need to. As you grow, information doesn't travel automatically.
  • Build systems, not dependencies. If only one person can do it, you have a risk, not a process.
  • Measure what matters. But don't measure everything—focus on the metrics that drive decisions.

The Messy Middle Is Where Winners Are Made

Scaling a mid-size business is harder than starting a company or running an enterprise. You have the complexity without the resources. AI helps you punch above your weight—use it to build the infrastructure that will support your next stage of growth.
