Why Business Size Matters for AI

Different Scale, Different Challenges

Your AI strategy should match your reality. Here's how business size shapes AI needs:


Enterprise (1000+ employees)

Challenges: Complex processes, compliance requirements, change management at scale, integration with legacy systems, governance and oversight.

AI Focus: Process automation, strategic analysis, enterprise-wide consistency, risk management.

Mid-Size (100-999 employees)

Challenges: Growing pains, limited specialist staff, need for efficiency without enterprise budgets, balancing growth and stability.

AI Focus: Scaling operations, knowledge management, customer experience, operational efficiency.

Small Business (10-99 employees)

Challenges: Wearing many hats, limited time and resources, competing with bigger players, customer relationship building.

AI Focus: Marketing amplification, customer service, administrative relief, competitive intelligence.

Startups & Solopreneurs

Challenges: Doing everything yourself, bootstrapping, building from scratch, finding product-market fit, time poverty.

AI Focus: Multiplying individual capacity, content creation, idea validation, rapid iteration.

Universal Business Prompts

These prompts work for any business size. Customize them to your context.

Competitor Analysis COSTAR
Context: You are a competitive intelligence analyst helping a [business type/industry] understand their competitive landscape.

Objective: Analyze the competitive position and identify strategic opportunities based on publicly available information.

Style: Strategic business analysis with actionable recommendations.

Tone: Professional, objective, insight-driven.

Audience: Business owner/leadership team making strategic decisions.

Response: Based on what I tell you about my business and competitors, provide: 1. **Competitive Overview** - Who are the main competitors (based on what I provide)? - How does each position themselves? - What are their apparent strengths/weaknesses? 2. **Differentiation Analysis** - Where do we overlap? - Where are the gaps in the market? - What makes us unique (or could)? 3. **Opportunity Identification** - Underserved customer segments - Unmet needs we could address - Positioning opportunities 4. **Threat Assessment** - What should we watch for? - Emerging competitors? - Industry shifts? 5. **Recommended Actions** - Short-term quick wins - Medium-term strategic moves - Things to avoid [Business Information] My business: [Describe your business, products/services, target market] Main competitors: [List known competitors and what you know about them] My current positioning: [How do you differentiate currently?] Key questions: [What specifically do you want to understand?] [Constraints] - Base analysis only on information I provide or publicly available facts - Acknowledge uncertainty where relevant - Focus on actionable insights, not just observations - Consider my resource constraints when recommending actions
Customer Persona Developer CRISPE
Capacity: You are a marketing strategist who specializes in customer research and persona development.

Role: Customer insight consultant helping businesses understand their target audience deeply.

Insight: I need to develop detailed customer personas for my [business/product]. I have some information about my current customers but want to develop richer, more actionable personas.

Statement: Help me create [X] detailed customer personas that include: For each persona: 1. **Demographics & Background** - Age range, location, occupation, income level - Life situation and context 2. **Psychographics** - Values, attitudes, lifestyle - How they see themselves - What they aspire to 3. **Pain Points & Needs** - What problems do they face? - What frustrates them? - What are they trying to achieve? 4. **Buying Behavior** - How do they make decisions? - What influences them? - What objections might they have? 5. **How to Reach Them** - Where do they spend time (online/offline)? - What content resonates with them? - What messaging approach works? 6. **How We Serve Them** - What do we offer that solves their problems? - What would make them choose us? - What could lose them?

Personality: Insightful, empathetic, focused on real human understanding.

Experiment: For each persona, suggest one "day in the life" scenario showing how they'd encounter and use our product/service. [My Business Context] Business: [What you sell/do] Current customers: [What you know about them] Customer feedback themes: [Common things customers say] Best customers: [Describe your ideal customer] [Constraints] - Base personas on the data I provide, not assumptions - Make them specific enough to be actionable - Include both rational and emotional factors - Consider anti-personas (who we're NOT for)
Business Problem Solver COSTAR
Context: You are a business consultant helping solve a specific operational or strategic challenge.

Objective: Help me think through a business problem systematically and identify potential solutions.

Style: Structured problem-solving approach (like a consulting framework).

Tone: Thoughtful, analytical, practical.

Audience: Business owner/manager who needs to make a decision.

Response: Walk me through solving this problem: 1. **Problem Clarification** - Restate the problem as you understand it - Ask clarifying questions if needed - Identify the root cause vs. symptoms 2. **Context Analysis** - What factors contribute to this problem? - What constraints do we have? - Who is affected? 3. **Option Generation** - What are possible solutions? - What are the pros/cons of each? - What resources does each require? 4. **Recommendation** - What do you suggest and why? - What's the implementation approach? - What could go wrong and how to mitigate? 5. **Action Plan** - Immediate next steps - How to measure success - When to reassess [My Problem] The problem: [Describe the specific challenge] Context: [Relevant background] What I've tried: [Previous attempts to solve it] Constraints: [Budget, time, resources, etc.] Ideal outcome: [What does success look like?] [Constraints] - Solutions must be realistic for my situation - Prioritize quick wins alongside longer-term fixes - Consider unintended consequences - Help me think, don't just tell me what to do

Getting ROI from AI

Where AI Creates Business Value
Time Savings

Automate repetitive tasks: email drafting, report generation, data analysis, scheduling, research. Calculate: hours saved x hourly rate = value.

Quality Improvement

Better first drafts, fewer errors, more consistent output, stronger analysis. Measure: reduction in revision cycles, customer satisfaction.

Capability Extension

Do things you couldn't before: analyze data without a data scientist, create marketing without an agency, research without a team.

Decision Quality

Better information, more perspectives considered, faster analysis. Harder to measure but often the highest value.
