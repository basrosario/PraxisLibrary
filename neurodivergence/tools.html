AI Tools by Challenge

Find tools that address your specific needs

Executive Function & Organization

Tools for task management, planning, time awareness, and decision support.

Goblin Tools

Best for: ADHD task breakdown

What it does: Magic To-Do breaks tasks into smaller steps. Formalizer adjusts tone. Judge estimates formality level.

Cost: Free

Why it's great: Designed specifically by/for neurodivergent users.

Reclaim.ai

Best for: Automatic calendar management

What it does: AI schedules tasks, protects focus time, creates buffer between meetings, manages habits.

Cost: Free tier / Paid plans

Why it's great: Removes decision fatigue from scheduling.

Notion AI

Best for: Project organization and planning

What it does: AI summarizes, brainstorms, creates action items, organizes information.

Cost: Free tier / AI features paid

Why it's great: All-in-one workspace with AI built in.

Todoist with AI

Best for: Simple task management

What it does: Natural language task entry, smart scheduling, priority suggestions.

Cost: Free tier / Pro paid

Why it's great: Quick capture without overthinking organization.

Communication & Social

Tools for email, tone checking, and social navigation.

Grammarly

Best for: Real-time writing assistance

What it does: Grammar/spelling check, tone detection, clarity suggestions, rewriting.

Cost: Free tier / Premium paid

Why it's great: Browser extension works everywhere. Catches tone issues before sending.

ChatGPT / Claude

Best for: Email drafting, social scripts, tone checking

What it does: Drafts messages, explains implied meanings, creates conversation scripts.

Cost: Free tier / Paid plans

Why it's great: Flexibleâ€”can handle any communication scenario.

Otter.ai

Best for: Meeting transcription and review

What it does: Records and transcribes meetings, highlights key points, allows review later.

Cost: Free tier / Pro paid

Why it's great: Review conversations you might have misunderstood in real-time.

Loom

Best for: Async video communication

What it does: Quick screen recordings with AI summaries, transcripts, and chapters.

Cost: Free tier / Pro paid

Why it's great: Communicate complex topics without real-time pressure.

Reading & Writing Support

Tools for text processing, summarization, and writing assistance.

Speechify / Natural Reader

Best for: Text-to-speech

What it does: Reads any text aloudâ€”documents, web pages, emails. Adjustable speed and voices.

Cost: Free tier / Premium paid

Why it's great: Listen instead of read. Works on mobile and desktop.

Immersive Reader (Microsoft)

Best for: Built-in reading support

What it does: Adjusts font, spacing, colors. Includes text-to-speech. Built into Office, Edge, OneNote.

Cost: Free (included in Microsoft products)

Why it's great: No extra installâ€”already in tools you use.

TLDR This

Best for: Quick article summarization

What it does: Summarizes articles and documents into key points.

Cost: Free tier / Paid plans

Why it's great: Browser extension for instant summaries while browsing.

Whisper / Voice Notes

Best for: Voice-to-text transcription

What it does: Transcribes spoken words to text with high accuracy.

Cost: Various (Whisper is open source)

Why it's great: Dictate instead of type. Great for dyslexia.

Focus & Sensory Management

Tools for concentration, environment management, and sensory support.

Brain.fm

Best for: AI-generated focus music

What it does: Creates music scientifically designed to improve focus, relaxation, or sleep.

Cost: Subscription

Why it's great: Not distracting like regular music. Actually helps focus.

Freedom / Cold Turkey

Best for: Blocking distractions

What it does: Blocks websites and apps during focus time. Can't be easily bypassed.

Cost: Free tier / Premium paid

Why it's great: Removes temptation rather than requiring willpower.

Focusmate

Best for: Virtual body doubling

What it does: Pairs you with someone else for focused work sessions via video.

Cost: Free tier / Paid plans

Why it's great: Accountability without needing a friend available.

Krisp

Best for: AI noise cancellation

What it does: Removes background noise from calls and recordings.

Cost: Free tier / Pro paid

Why it's great: Reduces sensory overwhelm during calls.

General-Purpose AI Assistants

Versatile AI tools that can help with multiple challenges.

ChatGPT (OpenAI)

Best for: Everythingâ€”most versatile

What it does: Conversation, writing, coding, analysis, summarization, brainstorming.

Cost: Free / Plus $20/mo

Why it's great: Can do almost anything. Mobile app with voice input.

Claude (Anthropic)

Best for: Long documents, nuanced conversation

What it does: Similar to ChatGPT. Better at long contexts and following complex instructions.

Cost: Free / Pro $20/mo

Why it's great: Excellent for analyzing long documents and thoughtful responses.

Google Gemini

Best for: Google integration, multimodal

What it does: AI assistant integrated with Google services. Can analyze images.

Cost: Free / Advanced paid

Why it's great: Works with your Google docs, email, etc.

Microsoft Copilot

Best for: Microsoft Office integration

What it does: AI built into Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Outlook.

Cost: Included with Microsoft 365 Copilot

Why it's great: AI directly in the apps you're already using.
