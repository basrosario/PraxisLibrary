ADHD-Friendly Prompting Tips

How to get better results when your brain works this way

Ask for Bullet Points

Walls of text are the enemy. Always add: "Give me this in bullet points" or "Keep it to 5 sentences max."

Request ONE Thing

When overwhelmed, specifically ask: "Tell me the ONE thing to do first" or "Make the decision for me." Remove the paralysis.

Use Voice Input

Speaking is faster than typing for ADHD brains. Use voice-to-text to dump thoughts, then ask AI to organize them.

Ask for Time Estimates

Always ask "How long will this actually take?" and specify "Assume I underestimate by 50%" to get realistic times.

Request Immediate Actions

Ask for "the first physical action" or "something I can do in under 2 minutes." Specificity beats vague advice.

Build in Accountability

Ask AI to be your body double: "Check in with me every 15 minutes" or "Ask me what I've accomplished."
