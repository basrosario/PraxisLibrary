AI Tools That Work for Dyslexia

Specific tools and how to use them for dyslexic needs

Natural Reader / Speechify

Best for: Text-to-speech, listening instead of reading

Dyslexia Tip: Use for emails, documents, articles. Adjust speed to your preference. Many integrate with browsers for web content.

ChatGPT / Claude

Best for: Summarization, simplification, writing assistance, explaining complex text

Dyslexia Tip: Use voice input to avoid typing. Ask for "simple language" and "bullet points" consistently.

Grammarly

Best for: Real-time spelling/grammar correction, catching errors as you type

Dyslexia Tip: Use the browser extension everywhere. Don't stress about errors while writingâ€”let Grammarly catch them after.

Otter.ai / Whisper

Best for: Voice-to-text transcription, dictating instead of typing

Dyslexia Tip: Speak your first drafts. Use AI to then organize and polish the transcription. Much faster than typing.

Notion AI

Best for: Summarizing documents, rewriting content, organizing notes

Dyslexia Tip: Paste any text and ask AI to simplify, summarize, or convert to bullet points right in your workspace.

Immersive Reader (Microsoft)

Best for: Reading support built into Office, Edge, OneNote

Dyslexia Tip: Changes font, spacing, background colors, and includes text-to-speech. Free and built into tools you already use.

Dyslexia-Friendly Prompting Tips

How to get better results when text is challenging

Use Voice Input

Don't type your promptsâ€”speak them. Use your phone's voice keyboard, Otter.ai, or AI apps with voice input. Your ideas flow faster when spoken.

Always Ask for Bullet Points

Add "use bullet points" to every prompt. Walls of text are the enemy. Bullet points are scannable and easier to process.

Request Simple Language

Add "use simple words" or "write for a 10-year-old" to prompts. AI defaults to complex languageâ€”tell it to simplify.

Ask for the "One Thing"

When summarizing, always ask: "What's the ONE most important thing?" This forces AI to identify the core message.

Don't Editâ€”Replace

Instead of fixing your own writing, tell AI: "Rewrite this from scratch" with your key points. It's faster than editing.

Build a Prompt Library

Save prompts that work well for you. Copy-paste them when needed instead of writing new ones each time.
