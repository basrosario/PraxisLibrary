Quick Method Guide

When to use each approach

CRISP - General Purpose

Best for: Everyday tasks, simple requests, quick questions, general writing tasks.

Use when: You have a clear, straightforward task and know what you want.

Learn CRISP Framework →
CRISPE - Creative & Consistent

Best for: Creative writing, style matching, tasks requiring specific formatting or voice.

Use when: You have examples of what you want, or need consistent style across outputs.

Learn CRISPE Framework →
COSTAR - Audience-Focused

Best for: Professional communications, marketing content, anything where audience matters.

Use when: The recipient of your content is important and tone/style needs to match them.

Learn COSTAR Technique →
Chain-of-Thought - Step-by-Step Reasoning

Best for: Math problems, logical deductions, tasks requiring transparent step-by-step reasoning.

Use when: You need the AI to show its work or walk through a logical process.

Learn Chain-of-Thought →
Tree of Thought - Multi-Path Exploration

Best for: Strategic decisions, brainstorming, evaluating multiple solution paths.

Use when: You need to compare approaches, weigh pros and cons, or explore alternatives.

Learn Tree of Thought →
Self-Refine - Iterative Improvement

Best for: Polishing drafts, iterating on quality, refining outputs through feedback.

Use when: Your first result is good but needs improvement, or quality is paramount.

Learn Self-Refine →
Self-Consistency - Reliable Answers

Best for: High-stakes accuracy, consensus-based answers, validation of results.

Use when: Accuracy is critical and you want the most reliable answer possible.

Learn Self-Consistency →
Few-Shot Learning - Learn from Examples

Best for: Classification tasks, pattern-following, when you have examples to guide the AI.

Use when: You can provide sample inputs and outputs to teach the pattern you want.

Learn Few-Shot Learning →
ReAct - Complex Problems

Best for: Research, analysis, multi-step problems requiring reasoning and action.

Use when: You need to understand the AI's thinking process or verify its reasoning.

Learn ReAct Framework →
Flipped Interaction - Exploration

Best for: When you're unsure what you need, exploring options, getting personalized advice.

Use when: You want the AI to ask you questions first to better understand your situation.

Learn Flipped Interaction →
Code Prompting - Programming Tasks

Best for: Writing code, debugging, software development, database queries, test generation.

Use when: Your task involves programming, scripting, or working with code in any language.

Learn Code Prompting →
Image Prompting - Visual Tasks

Best for: Image generation, visual analysis, graphic design, style transfer, image editing.

Use when: Your task involves creating, analyzing, or transforming images and visual content.

Learn Image Prompting →
Audio Prompting - Sound & Speech

Best for: Speech-to-text, text-to-speech, music generation, audio classification, voice cloning.

Use when: Your task involves audio processing, voice work, music, or speech tasks.

Learn Audio Prompting →
Video Prompting - Video Tasks

Best for: Video generation, video analysis, temporal reasoning, captioning, video editing.

Use when: Your task involves creating, analyzing, or editing video and motion content.

Learn Video Prompting →
3D Prompting - Spatial Tasks

Best for: 3D model generation, spatial reasoning, scene understanding, pose estimation, point clouds.

Use when: Your task involves 3D content, spatial analysis, or volumetric data.

Learn 3D Prompting →
