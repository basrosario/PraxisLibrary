Learn to identify when AI makes things up. Build intuition for catching errors and verifying outputs.

Why This Matters

AI can sound confident while being completely wrong

What is a Hallucination?

When AI generates information that sounds plausible but is factually incorrect, made up, or unsupported. This happens because AI predicts likely text patterns, not truth.

Common Types

  • Fabricated facts, dates, or statistics
  • Non-existent sources or citations
  • Confident explanations of things it doesn't understand
  • Mixing up similar concepts or people

Red Flags to Watch

  • Overly specific details (exact percentages, dates)
  • Claims that seem too perfect or convenient
  • Confident statements about recent events
  • Technical details in specialized fields

How to Verify AI Outputs

1

Check Primary Sources

If AI cites a study, paper, or statistic, look it up. AI often invents convincing-sounding citations.

2

Cross-Reference

Compare AI's claims with multiple reliable sources. If you can't find corroboration, be skeptical.

3

Ask for Reasoning

Ask the AI to explain its reasoning or show its work. Hallucinations often fall apart under scrutiny.

4

Trust Domain Experts

For specialized topics (legal, medical, financial), always verify with qualified professionals.
