Prompt Patterns Library

Reusable frameworks for common tasks. These aren't templates to copy, but patterns to understand and adapt.

Chain of Thought

Reasoning

Encourage step-by-step reasoning by asking AI to "think through" problems before answering.

How to use:

"Let's think through this step by step..." or "Walk me through your reasoning."

Best for: Math, logic puzzles, complex analysis, debugging

Few-Shot Learning

Output

Provide 2-5 examples of the input-output format you want before your actual request.

How to use:

"Example 1: [input] → [output]. Example 2: [input] → [output]. Now do: [your input]"

Best for: Consistent formatting, classification, translation

Role Prompting

Role

Assign AI a specific role or expertise to shape its responses and vocabulary.

How to use:

"You are an experienced [role] who specializes in [domain]..."

Best for: Technical writing, advice, specialized content

Structured Output

Output

Define the exact format you want (JSON, table, outline) before the request.

How to use:

"Return your response in this format: {title: ..., summary: ..., points: [...]}"

Best for: Data extraction, API integration, consistent reports

Self-Consistency

Reasoning

Ask AI to verify its own work or approach a problem multiple ways and compare.

How to use:

"After your answer, verify it's correct." or "Solve this two different ways."

Best for: Math, fact-checking, critical decisions

Prompt Chaining

Reasoning

Break complex tasks into sequential prompts where each output feeds the next input.

How to use:

1. Research → 2. Outline → 3. Draft → 4. Edit (separate prompts)

Best for: Long documents, research, multi-step workflows

Devil's Advocate

Role

Ask AI to argue against a position or find weaknesses in an idea.

How to use:

"Play devil's advocate and critique this plan." or "What could go wrong?"

Best for: Decision making, risk assessment, strengthening arguments

Constraints First

Output

State limitations and requirements before the main request to frame the response.

How to use:

"Constraints: 200 words max, no jargon, audience is beginners. Task: Explain..."

Best for: Length control, audience targeting, style consistency

Flipped Interaction

Interaction

Ask AI to interview you first before giving advice. This prevents generic responses.

How to use:

"Before answering, ask me 5 questions to understand my situation better."

Best for: Personalized advice, complex decisions, strategic planning

How to Use These Patterns

1. Understand, Don't Copy

These patterns are principles, not scripts. Understand why they work, then adapt them to your specific needs.

2. Combine Patterns

Patterns work well together. Use Role + Chain of Thought for expert analysis, or Few-Shot + Structured Output for consistent formatting.

3. Iterate and Refine

Start with a pattern, see what works, then adjust. The best prompts come from experimentation.

4. Match Pattern to Task

Not every task needs every pattern. Simple questions don't need Chain of Thought. Match complexity to need.
