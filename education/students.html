AI for Students

Focus: Learning Enhancement Approach: Understand, Don't Copy Goal: Become AI-Literate

AI is a powerful study partner—but only if you use it to understand more deeply, not to skip learning. These prompts will help you study smarter, think more critically, and actually learn the material. The goal isn't to get answers; it's to get better.

The Secret to Using AI in School

The students who succeed with AI aren't the ones who use it to avoid work—they're the ones who use it to work smarter. AI can explain things differently, quiz you endlessly, and help you identify what you don't understand. That's powerful.

Using AI Ethically (Yes, This Matters)

The Line Between Help and Cheating

Before we get to the prompts, let's be real: there's a difference between using AI to learn and using AI to cheat. Here's how to stay on the right side:


AI Can Help You...
  • Understand concepts you're struggling with
  • Explain things in different ways
  • Quiz you and check your understanding
  • Help you brainstorm and outline
  • Give feedback on drafts YOU wrote
  • Find and explain your mistakes
But Don't Use AI To...
  • Write your essays or assignments
  • Do your homework for you
  • Answer test questions
  • Pretend you understand something you don't
  • Submit AI work as your own
  • Skip the learning process

The test is simple: After using AI, do you understand the material better? Could you explain it to someone else? If yes, you used AI correctly. If no, you just delayed failing.

Understanding Difficult Concepts

When the textbook doesn't make sense and you've read it three times.

Explain It Like I'm Confused CRISP
Context: I'm a [grade/year] student studying [subject]. I'm struggling to understand [specific concept].

Request: Explain [concept] to me in a way that makes sense. I've already tried reading the textbook but it's not clicking.

Instructions: - Start with a simple, everyday analogy - Break it down into steps - Explain WHY it works, not just WHAT it is - Use examples I can relate to - Tell me what most students get wrong about this

Style: Conversational, like a smart friend explaining it, not a textbook.

Parameters: After explaining, give me 3 quick questions to check if I actually understood it. Then let me try to explain it back to you.
Connect It To What I Know CRISPE
Capacity: You're a tutor who's really good at connecting new concepts to things students already understand.

Role: Patient tutor who explains complex topics using familiar examples.

Insight: I need to understand [topic/concept] for my [subject] class. I'm interested in [list your interests: gaming, sports, music, social media, cooking, etc.]

Statement: Explain [concept] using examples and analogies from my interests. Help me see how this concept shows up in things I already care about.

Personality: Engaging, uses references I'll actually get, not boring.

Experiment: At the end, challenge me to come up with my own analogy for this concept to make sure I really get it.
Socratic Learning Partner COSTAR
Context: You're my learning partner using the Socratic method—instead of giving me answers, you'll help me discover them through questions.

Objective: Help me truly understand [concept/topic] by guiding me to figure it out myself.

Style: Ask thought-provoking questions that lead me toward understanding. Don't just tell me the answer.

Tone: Encouraging, patient, curious—like we're exploring together.

Audience: Me, a student who wants to actually understand, not just memorize.

Response: Start by asking me what I already know or think about [topic]. Based on my answer, ask follow-up questions that: - Expose gaps in my understanding - Help me make connections - Challenge me to think deeper - Lead me to "aha!" moments If I get stuck, give me a hint through another question, not the answer. [Constraints] - Only give direct answers if I'm completely stuck after 3 attempts - Celebrate when I figure something out - Help me see WHY I was wrong, not just that I was wrong

Studying & Test Prep

Turn AI into your personal quiz master and study partner.

Custom Quiz Generator CRISP
Context: I have a test coming up on [topic/unit] in my [subject] class. I need to study but just re-reading notes doesn't work for me.

Request: Quiz me on this material to help me identify what I know and what I need to study more.

Instructions: - Give me one question at a time - Wait for my answer before giving the next question - After I answer, tell me if I'm right or wrong AND explain why - If I get something wrong, explain it, then ask a similar question later to see if I learned it - Mix up question types: multiple choice, short answer, true/false, explain-this

Style: Like a study session with a smart friend, not a formal test.

Parameters: - Cover these topics: [list specific topics/chapters] - Start easier and get harder - After 10-15 questions, summarize what I know well and what I need to review [What I'm supposed to know] [Paste your study guide, notes, or list of topics here]
Flashcard Creator CRISP
Context: I need to memorize information for [subject/topic]. I learn best with flashcards but making them takes forever.

Request: Create a set of flashcards based on the material I'll provide.

Instructions: - Front of card: Question or term - Back of card: Answer or definition - Make the questions test understanding, not just memorization - Group them by subtopic - Include some "application" cards that ask me to USE the information

Style: Clear, concise, effective for quick review.

Parameters: - Create [X] flashcards - Format them so I can copy into Anki/Quizlet easily - Star (*) the ones that are most likely to be on a test [Source Material] [Paste your notes, textbook sections, or study guide here]
Practice Problem Generator CRISPE
Capacity: You're a math/science tutor who creates practice problems that build understanding step by step.

Role: Practice problem generator for [subject].

Insight: I'm studying [specific topic/skill] and I need practice problems to build my skills. I'm currently at [beginner/intermediate/advanced] level.

Statement: Create a set of practice problems that: 1. Start at my level and gradually increase in difficulty 2. Cover different types/variations of this concept 3. Include the kind of problems that actually show up on tests 4. Have full solutions with step-by-step explanations

Personality: Encouraging but challenging—push me just past my comfort zone.

Experiment: After I work through a problem, ask me to explain my reasoning before showing the answer. [Constraints] - Create [X] problems - Include at least one word problem / real-world application - Show work for all solutions - Point out common mistakes for each problem type

Writing Assistance (Without Cheating)

Use AI to improve YOUR writing—not to write FOR you.

The Right Way to Use AI for Writing

Do: Brainstorm ideas, get feedback on YOUR drafts, strengthen YOUR arguments, fix grammar in YOUR work.

Don't: Have AI write your essay, copy AI-generated paragraphs, submit anything you couldn't have written yourself.

Brainstorming Partner CRISP
Context: I have a [type of assignment: essay, research paper, creative writing] due for [class]. The assignment is: [paste prompt or describe assignment]

Request: Help me brainstorm ideas—don't write anything for me, just help me think through possibilities.

Instructions: - Ask me questions about my initial thoughts - Suggest different angles I could take - Help me see connections I might be missing - Play devil's advocate on my ideas - Help me narrow down to a strong thesis/focus

Style: Collaborative brainstorming, building on MY ideas.

Parameters: By the end, I should have: - A clear thesis or central idea (that I came up with) - 3-4 supporting points to develop - An understanding of potential counterarguments [Constraints] - Don't write content for me—help me generate MY ideas - If I ask you to write something, remind me to do it myself - Ask questions that help me think, don't just give answers
Draft Feedback Requested CRISPE
Capacity: You're a writing tutor who gives constructive feedback that helps students improve.

Role: Peer reviewer and writing coach.

Insight: I wrote a draft of my [assignment type] and I want feedback before I revise it. I'm NOT asking you to rewrite it—I want to know what's working and what I can improve myself.

Statement: Read my draft and give me feedback on: 1. Is my thesis/main point clear? 2. Does my argument make sense? Any logic gaps? 3. What's the strongest part of my writing? 4. What's the weakest part? (Be honest) 5. Are there any confusing sentences? 6. What would make this better?

Personality: Honest but encouraging—tell me the truth but help me see how to fix it.

Experiment: Don't fix things for me. Point out issues and ask questions that help me figure out how to fix them myself. [My Draft] Assignment: [Describe what the assignment asked for] [Paste your draft here] [Constraints] - Give feedback, not rewrites - Be specific—point to exact sentences/paragraphs - Explain WHY something doesn't work, not just that it doesn't - End with 2-3 specific things I should focus on in revision
Thesis Strengthener CRISP
Context: I'm working on a thesis statement for my [type of paper] about [topic]. I have a draft but I'm not sure if it's strong enough.

Request: Help me strengthen my thesis—don't rewrite it, but help me see what's weak and guide me to improve it.

Instructions: My current thesis: "[Your thesis]" Evaluate it on: - Is it arguable? (Could someone disagree?) - Is it specific? (Or too vague?) - Is it interesting? (Does it say something worth saying?) - Does it answer the assignment question?

Style: Socratic—ask me questions that help me see the problems myself.

Parameters: - Don't write a new thesis for me - Point out specific weaknesses - Ask guiding questions to help me revise it - After I revise, evaluate the new version

Research & Information Literacy

AI can help you research—but you MUST verify everything.

Warning: AI Can Be Wrong

Important: AI sometimes makes up facts, citations, and statistics. This is called "hallucination." You MUST verify any information AI gives you before using it in your work. If you can't find a source, don't use the information.

Research Direction Finder COSTAR
Context: I'm researching [topic] for a [assignment type] in my [class]. I need help figuring out what to look for and where to start.

Objective: Help me develop a research strategy—don't give me facts to use, help me figure out where to find them myself.

Style: Like a librarian helping me plan my research.

Tone: Helpful, organized, focused on building my research skills.

Audience: A student who needs to learn HOW to research, not just get information.

Response: Help me with: 1. What are the key questions I should be trying to answer? 2. What search terms should I use? 3. What types of sources should I look for? (Academic journals, news, primary sources, etc.) 4. What databases or search tools would be helpful? 5. How do I evaluate if a source is reliable? 6. What different perspectives exist on this topic? [Constraints] - Don't give me facts—help me find them myself - Focus on building research SKILLS - Remind me to verify everything in reliable sources - Suggest specific databases (Google Scholar, JSTOR, etc.)
Source Evaluator CRISP
Context: I found a source for my research paper and I'm not sure if it's reliable enough to use.

Request: Help me evaluate this source using the CRAAP test (Currency, Relevance, Authority, Accuracy, Purpose).

Instructions: Source info: - Title: [Title] - Author: [Author] - Publication/Website: [Where published] - Date: [Publication date] - URL (if online): [URL] Walk me through evaluating this source: - Is it current enough for my topic? - Is the author credible? How do I check? - Is the publication reputable? - What might be the bias or purpose? - Should I use this source?

Style: Teaching me HOW to evaluate, not just yes/no.

Parameters: End with a recommendation and explain your reasoning.

Critical Thinking & Analysis

Use AI to sharpen your thinking, not replace it.

Argument Stress-Tester COSTAR
Context: I'm developing an argument for [assignment/debate/essay] and I want to make sure it's strong before I commit to it.

Objective: Challenge my argument. Find the weaknesses. Help me make it bulletproof.

Style: Debate partner who argues the other side.

Tone: Challenging but constructive—trying to make my argument better, not destroy me.

Audience: A student who wants to think critically about their own ideas.

Response: My argument: [State your argument/thesis] My main evidence/reasons: [List your supporting points] Please: 1. Tell me the strongest counterarguments 2. Point out any logical fallacies in my reasoning 3. Identify what evidence would make my argument weaker 4. Suggest how I could address each counterargument 5. Tell me what's actually STRONG about my argument [Constraints] - Be tough but fair - Don't just agree with me—push back - Help me see blind spots - After challenging, help me strengthen my position
Perspective Explorer CRISPE
Capacity: You help students understand multiple perspectives on complex issues.

Role: Discussion facilitator who presents all sides fairly.

Insight: I'm studying [topic/issue] and I want to understand the different perspectives people have on it—not just my own view.

Statement: Present [X] different perspectives on this issue: - What does each group believe? - WHY do they believe it? (Steel-man their argument) - What evidence does each side point to? - What do they think is most important? - Where might they actually agree?

Personality: Neutral and fair—present each view as if you believed it.

Experiment: At the end, help me think about which perspective I find most convincing and why—but don't tell me what to think. [Constraints] - Don't advocate for any position - Represent each view fairly and charitably - Acknowledge that smart people disagree on this - Help me form MY OWN informed opinion

Quick Prompt Starters

Copy and customize these for common study situations.

Pre-Reading Prep

"I'm about to read [chapter/article] about [topic]. What are the key concepts I should look for? What questions should I be asking as I read?"

Confusion Clarifier

"I thought I understood [concept], but then [specific confusion]. Where am I going wrong in my thinking?"

Study Schedule Helper

"I have [X] days until my [test/exam] and need to study [list of topics]. Help me create a study schedule that uses spaced repetition and active recall."

Memory Technique Creator

"I need to memorize [list of items]. Help me create mnemonics, memory palaces, or other techniques to remember these. Make them funny or weird so they stick."

Mistake Analyzer

"I got this problem wrong: [problem]. My answer was [your answer]. The correct answer is [correct answer]. Where did my thinking go wrong?"

Teaching Test

"Let me try to explain [concept] to you. Stop me whenever I say something wrong or unclear. [Then explain in your own words]"

A Final Word on Academic Integrity

Why This Actually Matters (For Real)

Look, we could lecture you about cheating, but here's the actual truth:


  • You're only cheating yourself. Tests and assignments exist to build skills you'll need later. Skip the learning, and you'll pay for it eventually.
  • AI detection is getting better. Teachers and schools are getting better at catching AI-generated work. It's not worth the risk.
  • The skills matter more than the grades. In your career, no one will care what you got on a paper. They'll care if you can actually write.
  • Learning to use AI well IS the skill. Using AI to cheat misses the point. Learning to use AI to learn BETTER? That's the future-proof skill.

The students who will succeed aren't the ones who use AI to avoid work. They're the ones who use AI to learn more, understand deeper, and work smarter.

Be the Student Who Gets It

AI is going to be part of every career in your future. The question isn't IF you'll use AI—it's whether you'll know HOW to use it well. Start now. Learn to use AI as a tool for understanding, not a shortcut for avoiding work.
