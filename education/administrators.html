AI for Administrators

Focus: Strategic Leadership Approach: Policy-Driven Adoption Goal: Future-Ready Institutions

Educational leaders face a choice: fear AI and fight a losing battle, or embrace it strategically and prepare students for the world they'll actually enter. These prompts help administrators develop thoughtful AI policies, support teachers, communicate with stakeholders, and lead institutional transformation.

Leadership in the AI Era

The institutions that thrive won't be the ones that banned AI the longest. They'll be the ones that taught their communities to use it wisely, ethically, and effectively.

Policy Development

Create AI policies that empower rather than restrict.

AI Acceptable Use Policy Framework COSTAR
Context: You are helping a [K-12 school district / college / university] develop a comprehensive AI Acceptable Use Policy that balances academic integrity with the reality that AI is now part of professional life.

Objective: Create a policy framework that addresses AI use by students, faculty, and staff while maintaining educational integrity and preparing students for AI-integrated careers.

Style: Formal policy language appropriate for board approval, but clear enough for all stakeholders to understand.

Tone: Balanced, forward-thinking, grounded in educational mission—not fear-based.

Audience: School board, faculty, students, parents, legal/compliance review.

Response: Develop a policy framework including: 1. **Preamble/Philosophy Statement** - Why the institution is embracing AI adoption - Connection to educational mission and student preparation 2. **Definitions Section** - What constitutes "AI tools" for this policy - Distinction between AI assistance and AI completion 3. **Student Use Guidelines** - Default expectations for assignments - How teachers can modify for specific assignments - Required disclosure/attribution practices - Examples of acceptable and unacceptable use 4. **Faculty Guidelines** - Freedom to set classroom-specific policies - Requirements for communicating AI policies to students - Guidance on AI-assisted grading and feedback - Professional development expectations 5. **Assessment Integrity** - How to design AI-resilient assessments - Protocol for suspected policy violations - Progressive response framework (education before punishment) 6. **Data Privacy and Security** - Approved AI tools list (if applicable) - Student data protection requirements - Age-appropriate use considerations 7. **Review and Update Process** - Annual review commitment - Stakeholder input mechanism - Adaptation protocol for new developments [Constraints] - Balance integrity with innovation - Avoid unenforceable rules - Consider equity (access disparities) - Account for disability accommodations - Align with existing academic integrity policies - Include implementation timeline
Academic Integrity Policy Update CRISPE
Capacity: You are an academic policy expert who specializes in updating institutional policies for the AI era.

Role: Policy consultant for educational institutions navigating AI integration.

Insight: Our current academic integrity policy was written before generative AI. I need to update it to address AI use while maintaining the policy's core values. The current policy is attached/described below.

Statement: Review our existing policy and recommend specific updates that: - Address AI as a tool category (like calculators, spell-check) - Distinguish between AI assistance and academic dishonesty - Provide clear guidance students can actually follow - Give faculty flexibility within a consistent framework - Maintain focus on learning outcomes, not just rule compliance

Personality: Pragmatic, legally careful, educationally grounded.

Experiment: For each recommendation, explain the rationale and potential pushback we might face, with suggested responses. [Current Policy] [Paste existing academic integrity policy here] [Constraints] - Maintain the spirit of the original policy - Identify which existing language needs changing vs. removing vs. keeping - Provide track-changes style recommendations - Consider enforcement feasibility - Address AI detection tool limitations

Faculty Support & Development

Help your teachers embrace AI as a teaching tool.

Professional Development Session Designer COSTAR
Context: You are designing professional development for faculty on integrating AI into their teaching practice and classroom management.

Objective: Create a PD session plan that moves teachers from AI anxiety to AI competency, with practical skills they can use immediately.

Style: Hands-on, practical, with actual time for teachers to experiment.

Tone: Encouraging, acknowledging concerns, focused on empowerment.

Audience: Teachers with varying levels of AI familiarity, from skeptics to enthusiasts.

Response: Design a [X-hour] professional development session including: 1. **Opening (15-20 min)** - Acknowledge the elephant in the room (fears, frustrations) - Frame AI as tool, not threat - Quick wins: Things AI can do TODAY to help 2. **Hands-On Exploration (30-45 min)** - Guided practice with AI tools - Prompt engineering basics - Creating differentiated materials - Generating assessment questions 3. **Classroom Application (20-30 min)** - How to set AI policies for your class - AI-resilient assignment design - Using AI to enhance (not replace) teaching 4. **Concern Processing (15-20 min)** - Small group discussion of concerns - Q&A with practical answers - Resource sharing 5. **Action Planning (10-15 min)** - One thing I'll try this week - Support resources available - Follow-up session dates [Constraints] - Include activities that work for both tech-savvy and tech-hesitant teachers - Provide takeaway resources (quick reference cards, prompt templates) - Address specific subject-area applications - Plan for teachers who may be resistant - Include time for hands-on practice, not just talking
Teacher AI Coaching Guide CRISPE
Capacity: You are an instructional coach helping teachers integrate AI into their practice effectively.

Role: AI integration coach for K-12 educators.

Insight: I need to support a teacher who [describe situation: is resistant to AI / is over-relying on AI / doesn't know where to start / is struggling with classroom AI policies]. Help me prepare for a coaching conversation.

Statement: Prepare me for this coaching conversation: 1. Opening questions to understand their perspective 2. Key points to make (with evidence/examples) 3. Potential objections and how to address them 4. Concrete next steps to suggest 5. Resources to share 6. Follow-up plan

Personality: Supportive, non-judgmental, practical—meet teachers where they are.

Experiment: Role-play the conversation so I can practice before the actual meeting. [Teacher Context] Subject/Grade: [Subject and grade level] Current stance on AI: [Describe] Specific challenge: [What's the issue?] School's AI policy: [Brief summary] [Constraints] - Respect teacher autonomy while maintaining standards - Focus on student learning outcomes - Acknowledge legitimate concerns - Build on teacher's existing strengths - Avoid condescension or dismissiveness

Stakeholder Communication

Communicate your AI strategy to parents, board, and community.

Parent Communication Template COSTAR
Context: You are helping a school administrator communicate the institution's AI approach to parents who may have concerns or questions.

Objective: Draft a parent communication (letter, email, or FAQ) that explains the school's AI policy in a way that builds confidence rather than anxiety.

Style: Warm, professional, educational—help parents understand the thinking behind decisions.

Tone: Confident but not dismissive of concerns, forward-looking, focused on student preparation.

Audience: Parents with varying levels of AI understanding, some concerned, some supportive.

Response: Create a communication that addresses: 1. **The Reality We're Preparing For** - AI in the workplace students will enter - Why adaptation beats avoidance 2. **Our Approach** - What we're teaching students about AI - How we're handling academic integrity - Our teacher preparation efforts 3. **Common Concerns Addressed** - "Won't students just cheat?" - "Are you still teaching real skills?" - "Is my child's data safe?" - "How do I help at home?" 4. **How Parents Can Partner** - Conversations to have at home - What to encourage/discourage - How to contact us with questions 5. **Invitation to Engage** - Upcoming information sessions - Resources for parents - Feedback mechanisms [Constraints] - Avoid jargon—assume no AI background - Don't be defensive about the approach - Acknowledge legitimate concerns before addressing them - Keep to one page if possible - Provide next steps for concerned parents
Board Presentation Framework CRISPE
Capacity: You are a strategic communications expert helping an education leader present to their board of directors/trustees.

Role: Executive presentation consultant for educational leadership.

Insight: I need to present our AI strategy to the [school board / board of trustees]. They will want to understand our approach, the risks we're managing, and why we've made these choices. Some board members may be skeptical or have heard negative AI stories.

Statement: Create a presentation framework (not the slides themselves) that includes: 1. **Executive Summary** (2 min) - The bottom line: What we're doing and why 2. **Context Setting** (5 min) - Why AI in education is unavoidable - What peer institutions are doing - The cost of inaction 3. **Our Strategy** (10 min) - Philosophy and principles - Key policy decisions - Implementation timeline - Resource requirements 4. **Risk Management** (5 min) - Risks we've identified - How we're addressing each - What we're monitoring 5. **Evidence and Outcomes** (5 min) - Early indicators (if available) - How we'll measure success - Benchmark comparisons 6. **Q&A Preparation** (not in presentation) - Likely questions and prepared answers - Topics to avoid/redirect

Personality: Confident, data-informed, strategic—speak the board's language.

Experiment: Anticipate the toughest question a skeptical board member might ask and prepare the response. [Constraints] - Total presentation: 25-30 minutes - Use concrete examples, not abstractions - Connect to institutional mission and strategic plan - Prepare for both supportive and challenging questions - Include a clear ask (approval, resources, support)

Administrative Operations

Use AI to streamline your own administrative work.

Report Writing Assistant CRISP
Context: I'm a [position: principal, department head, superintendent] writing a [report type: annual report, board update, accreditation self-study, grant application narrative].

Request: Help me draft sections of this report based on the data and information I'll provide. I'll provide the content—you help me write it professionally.

Instructions: Report purpose: [What decision or action should this support?] Audience: [Who reads this?] Required sections: [List the sections needed] Key messages: [What 2-3 things MUST come through?] Data/Information to incorporate: [Paste or list your data points, achievements, challenges, etc.]

Style: Professional, evidence-based, appropriate for [audience].

Parameters: - Length: [Target length per section or total] - Tone: [Formal/semi-formal] - Include: [Specific requirements—charts description, citations, etc.] [Constraints] - Ground all claims in the data I provide - Maintain appropriate confidentiality (no specific student/staff names) - Flag areas where more data would strengthen the claim - Write for the actual audience, not just to impress
Meeting Preparation Assistant CRISPE
Capacity: You are an executive assistant helping an administrator prepare for important meetings.

Role: Strategic meeting preparation consultant.

Insight: I have a meeting with [stakeholder: angry parent, union representative, board member, accreditation team, etc.] about [topic]. I need to prepare thoroughly.

Statement: Help me prepare for this meeting: 1. What questions should I expect? 2. What information should I have ready? 3. What are the stakeholder's likely priorities/concerns? 4. What outcomes should I aim for? 5. What pitfalls should I avoid? 6. Suggested talking points for key topics

Personality: Strategic, thorough, realistic about potential challenges.

Experiment: Role-play the most challenging part of the conversation so I can practice my responses. [Meeting Context] Who: [Describe the person/group] Topic: [What's the meeting about?] Background: [Relevant history or context] My goal: [What do I want to achieve?] Their likely goal: [What do they want?] [Constraints] - Assume they're coming with legitimate concerns - Prepare for emotional as well as logical arguments - Help me find win-win outcomes where possible - Include de-escalation strategies if needed
Data Analysis Interpreter COSTAR
Context: You are helping an educational administrator interpret assessment data, survey results, or other institutional metrics.

Objective: Help me understand what this data is telling us and what questions we should be asking next.

Style: Analytical but accessible—help me explain this to non-data-people.

Tone: Objective, inquiry-focused, honest about limitations.

Audience: Me (administrator), and secondarily the audiences I'll need to communicate with (board, faculty, parents).

Response: Here's the data: [Paste or describe the data] Please help me: 1. Identify the key findings (what does this actually tell us?) 2. Spot concerning trends or anomalies 3. Note limitations of this data (what can't we conclude?) 4. Suggest follow-up questions to investigate 5. Recommend how to present this to different audiences 6. Identify action implications (if any) [Constraints] - Don't overclaim—be honest about what data does/doesn't show - Consider alternative explanations for patterns - Distinguish correlation from causation - Note sample size and confidence issues - Suggest what additional data would help

Strategic AI Planning

Build a long-term strategy for AI integration.

AI Integration Roadmap Developer COSTAR
Context: You are helping an educational institution develop a multi-year AI integration strategy.

Objective: Create a phased roadmap for AI adoption that builds capability systematically while managing risk.

Style: Strategic planning document with clear milestones and accountability.

Tone: Ambitious but realistic, acknowledging constraints.

Audience: Senior leadership, board, strategic planning committee.

Response: Develop a 3-year AI integration roadmap: **Year 1: Foundation** - Policy development and approval - Faculty awareness and basic training - Pilot programs in volunteer classrooms - Infrastructure assessment - Success metrics establishment **Year 2: Expansion** - Broad faculty professional development - Curriculum integration guidelines - Student AI literacy program - Expanded tool adoption - Assessment of Year 1 pilots **Year 3: Optimization** - Full integration into practice - Advanced applications - Continuous improvement processes - Preparation for next technology evolution - Outcome assessment For each phase, specify: - Key milestones - Resource requirements - Risk factors - Success indicators - Responsible parties [Institution Context] Type: [K-12 / Higher Ed / District] Size: [Student population, faculty count] Current state: [Where are we now with AI?] Key constraints: [Budget, culture, etc.] Strategic priorities: [How does AI connect to broader goals?] [Constraints] - Account for budget cycles - Consider change management (people don't change overnight) - Build in evaluation and adjustment points - Plan for technology evolution - Include contingencies for setbacks
AI Tool Evaluation Framework CRISPE
Capacity: You are an EdTech evaluation specialist helping administrators make informed AI tool adoption decisions.

Role: Technology assessment consultant for educational institutions.

Insight: We're evaluating [AI tool name or category] for potential adoption. I need a structured framework to assess whether this tool is appropriate for our institution.

Statement: Create an evaluation rubric that assesses: 1. **Educational Value** - Does it support learning outcomes? - Does it address a real need? - What's the pedagogical fit? 2. **Data Privacy & Security** - Student data handling - FERPA/COPPA compliance - Data storage and retention - Third-party sharing 3. **Accessibility & Equity** - Works for all students (including disabilities) - Doesn't create access disparities - Language/cultural considerations 4. **Implementation Feasibility** - Training requirements - Integration with existing systems - Support and documentation - Total cost of ownership 5. **Risk Assessment** - What could go wrong? - Vendor stability - Dependency concerns - Exit strategy

Personality: Thorough, skeptical, focused on protecting students and institution.

Experiment: Apply this framework to evaluate [specific tool] if I provide details. [Constraints] - Include scoring criteria (not just yes/no) - Identify deal-breakers vs. nice-to-haves - Consider pilot testing requirements - Include stakeholder input mechanism - Plan for ongoing monitoring post-adoption

Quick Prompt Starters

Copy and customize for common administrative needs.

Email Response Drafter

"Help me draft a professional response to this parent concern about [issue]. The tone should be [empathetic/firm/informative] and I want to [outcome]. Here's the original email: [paste]"

Policy Plain Language Converter

"Rewrite this policy section in plain language that a [parent/student/new teacher] would understand without losing the key requirements: [paste policy text]"

Meeting Agenda Developer

"Help me create an agenda for a [X]-minute meeting about [topic] with [attendees]. Goals: [list]. Include time allocations and discussion questions."

Difficult Conversation Prep

"I need to have a conversation with [person/role] about [sensitive topic]. Help me plan what to say, anticipate their reactions, and find a constructive path forward."

Announcement Drafter

"Draft an announcement for [audience: staff/parents/students] about [topic]. Key messages: [list]. Tone should be [urgent/celebratory/informative]. Keep it to [length]."

Decision Analysis Helper

"I'm trying to decide between [Option A] and [Option B] for [situation]. Help me think through the pros, cons, risks, and stakeholder impacts of each option."

Leadership Principles for AI Adoption

The Administrator's AI Adoption Framework
Lead by Example
Use AI in your own work. Model the behavior you want to see.
Communicate Clearly
Over-communicate your AI strategy. Silence breeds anxiety.
Protect Privacy
Be the guardian of student and staff data. No exceptions.
Stay Adaptive
AI evolves fast. Your policies and practices should too.

The Administrator's Choice

You can spend the next decade fighting AI adoption (and losing), or you can lead your institution through this transformation thoughtfully. The administrators who succeed won't be the ones who resisted longest—they'll be the ones who adapted wisest.
