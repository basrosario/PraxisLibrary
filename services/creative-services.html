Creative & Media Prompts

Industries: Photographers, Designers, Artists Focus: Business of Creativity Goal: Paid for Your Art

Your creative work is irreplaceable—AI can't capture that moment, design that logo, or write that song. But AI CAN help with the business side that most creatives hate: proposals, contracts, marketing, client communication. Let AI handle the admin so you can create.

Built for Creative Professionals

Photographers, videographers, graphic designers, web designers, writers, musicians, artists, content creators, and everyone making a living from creative work.

Client Communication

Professional communication without losing your creative soul.

Project Inquiry Response CRISP
Context: I'm a [photographer/designer/creative type] who received an inquiry about [type of project]. I want to respond professionally, show interest, and move toward booking—without underselling myself.

Request: Write a response to this inquiry that qualifies the lead, shows enthusiasm, and guides toward next steps.

Instructions: Their inquiry: "[Paste the inquiry]" My response should: 1. Thank them and show genuine interest 2. Ask qualifying questions (budget, timeline, scope) 3. Briefly mention my approach/why I'm a good fit 4. Include a clear call to action (schedule a call, share more details) 5. Sign off warmly

Style: Professional but personable—not stuffy or corporate.

Parameters: - My minimum project rate: [If relevant] - My typical timeline: [Booking lead time] - Include: [Link to portfolio, availability, etc.] [Constraints] - Sound like a human, not a template - Show expertise without being arrogant - Don't give away too much before qualifying - Create excitement about working together
Creative Brief Questionnaire COSTAR
Context: You're helping a creative professional develop a client questionnaire that gathers all necessary project information upfront.

Objective: Create a questionnaire for [project type] clients that gets the information needed to deliver great work and avoid scope creep.

Style: Professional but friendly—clients should feel good filling it out, not interrogated.

Tone: Warm, professional, organized.

Audience: Clients who may not know exactly what they want.

Response: Create a questionnaire covering: 1. **Project Overview** - What is this project for? - Who is the target audience? - What problem does this solve? 2. **Goals & Objectives** - What does success look like? - How will this be used? - What action should viewers take? 3. **Creative Direction** - Examples they love (and why) - Examples they hate (and why) - Brand guidelines to follow - Words that describe the feel 4. **Practical Details** - Timeline requirements - Deliverable specifications - Technical requirements - Who approves and how 5. **Budget & Scope** - Budget range - What's flexible vs. fixed - Additional needs anticipated [Project Type] Creative discipline: [Photography, design, video, etc.] Typical project: [Wedding, branding, commercial, etc.] Key information I always need: [Must-haves] Common misunderstandings: [What clients often don't know to tell you] [Constraints] - Keep it manageable length (clients won't fill out 50 questions) - Ask open-ended questions that reveal true preferences - Include examples where helpful - Make it easy to complete (not a chore)

Proposals & Contracts

Get paid what you're worth with professional documents.

Creative Proposal Builder COSTAR
Context: You're helping a [creative professional] write a proposal that communicates value and wins the project at the right price.

Objective: Create a proposal for [project type] that shows you understand their vision and makes them excited to work with you.

Style: Creative but professional—show you're an artist AND a business person.

Tone: Confident, enthusiastic, collaborative.

Audience: [The client and any decision makers]

Response: Create a proposal including: 1. **Introduction** - Personal greeting - Excitement about the project - Summary of what they'll get 2. **Understanding Their Vision** - Reflect back what they want (show you listened) - Your creative interpretation - How you'll bring it to life 3. **The Experience** - What working together looks like - Your creative process - What makes your approach unique 4. **What's Included** - Detailed deliverables - Timeline with milestones - What they need to provide 5. **Investment Options** (if applicable) - Good/Better/Best packages OR - Single pricing with add-ons - What's included in each 6. **Next Steps** - How to book - Deposit and payment terms - What happens after they say yes [Project Details] Project: [Description] Client: [Who they are] Deliverables: [What you'll provide] Pricing: [Your rate/package] Timeline: [Project duration] Your differentiator: [Why you specifically?] [Constraints] - Lead with their vision, not your features - Justify pricing with value, not cost - Make it easy to say yes - Include clear next steps - Show personality—you're a creative!
Scope Creep Response CRISPE
Capacity: You are a business coach helping a creative professional handle scope creep professionally.

Role: Client communication advisor for creative businesses.

Insight: My client is asking for [additional work that wasn't in the original agreement]. I want to maintain the relationship but protect my boundaries and get paid for additional work.

Statement: Help me respond to this scope creep request: 1. **Assess the Situation** - Is this truly outside scope or a gray area? - What's the relationship value? - What would it cost me to do this? 2. **Response Options** - Option A: Push back politely (and how) - Option B: Do it this time with a boundary - Option C: Charge additionally (and how to position) 3. **Draft Response** - Acknowledge their request positively - Clarify what's possible - Present options clearly - Maintain positive relationship 4. **Future Prevention** - How to prevent this with future clients - Contract language to add - Conversation to have upfront

Personality: Professional, firm but kind, solutions-oriented.

Experiment: Give me a "firm" version and a "flexible" version depending on whether I want to keep this client long-term. Portfolio & Marketing

Get discovered by your ideal clients.

Portfolio Case Study Writer COSTAR
Context: You're helping a creative write a portfolio case study that shows not just the work, but the thinking and results behind it.

Objective: Write a case study for [project] that demonstrates my creative process and positions me for similar high-value work.

Style: Visual-narrative format (even in text)—story-driven, not just facts.

Tone: Confident, insightful, showing expertise without bragging.

Audience: Potential clients considering hiring me for similar work.

Response: Write a case study including: 1. **The Challenge** - What the client needed - Why it mattered to them - What made it interesting/challenging 2. **The Approach** - How you thought about the problem - Key creative decisions and why - Collaboration with client 3. **The Solution** - What you created (describe visually) - Key features/elements - How it addressed the challenge 4. **The Results** (if available) - Impact on client's goals - Client feedback - Awards/recognition 5. **Key Learnings** - What made this project successful - Insight that shows expertise [Project Details] Client: [Who was this for?] Project: [What did you create?] Challenge: [What problem did it solve?] Your approach: [How did you tackle it?] Results: [What was the outcome?] Visuals: [What images will accompany this?] [Constraints] - Make the client look good (they might share this) - Show your thinking, not just your doing - Be specific about what YOU contributed - Include searchable keywords for SEO - Keep it scannable with headers

Quick Creative Prompts

Project Kickoff Email

"Write an email to send after [client] books. Include: what happens next, what they need to prepare, timeline, and excitement about working together."

Revision Policy Message

"Write a message explaining my revision policy: [X] rounds included, additional rounds at [Y] cost. Make it clear but not cold."

Final Delivery Email

"Write an email delivering the final [photos/designs/files] to [client]. Include: how to access, usage rights, request for review/testimonial."

Portfolio Caption

"Write a caption for my portfolio/Instagram featuring [this project]. Include the creative story, not just 'New work for [client].'"

Late Payment Follow-up

"Write a friendly but firm follow-up about an invoice that's [X] days overdue. Amount: [Y]. Keep the relationship but get paid."

Collaboration Response

"Write a response to a collaboration request from [brand/person]. I'm [interested/not interested]. Either way, be professional and leave the door open."

Creative Business Principles

AI for the Creative Business
  • Your creativity is the product. AI helps with business; your creative vision can't be replicated.
  • Don't undersell through templates. Customize AI output to match the premium positioning your work deserves.
  • Document your process. Great for marketing, training, and justifying your rates.
  • Set boundaries clearly. AI helps you communicate policies professionally—use it.
  • Get paid, then create. Use AI to make the business side efficient so you have energy for the art.

Create More, Admin Less

You didn't become a creative to write proposals and chase invoices. Use AI to handle the business side professionally so you can spend more time doing the work you actually love—and getting paid well for it.
