Real Estate Agents

Create compelling listings and nurture client relationships.

Listing Description WriterCOSTAR
[Context] You are helping a real estate agent write a compelling property listing that stands out in a competitive market and attracts qualified buyers.
[Objective] Create a listing description for [property address] that highlights unique features, creates emotional appeal, and drives showings.
[Style] Engaging, descriptive, professional—paint a picture without being over-the-top.
[Tone] [Luxury / Family-friendly / Investment-focused / First-time buyer friendly].
[Audience] [Describe target buyer: young professionals, families, investors, retirees].
[Response] Create a listing including: (1) Attention-Grabbing Headline - unique selling point, emotional hook; (2) Opening Hook - lifestyle or feature lead, neighborhood context; (3) Key Features - bedrooms/bathrooms, square footage, lot size, year built, notable upgrades; (4) Lifestyle Benefits - how buyers will live here, neighborhood highlights, nearby amenities; (5) Call to Action - urgency element, showing invitation.
[Property Details] Address: [Full address]. Property type: [Single family, condo, townhouse, etc.]. Beds/Baths: [X bed / X bath]. Square feet: [Size]. Lot size: [If applicable]. Key features: [List standout features]. Recent updates: [Renovations/upgrades]. Neighborhood: [Area highlights]. Price: [List price]. Unique selling points: [What makes this special].
[Constraints] 150-300 words for MLS. Avoid cliches like "must see" or "won't last." Accurate—no exaggeration. Include keywords for search. Fair Housing compliant—no discriminatory language.
Why this works:
Listings compete for attention in seconds. COSTAR's audience-first approach ensures descriptions resonate with target buyers, while the constraint on Fair Housing compliance prevents costly violations. The lifestyle focus transforms features into benefits—buyers don't just see "3 bedrooms" but envision their life in the home.

2026 Compliance: Fair Housing Act prohibits discriminatory language in listings—never describe neighborhoods using language that signals racial, religious, or familial preferences (e.g., avoid "perfect for families," "near churches," or coded language). AI-generated descriptions must be reviewed for Fair Housing compliance before publishing. MLS accuracy requirements mean all stated facts (square footage, year built, features) must be verified. Some states require specific disclosures (lead paint, flood zones, material facts) that should be added separately from marketing copy.
Buyer Communication TemplatesCRISPE
[Capacity] You are a real estate communication specialist helping agents maintain relationships with buyers throughout the home search journey.
[Role] Client communication expert for residential real estate.
[Insight] I need to communicate effectively with buyers at different stages of their home search while staying top of mind and providing value.
[Statement] Create email/text templates for: (1) New Lead Follow-Up - acknowledge inquiry, establish next steps, provide value upfront; (2) After Showing - recap what we saw, gauge interest, suggest next steps; (3) New Listing Alert - personalized property match, why I thought of them, easy viewing scheduling; (4) Market Update - relevant market data, what it means for their search, position as trusted advisor; (5) Re-Engagement - for leads gone quiet, add value, soft ask; (6) Offer Submitted - confirmation, what happens next, timeline expectations; (7) Under Contract Milestones - inspection, appraisal, closing countdown.
[Personality] Professional, knowledgeable, genuinely helpful—not pushy.
[Experiment] Include one template for a buyer who lost out on a competitive offer.
[Agent Details] Your name: [Name]. Brokerage: [Company]. Market area: [Location]. Your specialty: [First-time buyers, luxury, investment, etc.]. Communication style: [Professional, casual, etc.].
[Constraints] Keep texts under 160 characters. Emails scannable with clear CTA. Sound human, not automated. Provide genuine value in each touchpoint.
Why this works:
The home buying journey is emotional and uncertain—timely, thoughtful communication builds trust and keeps you top-of-mind. CRISPE's "lost competitive offer" experiment addresses a critical moment when buyers need support most. The constraint on providing genuine value in each touchpoint prevents spammy outreach that damages relationships.

2026 Compliance: Real estate communications are subject to TCPA for text messages (prior express consent required) and CAN-SPAM for emails (unsubscribe required). Under state licensing laws, all client communications may be subject to brokerage supervision and recordkeeping requirements. NAR's Code of Ethics requires truthful representations—market data shared must be accurate and sourced. Do not include specific financing projections without appropriate disclaimers about rate variability.

Property Managers

Streamline tenant communications and coordinate maintenance efficiently.

Tenant Communication HubCOSTAR
[Context] You are helping a property manager create professional, consistent communications that maintain positive tenant relationships while protecting the property owner's interests.
[Objective] Create a library of tenant communication templates for common situations that save time while maintaining professionalism.
[Style] Professional, clear, friendly but firm when needed.
[Tone] [Formal / Friendly-professional / Direct].
[Audience] Residential tenants in [property type: apartments, single-family, etc.].
[Response] Create templates for: (1) Move-In Communications - welcome letter, move-in checklist, utility setup reminders; (2) Rent & Payments - payment confirmation, late payment reminder (friendly), late payment notice (formal), payment plan offer; (3) Maintenance - request received confirmation, scheduled visit notice, work completed follow-up, emergency maintenance instructions; (4) Lease Management - renewal offer, rent increase notice, lease violation warning, move-out instructions; (5) Community Updates - policy reminders, seasonal maintenance notices, emergency notifications.
[Property Details] Property type: [Apartments, single-family, commercial]. Number of units: [X]. Management company: [Name]. Tenant portal: [Yes/No]. Emergency contact: [Number].
[Constraints] Legal compliance—follow landlord-tenant laws. Clear action items and deadlines. Document everything. Maintain professional boundaries. Include relevant contact information.
Why this works:
Property management is relationship management at scale. COSTAR's structured response ensures comprehensive coverage of common scenarios, while the constraint on legal compliance protects owners and managers from costly mistakes. The "friendly but firm" style maintains positive tenant relationships while ensuring policies are enforced consistently.

2026 Compliance: Landlord-tenant laws vary significantly by state and locality—late payment notices, entry notices, and eviction procedures have specific legal requirements. Some jurisdictions require specific formatting, delivery methods, and waiting periods for notices. Fair Housing Act applies to all tenant communications—no discriminatory treatment or language. Rent increase notices may require 30-90 days advance notice depending on jurisdiction and lease terms. In rent-controlled areas, additional restrictions apply.

The triage system ensures emergencies get priority while routine requests are handled efficiently—protecting both tenant safety and owner budgets.

2026 Compliance: State and local laws define landlord maintenance obligations—habitability requirements (heat, water, structural integrity) are typically non-negotiable. Entry notice requirements (usually 24-48 hours except emergencies) must be followed. Maintain detailed maintenance records for legal defense and insurance claims. In some jurisdictions, failure to address certain issues within specified timeframes allows tenants to withhold rent or repair-and-deduct. Lead paint and mold issues have specific disclosure and remediation requirements.

Real Estate Investors

Analyze markets and evaluate deals with data-driven insights.

Market Analysis FrameworkCOSTAR
[Context] You are helping a real estate investor analyze market conditions to identify investment opportunities and assess risk.
[Objective] Create a comprehensive market analysis framework for [target market/area] to guide investment decisions.
[Style] Data-driven, analytical, actionable.
[Tone] Professional, objective, investment-focused.
[Audience] Real estate investor evaluating [market type: residential, commercial, multifamily].
[Response] Analyze: (1) Market Fundamentals - population growth trends, employment data, major employers, income levels, economic diversification; (2) Supply & Demand - inventory levels, days on market, absorption rates, new construction pipeline, vacancy rates; (3) Price Trends - median prices, price per square foot, appreciation rates, rent growth, cap rate trends; (4) Investment Metrics - typical cash-on-cash returns, rent-to-price ratios, expense ratios, property tax rates; (5) Risk Factors - economic concentration, natural disaster risk, regulatory environment, rent control status; (6) Opportunity Indicators - emerging neighborhoods, infrastructure projects, rezoning potential, value-add opportunities.
[Investment Details] Target market: [City/region]. Investment strategy: [Buy and hold, BRRRR, flip, etc.]. Property type: [SFR, multifamily, commercial]. Investment range: [Budget]. Target returns: [Cash flow, appreciation goals]. Timeline: [Investment horizon].
[Constraints] Use available public data sources. Identify data gaps. Flag assumptions. Compare to alternative markets. Highlight both opportunities and risks.
Why this works:
Market analysis prevents emotional investment decisions. COSTAR's constraint on flagging assumptions forces intellectual honesty about what you know vs. what you're guessing. The comparison to alternative markets prevents tunnel vision on a single opportunity, while the risk factors section addresses the due diligence investors often skip when excited about potential returns.

2026 Compliance: Investment property analysis is for informational purposes—it does not constitute investment advice. AI-generated market projections should be validated against authoritative sources (Census, BLS, local MLS data). If sharing analysis with investment partners or syndicators, SEC regulations may apply to how projections are presented. For markets with rent control (California, Oregon, some cities), ensure analysis accounts for rent increase limitations.
Deal Analysis CalculatorCRISPE
[Capacity] You are a real estate investment analyst helping investors evaluate potential deals with rigorous financial analysis.
[Role] Investment underwriting specialist for residential real estate.
[Insight] I'm evaluating a potential investment property and need to run the numbers to determine if it meets my investment criteria.
[Statement] Analyze this deal: (1) Acquisition Costs - purchase price, closing costs, immediate repairs/renovations, total investment; (2) Income Analysis - gross rent, vacancy allowance, other income, effective gross income; (3) Operating Expenses - property taxes, insurance, maintenance, property management, utilities, reserves; (4) Cash Flow Analysis - NOI, debt service, cash flow, cash-on-cash return; (5) Return Metrics - cap rate, ROI, equity build-up, total return projection; (6) Sensitivity Analysis - best/worst case scenarios, break-even occupancy, rent growth impact; (7) Exit Strategy - hold period returns, sale price projections, refinance potential.
[Personality] Analytical, conservative in estimates, thorough.
[Experiment] Include a "deal killer" checklist—red flags that would make you walk away.
[Property Details] Address: [Location]. Purchase price: [Amount]. Property type: [SFR, duplex, etc.]. Current rent: [Monthly rent]. Market rent: [Comparable rents]. Condition: [Turnkey, value-add, etc.]. Financing: [Down payment %, rate, terms].
[Constraints] Conservative assumptions. Account for all expenses. Compare to market benchmarks. Clear go/no-go recommendation. Identify value-add opportunities.
Why this works:
Investors often underestimate expenses and overestimate returns. CRISPE's conservative personality forces realistic projections, while the "deal killer" checklist surfaces non-financial factors that spreadsheets miss. The sensitivity analysis shows how small changes in assumptions dramatically affect returns—building the discipline that separates successful investors from speculators.

2026 Compliance: Deal analysis for personal investment decisions is unregulated, but if sharing projections with partners or in syndication offerings, SEC regulations apply. AI-generated return projections should be clearly labeled as estimates based on stated assumptions. For properties in rent-controlled jurisdictions, income projections must account for regulatory constraints. Property tax assumptions should be verified against local assessor records, as AI may not have current rates.

Mortgage Brokers

Explain complex rates and educate clients effectively.

Rate Explanation TemplatesCOSTAR
[Context] You are helping a mortgage broker explain complex loan terms and rate options to clients in a way that builds trust and helps them make informed decisions.
[Objective] Create clear explanations of mortgage options that help clients understand their choices without overwhelming them.
[Style] Educational, clear, jargon-free.
[Tone] Helpful, patient, trustworthy advisor.
[Audience] [First-time homebuyers / Move-up buyers / Refinancing homeowners / Investors].
[Response] Create explanations for: (1) Rate Comparison - how to compare loan options, APR vs interest rate, total cost of loan; (2) Fixed vs Adjustable - when each makes sense, risk/reward tradeoff, rate adjustment mechanics; (3) Points & Buydowns - how points work, break-even analysis, temporary buydown options; (4) Loan Types - conventional vs FHA vs VA vs USDA, pros/cons of each, qualification differences; (5) Monthly Payment Breakdown - principal, interest, taxes, insurance, PMI explanation; (6) Rate Lock Strategy - when to lock, float-down options, market timing.
[Client Scenario] Client type: [First-time, refinance, investor]. Loan amount: [Approximate]. Credit profile: [Excellent, good, fair]. Goals: [Lowest payment, lowest total cost, flexibility]. Timeline: [When they need to close].
[Constraints] Comply with TILA/RESPA disclosure requirements. No specific rate promises without proper disclosure. Empower decision-making, don't pressure. Address common misconceptions. Use analogies to clarify complex concepts.
Why this works:
Mortgage confusion leads to poor decisions and lost deals. COSTAR's jargon-free style transforms complex financial concepts into clear comparisons clients can act on. The constraint on TILA/RESPA compliance isn't optional—these federal laws mandate specific disclosures, and AI-generated explanations must never substitute for required documentation.

2026 Compliance: Truth in Lending Act (TILA) and RESPA require specific disclosures for mortgage transactions—AI explanations are educational supplements, not substitutes for Loan Estimates and Closing Disclosures. NMLS licensing requires all rate quotes and loan terms come from licensed mortgage loan originators. Under the CFPB's mortgage servicing rules, any payment or term information must be accurate and verifiable. State-specific requirements (like California's Mortgage Loan Broker Law) may impose additional disclosure obligations.
Client Education ContentCRISPE
[Capacity] You are a mortgage education specialist helping brokers create content that positions them as trusted advisors while generating leads.
[Role] Financial education content creator for mortgage professionals.
[Insight] I want to educate potential clients about the mortgage process to build trust and attract qualified leads to my business.
[Statement] Create educational content: (1) Pre-Approval Guide - what it is, why it matters, how to prepare, documents needed; (2) Credit Score Optimization - how scores affect rates, quick wins for improvement, timeline for changes; (3) Down Payment Options - conventional amounts, low down payment programs, down payment assistance, gift funds; (4) Closing Cost Breakdown - what to expect, negotiable vs fixed costs, seller concessions; (5) Rate Environment Updates - how to interpret market news, Fed decisions impact, when to act; (6) Common Mistakes - pitfalls to avoid during the loan process, what not to do before closing.
[Personality] Educator first, salesperson second.
[Experiment] Create a "mortgage myth busters" piece addressing common misconceptions.
[Business Details] Your name: [Name]. Company: [Lender/Broker]. NMLS#: [Number]. Markets served: [States/areas]. Specialties: [First-time buyers, jumbo, VA, etc.]. Content platform: [Email, social, blog].
[Constraints] Regulatory compliance—no specific rate promises. Educational tone, not sales pitch. Include appropriate disclaimers. Drive action without pressure. Position as trusted resource.
Why this works:
Education builds trust before sales conversations begin. CRISPE's "myth busters" experiment addresses the misinformation that causes clients to make poor decisions or distrust lenders. The "educator first" personality shifts the relationship dynamic—clients who learn from you naturally want to work with you.

2026 Compliance: Educational content is less regulated than rate advertising, but accuracy matters—misinformation can create UDAP (Unfair, Deceptive, Abusive Acts or Practices) liability. Content suggesting specific loan products must include required disclosures per TILA. Social media posts promoting rates require the same disclosures as traditional advertising. NMLS numbers should appear on all marketing materials per state requirements. Under California's SB 1235 (commercial lending disclosure), even educational content should not make implied promises about approval.

[Objective] Create a summary of inspection findings that helps buyers understand the property's condition and make informed decisions.
[Style] Clear, objective, balanced.

[Inspection Details] Property address: [Address]. Property type: [Single family, condo, etc.]. Year built: [Year]. Square footage: [Size]. Key findings: [List major issues discovered]. Systems inspected: [What was covered]. Limitations: [Areas not accessible].
[Constraints] Factual, not speculative. Distinguish between defects and maintenance. Appropriate urgency levels. No cost estimates (liability). Clear, jargon-free language. Photos referenced where helpful.

Quick Real Estate Prompts

Open House Follow-Up

"Write a follow-up email to someone who visited my open house at [address]. They seemed interested in [features]. Ask about their timeline and offer to schedule a private showing."

Price Reduction Announcement

"Help me announce a price reduction on [address] from [old price] to [new price]. Make it sound like an opportunity, not desperation. Include the key selling points."

Neighborhood Guide

"Create a neighborhood guide for [area] that I can share with buyers. Include schools, dining, parks, commute times, and what makes this area special."

Offer Presentation Script

"Help me present my buyer's offer of [amount] on [address]. Seller asking [price]. My buyer's strengths: [pre-approval, flexibility, etc.]. Make a compelling case."

Expired Listing Letter

"Write a letter to the owner of [address] whose listing just expired. Be respectful of their previous agent relationship while showing why I might get different results."

Just Sold Announcement

"Create a 'Just Sold' social media post for [address]. Sold for [price] in [days on market]. Highlight the successful outcome without revealing confidential details."

Real Estate AI Principles

AI for the Real Estate Professional
  • Relationships close deals. AI helps you communicate better; it doesn't replace the trust you build.
  • Local knowledge matters. Use AI for efficiency, but your market expertise is irreplaceable.
  • Accuracy is everything. Always verify AI-generated property details, market data, and legal language.
  • Fair Housing compliance. Review all AI content for discriminatory language—you're responsible.
  • Client confidentiality. Never input sensitive financial or personal client data into AI without safeguards.

Your Market Knowledge + AI Efficiency = More Closings

The best real estate professionals embrace AI to handle writing, analysis, and communication—freeing them to do what matters most: building relationships, negotiating deals, and guiding clients through one of the biggest decisions of their lives.
