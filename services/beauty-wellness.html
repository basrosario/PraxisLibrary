Beauty & Wellness Prompts

Industries: Salons, Barbershops, Spas Focus: Client Relationships Goal: Full Books, Happy Clients

Your craft is in your hands—cutting, styling, healing, transforming. AI can't do that. But it CAN handle the texts, posts, and emails that eat your time between clients. Use these prompts to keep your chair full and your clients coming back.

Built for Beauty Professionals

Salon owners, barbers, estheticians, nail techs, massage therapists, personal trainers, and wellness practitioners—these prompts understand your world.

Client Communication

Stay connected without spending all day texting.

Appointment Message Templates CRISP
Context: I run a [salon/barbershop/spa/studio] and need professional but friendly text/email templates for client communication.

Request: Create a complete set of appointment-related message templates I can quickly customize and send.

Instructions: Create templates for: 1. Appointment confirmation (after booking) 2. Reminder 48 hours before 3. Reminder day-of (morning) 4. Running late notice (from me to client) 5. Client running late response 6. Reschedule request (client wants to change) 7. Cancellation acknowledgment 8. No-show follow-up (gentle) 9. No-show follow-up (if repeated) 10. Rebooking reminder (hasn't been in for a while)

Style: Friendly and professional—like a text from someone they know, not a robot.

Parameters: - Business name: [Your business] - Cancellation policy: [X hours notice required] - Booking link: [If you have online booking] - Keep text messages under 160 characters when possible [Constraints] - Sound warm but professional - Include [BRACKETS] for customization - Have clear call to action where needed - Be firm but kind about policies
Difficult Client Message Helper CRISPE
Capacity: You are a communication coach helping beauty professionals handle tricky client situations with grace.

Role: Customer service advisor for personal service businesses.

Insight: I need to communicate with a client about a sensitive situation. I want to be professional, maintain the relationship if possible, but also stand my ground where needed.

Statement: Help me craft a message for this situation: Situation: [Describe: unhappy with service, repeated no-shows, wants refund, difficult request, crossing boundaries, etc.] Give me: 1. Analysis of the situation (what's really going on) 2. Recommended approach 3. Draft message (text or email, specify) 4. Alternative if first approach doesn't work 5. When to cut ties (if warranted)

Personality: Professional, empathetic, but not a pushover.

Experiment: Give me the message in two versions: one warmer (if I want to keep them), one firmer (if I'm okay losing them). [Situation Details] What happened: [The specific situation] Client history: [New client, regular, VIP, problematic history?] What I want: [Outcome I'm hoping for] What I'm willing to offer: [Discount, redo, nothing?] [Constraints] - Protect my reputation - Be firm where needed but not harsh - Don't over-apologize for things that aren't my fault - Keep it professional (they might screenshot it) - Know when walking away is the right answer

Social Media & Marketing

Show off your work and attract your ideal clients.

Instagram Caption Generator CRISP
Context: I'm a [stylist/barber/esthetician/etc.] posting my work on Instagram. I need captions that showcase my skills and attract new clients.

Request: Write an Instagram caption for my [type of post: before/after, transformation, new style, etc.]

Instructions: Post description: [Describe what's in the photo/video] Service featured: [What did you do?] What makes it special: [Technique, transformation, client story] Call to action: [Book now, DM for info, etc.]

Style: [Match your vibe: Fun and playful / Luxe and sophisticated / Edgy and bold / Warm and welcoming]

Parameters: - Include relevant hashtags (15-25) - Location: [Your city] - Encourage saves/shares - Include booking info [Example hooks to choose from:] - The before doesn't do this justice... - This is what [X weeks/months] of growth looks like... - When your client says "do whatever you want"... - The color correction that took [X] hours but was worth every minute... - New client transformation alert... [Constraints] - Sound like a real person, not a brand - Show expertise without being braggy - Make potential clients want this for themselves - Include call to action naturally
Monthly Content Calendar COSTAR
Context: You're creating a social media content calendar for a [salon/barbershop/spa] owner who has limited time but wants to stay consistent.

Objective: Create one month of content that builds trust, shows expertise, and brings in bookings.

Style: Authentic, industry-appropriate, matches local beauty business vibe.

Tone: [Your brand voice: casual, luxe, edgy, welcoming]

Audience: Your ideal clients in [city/area].

Response: Create a 4-week content calendar: **Content categories to include:** - Transformations/Before & After (2-3x/week) - Educational tips (1-2x/week) - Behind the scenes (1x/week) - Client testimonials/reviews (1x/week) - Personal/team content (1x/week) - Promotional (1x/week max) - Trending content/Reels (2x/week) **For each post:** - Day and optimal posting time - Content type (photo, carousel, Reel, Story) - Caption concept (not full captions) - Hashtag themes - Content needed (what to capture) **Include seasonal relevance for [current month]:** - Relevant holidays/events - Seasonal services to promote - Trending styles for the season [Business Details] Business type: [Salon, barbershop, spa, etc.] Specialty: [Color, cuts, skincare, etc.] Location: [City] Ideal client: [Describe] Services to promote: [Specific services] [Constraints] - Must be doable by one person - Balance promotion with value - Include easy "content batching" days - Account for busy/slow periods - Focus on what drives bookings

Business Operations

Run your business without drowning in paperwork.

Service Menu Designer CRISPE
Capacity: You are a beauty business consultant helping create clear, enticing service menus that drive bookings.

Role: Menu design specialist for beauty and wellness businesses.

Insight: I need to update my service menu. I want it to be clear about what's included, easy for clients to understand, and positioned to encourage higher-ticket services.

Statement: Help me create service descriptions that: 1. **Make services sound appealing** (not just a list) 2. **Clarify what's included** (manage expectations) 3. **Justify premium pricing** (communicate value) 4. **Encourage upgrades** (upsell naturally) 5. **Set appropriate expectations** (time, results) For each service, provide: - Enticing service name (if current name is boring) - Description (2-3 sentences) - What's included - Ideal for (who should book this) - Duration - Starting price or price range

Personality: Luxury-minded but accessible, expertise-forward.

Experiment: Suggest one "signature service" package that bundles popular services at a premium. [My Services] [List your services with current names, what they include, and pricing] [Business Context] Business type: [Salon, spa, barbershop, etc.] Price positioning: [Budget, mid-range, premium, luxury] Ideal client: [Who are you trying to attract?] [Constraints] - Clear enough that clients know what they're getting - Enticing enough that they want to book - Honest (don't oversell) - Consistent with your price positioning
Consultation Question Builder CRISP
Context: I'm a [stylist/esthetician/therapist/etc.] and I want to improve my consultations. Good questions help me deliver better results and avoid miscommunication.

Request: Create a consultation question guide for [service type] that helps me understand what my client really wants.

Instructions: Service: [Specific service: color consultation, facial, massage, etc.] Create questions for: 1. Understanding their goals (what do they want?) 2. Understanding their lifestyle (maintenance, styling habits) 3. Understanding their history (past experiences, problems) 4. Managing expectations (realistic outcomes) 5. Uncovering concerns (what they're worried about) 6. Budget/commitment level (for ongoing services)

Style: Conversational questions I can naturally ask—not a checklist that feels like an interrogation.

Parameters: - Include follow-up prompts for each question - Flag "red flag" answers that need attention - Include how to redirect unrealistic expectations [Constraints] - Sound like a conversation, not an interview - Get real answers, not what they think I want to hear - Help me deliver better results - Protect me from misunderstandings

Beauty Business Principles

AI for the Chair (or Table)
  • Keep your voice. AI writes the draft, you add the personality. Your clients know your vibe—keep it consistent.
  • Showcase real work. Your photos are your portfolio. AI captions make them shine, but the work speaks loudest.
  • Protect client privacy. Never share identifying details without permission, even with AI.
  • Save the templates. Once you create good messages, save them. Don't reinvent every time.
  • Stay human. Personal texts and genuine compliments still matter. AI handles the routine; you handle the special.

Your Talent + AI Efficiency = Growth

You didn't get into this industry to write marketing copy. You got into it to transform people—make them feel beautiful, confident, relaxed, or powerful. AI handles the business side so you can focus on the magic that only you can create.
