Client Communication

Stay connected without spending all day texting.

Appointment Message TemplatesCRISP
[Context] I run a [salon/barbershop/spa/studio] and need professional but friendly text/email templates for client communication.
[Role] Client communications specialist for beauty businesses.
[Instruction] Create appointment message templates for: (1) Booking confirmation, (2) 48-hour reminder, (3) Day-of reminder, (4) Running late notice (me to client), (5) Client running late response, (6) Reschedule request, (7) Cancellation acknowledgment, (8) No-show follow-up (gentle), (9) No-show follow-up (repeated), (10) Rebooking reminder (been a while).
[Style] Friendly and professional—like a text from someone they know, not a robot.
[Parameters] Business name: [Your business]. Cancellation policy: [X hours notice]. Booking link: [If online]. Keep texts under 160 characters when possible.
[Constraints] Sound warm but professional. Include [BRACKETS] for customization. Clear CTAs. Be firm but kind about policies.
Why this works:
No-shows cost beauty professionals an average of $67,000 annually. CRISP's structured approach creates templates that sound personal (not robotic) while being consistent enough to save hours weekly. The 160-character constraint ensures texts display fully on mobile, and the firm-but-kind policy enforcement reduces conflicts while protecting your income.

2026 Compliance: Text message marketing requires prior express consent under TCPA—appointment confirmations to existing clients are generally permitted, but promotional texts require explicit opt-in. California's CCPA gives clients the right to know what personal data you store and request deletion. Keep client phone numbers secure and never share them with third parties without consent. If using automated texting services, ensure they comply with carrier filtering requirements.
Difficult Client Message HelperCRISPE
[Context] You are a communication coach helping beauty professionals handle tricky client situations with grace.
[Role] Customer service advisor for personal service businesses.
[Instruction] I need to communicate about a sensitive situation. Situation: [Describe: unhappy with service, repeated no-shows, wants refund, difficult request, crossing boundaries]. Give me: (1) Analysis of situation, (2) Recommended approach, (3) Draft message (text or email), (4) Alternative if first approach fails, (5) When to cut ties. Provide two versions: warmer (if keeping them) and firmer (if okay losing them).
[Style] Professional, empathetic, but not a pushover.
[Parameters] Keep it professional (they might screenshot it). Don't over-apologize for things that aren't my fault.
[Example] Warmer: "I understand this wasn't what you expected, and I want to make it right..." Firmer: "I appreciate your feedback. Unfortunately, the result is within the scope of what we discussed..."
[Situation Details] What happened: [Specific situation]. Client history: [New/regular/VIP/problematic]. What I want: [Outcome]. What I'll offer: [Discount/redo/nothing].
[Constraints] Protect my reputation. Be firm where needed but not harsh. Know when walking away is right.
Why this works:
Difficult conversations trigger emotional responses that lead to unprofessional messages. CRISPE's two-version approach gives you options depending on whether the relationship is worth saving. The "screenshot test" constraint reminds you that written communications become permanent records—protecting your reputation even when you're frustrated.

2026 Compliance: Written communications can become evidence in disputes or chargebacks, so keep them professional and factual. Never make promises you can't keep or admit fault for things that aren't your responsibility. Under state consumer protection laws, refund and cancellation policies must be clearly disclosed before service; you can enforce only policies clients agreed to. If terminating a client relationship, document the reasons but avoid inflammatory language.

Social Media & Marketing

Show off your work and attract your ideal clients.

Instagram Caption GeneratorCRISP
[Context] I'm a [stylist/barber/esthetician/etc.] posting my work on Instagram. I need captions that showcase my skills and attract new clients.
[Role] Social media caption writer for beauty professionals.
[Instruction] Write an Instagram caption for my [type of post: before/after, transformation, new style]. Post description: [What's in photo/video]. Service featured: [What you did]. What makes it special: [Technique, transformation, client story]. Call to action: [Book now, DM for info].
[Style] [Match your vibe: Fun and playful / Luxe and sophisticated / Edgy and bold / Warm and welcoming].
[Parameters] Include 15-25 relevant hashtags. Location: [Your city]. Encourage saves/shares. Include booking info.
[Example] Hook options: "The before doesn't do this justice..." / "When your client says 'do whatever you want'..." / "Color correction that took [X] hours but was worth every minute..."
[Constraints] Sound like a real person, not a brand. Show expertise without being braggy. Make potential clients want this. Include CTA naturally.
Why this works:
Beauty professionals often post great photos with weak captions—or worse, no caption at all. CRISP's hook examples provide proven opener formulas that stop scrollers, while the constraint on sounding like a real person prevents the corporate-speak that kills engagement. The hashtag guidance ensures discoverability without looking spammy.

2026 Compliance: Always get written permission before posting client photos—verbal consent isn't enough if disputes arise later. Under California's publicity rights laws, using someone's likeness commercially requires consent. If photos show results from specific products, FTC guidelines may require disclosure of any brand relationships. Before/after photos should accurately represent typical results; exaggerated claims can violate state consumer protection laws.
Monthly Content CalendarCOSTAR
[Context] You're creating a social media content calendar for a [salon/barbershop/spa] owner who has limited time but wants to stay consistent.
[Objective] Create one month of content that builds trust, shows expertise, and brings in bookings.
[Style] Authentic, industry-appropriate, matches local beauty business vibe.
[Tone] [Your brand voice: casual, luxe, edgy, welcoming].
[Audience] Your ideal clients in [city/area].
[Response] Create 4-week calendar with content mix: Transformations (2-3x/week), Educational tips (1-2x/week), Behind the scenes (1x/week), Testimonials (1x/week), Personal/team (1x/week), Promotional (1x/week max), Trending/Reels (2x/week). For each: day, time, content type, caption concept, hashtags, what to capture. Include seasonal relevance for [current month]: holidays, seasonal services, trending styles.
[Business Details] Business type: [Salon/barbershop/spa]. Specialty: [Color, cuts, skincare]. Location: [City]. Ideal client: [Describe]. Services to promote: [Specific services].
[Constraints] Doable by one person. Balance promotion with value. Include content batching days. Account for busy/slow periods. Focus on what drives bookings.
Why this works:
Inconsistent posting kills social media growth. COSTAR's one-month structure with batching days makes consistency realistic for busy professionals. The constraint on being "doable by one person" prevents AI from suggesting a content schedule that would require a marketing team. The booking-focused filter ensures every post has business purpose, not just engagement.

2026 Compliance: Testimonials in content must reflect genuine client experiences—FTC guidelines prohibit fake reviews or undisclosed incentivized testimonials. If reposting client content, secure permission first. For licensed services (cosmetology, esthetics, massage), ensure claims about results comply with your state's scope of practice regulations. Music in Reels must be properly licensed; platform-provided music libraries are generally safe for business accounts.

Business Operations

Run your business without drowning in paperwork.

Service Menu DesignerCRISPE
[Context] You are a beauty business consultant helping create clear, enticing service menus that drive bookings.
[Role] Menu design specialist for beauty and wellness businesses.
[Instruction] I need to update my service menu to be clear, enticing, and encourage higher-ticket services. Help me create descriptions that: (1) Make services sound appealing, (2) Clarify what's included, (3) Justify premium pricing, (4) Encourage upgrades naturally, (5) Set expectations. For each service provide: enticing name, 2-3 sentence description, what's included, ideal for, duration, starting price. Also suggest one "signature service" package bundling popular services at a premium.
[Style] Luxury-minded but accessible, expertise-forward.
[Parameters] Make services sound appealing while being honest about what's included.
[Example] Before: "Women's Haircut - $65." After: "Signature Cut & Style - Precision cut tailored to your face shape and lifestyle, includes consultation, shampoo, conditioning treatment, and blowout. Ideal for: Anyone ready for a fresh, polished look. 60 min - Starting at $65."
[My Services] [List services with current names, what they include, and pricing].
[Business Context] Business type: [Salon/spa/barbershop]. Price positioning: [Budget/mid/premium/luxury]. Ideal client: [Who to attract].
[Constraints] Clear enough clients know what they're getting. Enticing enough they want to book. Honest—don't oversell. Consistent with price positioning.
Why this works:
Generic service menus ("Haircut - $45") leave money on the table and create confusion. CRISPE's before/after example shows AI exactly what transformation you want, while the constraint on honesty prevents descriptions that set unrealistic expectations. The signature package suggestion increases average ticket by guiding clients toward premium bundled services.

2026 Compliance: Service descriptions must accurately represent what's included—misleading descriptions can violate state consumer protection laws. "Starting at" pricing is acceptable but actual costs should be disclosed before service begins. For health-related services (skincare, massage), avoid medical claims that exceed your license scope. Display pricing must comply with state regulations; some states require posted prices for standard services.
Consultation Question BuilderCRISP
[Context] I'm a [stylist/esthetician/therapist/etc.] and I want to improve my consultations. Good questions help me deliver better results and avoid miscommunication.
[Role] Consultation specialist for beauty and wellness professionals.
[Instruction] Create a consultation question guide for [service type: color consultation, facial, massage, etc.]. Include questions for: (1) Understanding goals - what they want, (2) Understanding lifestyle - maintenance, styling habits, (3) Understanding history - past experiences, problems, (4) Managing expectations - realistic outcomes, (5) Uncovering concerns - what they're worried about, (6) Budget/commitment level. Include follow-up prompts for each, flag "red flag" answers, include how to redirect unrealistic expectations.
[Style] Conversational questions I can naturally ask—not a checklist that feels like an interrogation.
[Parameters] Make questions feel like natural conversation, not formal interview.
[Constraints] Sound like a conversation, not interview. Get real answers. Help me deliver better results. Protect me from misunderstandings.
Why this works:
Most client complaints stem from mismatched expectations, not poor technique. CRISP's conversational constraint ensures questions feel like genuine interest rather than a checklist, building rapport while gathering critical information. The "red flag" identification helps you spot potential problems before you're holding scissors or applying product.

2026 Compliance: Consultation documentation protects you in disputes—consider having clients sign consultation forms for significant services (color, chemical treatments, extensions). For services with health implications (chemical treatments, certain skincare), document allergies, sensitivities, and contraindications. HIPAA doesn't apply to most beauty services, but if you collect health information, keep it confidential. Some states require specific consultation disclosures before certain procedures.

Beauty Business Principles

AI for the Chair (or Table)
  • Keep your voice. AI writes the draft, you add the personality. Your clients know your vibe—keep it consistent.
  • Showcase real work. Your photos are your portfolio. AI captions make them shine, but the work speaks loudest.
  • Protect client privacy. Never share identifying details without permission, even with AI.
  • Save the templates. Once you create good messages, save them. Don't reinvent every time.
  • Stay human. Personal texts and genuine compliments still matter. AI handles the routine; you handle the special.

Your Talent + AI Efficiency = Growth

You didn't get into this industry to write marketing copy. You got into it to transform people—make them feel beautiful, confident, relaxed, or powerful. AI handles the business side so you can focus on the magic that only you can create.
