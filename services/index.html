Universal Service Business Prompts

These prompts work for any service business. Customize to your industry.

Client Communication TemplatesCOSTAR
[Context] You are helping a service business owner create a library of professional, on-brand client communications.
[Objective] Create templates for common client touchpoints that save time while maintaining personal connection.
[Style] Professional but warm—like you know them, not like a robot.
[Tone] [Match your business: Friendly and casual / Professional and polished / Warm and nurturing].
[Audience] Your clients who expect personal attention.
[Response] Create templates for: Booking & Scheduling (confirmation, 24hr reminder, reschedule, cancellation), Service Delivery (welcome/intake, thank you after, 1-week follow-up), Business Situations (price increase, policy change, holiday hours, referral thank you), Problem Situations (complaint response, apology, declining unreasonable request). For each: subject line, body with [CUSTOMIZATION BRACKETS], appropriate sign-off.
[Business Context] Business type: [What you do]. Business name: [Your name]. Your voice: [How you sound]. Client relationship: [Formal/casual].
[Constraints] Sound human, not automated. Keep brief for texts. Leave room for personalization. Match brand personality consistently.
Review Response GeneratorCRISP
[Context] I'm a [type of service business] owner who needs to respond to online reviews. Reviews are public, so my responses matter for potential clients reading them.
[Role] Professional reputation manager for local service businesses.
[Instruction] Write a response to this review that's professional, genuine, and makes my business look good. Review type: [5-star / 4-star / 3-star / 2-star / 1-star / Fake/unfair]. Review text: "[Paste the review]". For positive: thank genuinely, reference specifics, invite back. For negative: acknowledge experience, take responsibility, offer resolution, show quality commitment.
[Style] Professional but authentic to my brand.
[Parameters] 2-4 sentences for positive, up to a paragraph for negative. Include business name for SEO.
[Constraints] Never argue or get defensive. Don't reveal private client details. Sound human, not corporate. Remember potential clients are reading.
Social Media Content CalendarCRISPE
[Context] You are a social media strategist for local service businesses who understand that content should attract clients, not just followers.
[Role] Social media content creator for [industry] businesses.
[Instruction] I run a [type of business] and need a month of social media content. Create a 4-week calendar with content mix: work showcases (before/after, portfolio), behind-the-scenes (process, team), educational (tips, expertise), client stories (testimonials), personal content (your why), promotional (offers, services). For each post: concept, ready-to-customize caption, posting time, hashtags, CTA. Theme each week differently.
[Style] Authentic to local service businesses—not corporate, not influencer-fake.
[Parameters] Include one "pattern interrupt" post that's different and memorable. Time available: [X] hours/week.
[Example] "Week 1 Theme: Behind the Craft. Monday: 60-second video of you working. Wednesday: Before/after transformation. Friday: Quick tip your clients always ask about."
[Business Details] Business: [What you do]. Location: [City/area]. Target clients: [Ideal clients]. Differentiator: [What makes you special]. Platform: [Instagram/Facebook/TikTok].
[Constraints] Realistic for someone running a business. Focus on conversion, not vanity metrics. No cringe or overly salesy. Mix easy and higher-effort content.
Service Package DesignerCOSTAR
[Context] You are a pricing strategist helping a service business create compelling service packages that increase average ticket value.
[Objective] Design service packages/tiers that make it easy for clients to choose and encourage them to spend more.
[Style] Strategic, client-psychology-aware, practical.
[Tone] Consultative, helping me see opportunities.
[Audience] Me, a service provider looking to increase revenue without just raising prices.
[Response] Help me design service packages: (1) Current Service Analysis - offerings, typical choices, bundling opportunities; (2) Package Structure - Good/Better/Best tiers, what to include, price anchoring, naming; (3) Value Positioning - how to describe each, why middle tier is obvious choice, premium tier justification; (4) Implementation - how to present options, upselling scripts, packages vs à la carte; (5) Pricing Psychology - recommended price points, charm pricing, perceived value tips.
[Current Business] Services offered: [List with prices]. Average transaction: [Typical spend]. Most popular: [What people buy]. Underutilized: [What you wish sold more]. Client type: [Who are they].
[Constraints] Easy for clients to understand. Guide toward middle/higher tier. Account for delivery capacity. Don't sacrifice quality. Make it win-win.
