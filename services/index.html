Universal Service Business Prompts

These prompts work for any service business. Customize to your industry.

Client Communication Templates COSTAR
Context: You are helping a service business owner create a library of professional, on-brand client communications.

Objective: Create templates for common client touchpoints that save time while maintaining personal connection.

Style: Professional but warm—like you know them, not like a robot.

Tone: [Match your business: Friendly and casual / Professional and polished / Warm and nurturing]

Audience: Your clients who expect personal attention.

Response: Create templates for: **Booking & Scheduling** - Appointment confirmation - Appointment reminder (24 hours) - Reschedule request from client - Cancellation acknowledgment **Service Delivery** - Welcome/intake message - Thank you after service - Follow-up check-in (1 week after) **Business Situations** - Price increase announcement - Policy change notification - Holiday hours notice - Referral thank you **Problem Situations** - Response to complaint - Apology for delay/mistake - Declining unreasonable request (professionally) **For each template include:** - Subject line (if email/text) - Body text with [CUSTOMIZATION BRACKETS] - Appropriate sign-off [Business Context] Business type: [What you do] Business name: [Your business name] Your voice: [How you want to sound] Client relationship: [Formal, casual, etc.] [Constraints] - Sound human, not automated - Keep brief for text messages - Leave room for personalization - Match brand personality consistently
Review Response Generator CRISP
Context: I'm a [type of service business] owner who needs to respond to online reviews. Reviews are public, so my responses matter for potential clients reading them.

Request: Write a response to this review that's professional, genuine, and makes my business look good to people reading it.

Instructions: Review type: [5-star / 4-star / 3-star / 2-star / 1-star / Fake/unfair] Review text: "[Paste the review]" For positive reviews: - Thank them genuinely (not generically) - Reference something specific they mentioned - Invite them back For negative reviews: - Acknowledge their experience (don't get defensive) - Take responsibility where appropriate - Offer to make it right (if warranted) - Show future readers you care about quality

Style: Professional but authentic to my brand.

Parameters: - Keep to 2-4 sentences for positive reviews - Up to a paragraph for negative reviews - Include business name mention for SEO [Constraints] - Never argue or get defensive - Don't reveal private client details - Sound like a human, not corporate - Remember potential clients are reading this
Social Media Content Calendar CRISPE
Capacity: You are a social media strategist for local service businesses who understand that content should attract clients, not just followers.

Role: Social media content creator for [industry] businesses.

Insight: I run a [type of business] and need a month of social media content that showcases my work, builds trust, and brings in clients. I can spend [X] hours per week on social media.

Statement: Create a 4-week content calendar including: **Content Mix:** - Work showcases (before/after, portfolio, projects) - Behind-the-scenes (process, workspace, team) - Educational content (tips, how-tos, expertise) - Client stories (testimonials, transformations) - Personal/humanizing content (about you, your why) - Promotional (offers, availability, services) **For each post:** - Post concept/description - Caption (ready to customize) - Best time to post - Hashtag suggestions - Call to action **Week structure:** - Week 1: [Theme/focus] - Week 2: [Theme/focus] - Week 3: [Theme/focus] - Week 4: [Theme/focus]

Personality: Authentic to local service businesses—not corporate, not influencer-fake.

Experiment: Include one "pattern interrupt" post that's different and memorable. [Business Details] Business: [What you do] Location: [City/area] Target clients: [Who are your ideal clients?] Differentiator: [What makes you special?] Platform focus: [Instagram, Facebook, TikTok, etc.] [Constraints] - Realistic for someone running a business - Focus on conversion, not vanity metrics - No cringe or overly salesy posts - Mix of easy-to-create and higher-effort content
Service Package Designer COSTAR
Context: You are a pricing strategist helping a service business create compelling service packages that increase average ticket value.

Objective: Design service packages/tiers that make it easy for clients to choose and encourage them to spend more.

Style: Strategic, client-psychology-aware, practical.

Tone: Consultative, helping me see opportunities.

Audience: Me, a service provider looking to increase revenue without just raising prices.

Response: Help me design service packages: 1. **Current Service Analysis** - What you're offering now - What clients typically choose - Opportunities for bundling 2. **Package Structure Recommendation** - Good/Better/Best tiers (or alternative structure) - What to include in each tier - Price anchoring strategy - How to name the packages 3. **Value Positioning** - How to describe each package - Why the middle tier should be the obvious choice - What makes the premium tier worth it 4. **Implementation** - How to present options to clients - Scripts for upselling - When to offer packages vs. à la carte 5. **Pricing Psychology** - Recommended price points - Charm pricing considerations - Perceived value tips [Current Business] Services offered: [List your services and current prices] Average transaction: [What clients typically spend] Most popular service: [What most people buy] Underutilized services: [What you wish people bought more] Client type: [Who are your clients?] [Constraints] - Must be easy for clients to understand - Should naturally guide toward middle/higher tier - Account for my actual delivery capacity - Don't sacrifice quality for revenue - Make it win-win (clients get value, I get revenue)
