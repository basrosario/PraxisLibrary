Food & Hospitality Prompts

Industries: Restaurants, Caterers, Events Focus: Guest Experience Goal: Full Tables, Happy Guests

Hospitality is about creating experiences—the perfect meal, the memorable event, the welcoming stay. AI can't cook your signature dish or greet your guests with genuine warmth. But it CAN help with menus, marketing, reservations, reviews, and all the behind-the-scenes work that competes for your attention.

Built for Hospitality Professionals

Restaurant owners, chefs, food trucks, caterers, event planners, hotels, B&Bs, cafes, bars, and everyone in the business of making people feel welcome.

Menu & Marketing Content

Make mouths water before they even arrive.

Menu Description Writer COSTAR
Context: You're helping a [restaurant/cafe/caterer] write menu descriptions that make dishes irresistible and match their brand.

Objective: Write compelling menu descriptions that make customers want to order and justify premium pricing.

Style: Match the restaurant's vibe: [fine dining / casual / rustic / modern / ethnic]

Tone: Appetizing, evocative, on-brand.

Audience: Guests deciding what to order.

Response: Write menu descriptions for these dishes: For each dish, provide: 1. **Dish name** (if it needs improving) 2. **Description** (2-3 lines max) 3. **Key selling points** (ingredients, technique, origin) 4. **Dietary/allergen flags** (where relevant) Guidelines: - Lead with the star ingredient or technique - Use sensory words (crispy, silky, tangy, etc.) - Tell a mini-story when appropriate - Justify premium ingredients - Make vegetarian/healthy options sound indulgent [Dishes to Describe] [List your dishes with ingredients and preparation] [Restaurant Context] Restaurant type: [Style/cuisine] Price point: [Casual, upscale, etc.] Brand voice: [How you want to sound] Target customer: [Who dines with you] [Constraints] - Keep descriptions scannable (people don't read long) - Avoid clichés ("nestled on a bed of...") - Be accurate (don't oversell what you deliver) - Consider dietary restrictions disclosure - Make every dish sound worth ordering
Social Media Food Content CRISP
Context: I run a [restaurant/food truck/cafe/catering business] and need to post engaging content that brings people in.

Request: Create a week of social media content that showcases our food, personality, and gives people reasons to visit.

Instructions: For each day, provide: - Post type (photo idea, Reel concept, Story series) - Caption (ready to use) - Hashtags - Best time to post - Call to action Content categories: - Food porn (make them hungry) - Behind the scenes (kitchen, prep, team) - Specials/promotions - Customer moments (with permission) - Story/heritage (your why) - Engagement (polls, questions) - User-generated content prompts

Style: [Match your vibe: Fun, sophisticated, casual, authentic]

Parameters: - Platform: [Instagram, TikTok, Facebook] - Posting frequency: [How often] - Key things to promote: [Specials, events, etc.] [Business Details] Business: [Restaurant name and type] Location: [City] Specialty: [What you're known for] Vibe: [Atmosphere description] Current promotions: [Any specials?] [Constraints] - Must make people hungry - Show personality, not just food - Include clear CTAs (visit, order, reserve) - Balance promo with value - Feasible for someone running a kitchen

Guest Communication

Handle reservations, reviews, and requests professionally.

Request: Create response templates for guest communications.

Instructions: Create templates for: **Reservations:** 1. Confirmation 2. Reminder (day before) 3. Waitlist notification (table opened) 4. Can't accommodate request (fully booked) 5. Large party inquiry 6. Special occasion request (birthday, anniversary) **General Inquiries:** 7. Private event inquiry 8. Dietary restriction question 9. Menu question 10. Hours/location **Issues:** 11. Response to complaint 12. Cancellation policy explanation 13. No-show follow-up For each template: - Subject line (if email) - Body text with [CUSTOMIZATION] - Warm sign-off

Style: Warm, professional, matches hospitality standards.

Parameters: - Business name: [Your venue] - Reservation policy: [Deposit, cancellation, etc.] - Include links to: [Menu, booking, directions] [Constraints] - Sound genuinely welcoming - Clear on policies but not cold - Make guests feel valued - Easy for staff to customize quickly
Review Response Generator CRISPE
Capacity: You are a hospitality communications expert helping respond to online reviews professionally.

Role: Guest relations manager for food & beverage businesses.

Insight: Reviews make or break restaurants. I need to respond in a way that thanks happy guests, addresses concerns from unhappy ones, and shows potential guests we care about experience.

Statement: Write a response to this review: Review: "[Paste the review]" Star rating: [X stars] What actually happened (my side): [Context if negative] For positive reviews: - Genuine thanks (reference specifics they mentioned) - Mention what they enjoyed - Invite them back - Keep it warm but brief For negative reviews: - Acknowledge their experience - Apologize where appropriate (without admitting fault unnecessarily) - Explain if there were circumstances (without making excuses) - Offer to make it right - Invite them to contact us directly For fake/unfair reviews: - Stay professional - State facts calmly - Don't argue - Let readers draw their own conclusions

Personality: Gracious host, even when criticized.

Experiment: For negative reviews, show me two versions: one apologetic (if we messed up) and one diplomatic (if we didn't). [Constraints] - Future guests are reading this - Never argue or get defensive - Show we care about every guest - Keep it proportional (don't over-respond) - Include business name for SEO

Events & Catering

Win the big bookings with professional proposals.

Catering Proposal Builder COSTAR
Context: You're helping a caterer/restaurant create a professional proposal for a [type of event].

Objective: Create a compelling catering proposal that wins the booking and sets clear expectations.

Style: Professional, appetizing, clear on logistics.

Tone: Warm, confident, focused on making their event special.

Audience: Event planner, host, or decision maker.

Response: Create a proposal including: 1. **Introduction** - Thank them for considering us - Show we understand their event - Why we're a great fit 2. **Menu Proposal** - Recommended menu items with descriptions - Options/alternatives - Dietary accommodations 3. **Service Details** - Service style (plated, buffet, stations, passed) - Staff included - Timeline of service - Setup/breakdown 4. **What's Included** - Food & beverage - Equipment - Staff - Rentals (if any) 5. **Investment** - Per-person pricing breakdown - Total estimate - What's not included - Deposit and payment terms 6. **Next Steps** - How to book - Timeline to confirm - Tasting offer (if applicable) [Event Details] Event type: [Wedding, corporate, party, etc.] Guest count: [Number] Date: [When] Location: [Where] Budget indication: [If known] Menu preferences: [Any requests?] Special requirements: [Dietary, cultural, etc.] [Constraints] - Make the food sound irresistible - Be clear on what's included vs. additional - Set realistic expectations - Professional presentation - Make it easy to say yes

Quick Hospitality Prompts

Daily Special Announcement

"Write a social post announcing today's special: [dish]. Make it sound irresistible. Include: what it is, why it's special, and that it's limited."

Holiday Hours Post

"Write an announcement about our holiday hours: [schedule]. Include a festive message, reservation encouragement, and our appreciation for guests."

New Menu Item Description

"Write a menu description and social post for our new [dish]. Ingredients: [list]. Inspiration: [story]. Make people need to try it."

Staff Spotlight Post

"Write a post introducing/celebrating [team member]. Role: [position]. Fun fact: [something personal]. Show the humans behind the food."

Busy Night Apology

"Write a message apologizing for [long wait/running out of dish/service issue] last night. Acknowledge the problem, take responsibility, invite them back."

Event Recap Post

"Write a post thanking [client] for letting us cater their [event]. Highlight what we served, the vibe, and encourage inquiries for similar events."

Hospitality Business Principles

AI for the Host
  • Your food is the star. AI helps with words; your cooking creates the experience.
  • Reviews need responses. Every review deserves acknowledgment. AI makes it manageable.
  • Consistency builds trust. Templates ensure professional communication even during rush.
  • Photos still matter most. The best caption can't save a bad food photo. Invest in visuals.
  • Personal touch wins. Use AI for the routine so you have energy for the personal moments that create loyalty.

Hospitality is Human. AI Supports the Humans.

No AI can replicate the warmth of a genuine greeting, the artistry of a well-plated dish, or the care of remembering a regular's favorite table. AI handles the marketing, the responses, the admin—so you can focus on creating the experiences that turn first-time visitors into lifelong guests.
