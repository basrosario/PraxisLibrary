Thought Leadership & Content

Establish yourself as the expert clients trust.

LinkedIn Article WriterCOSTAR
[Context] You are helping a [type of professional] write thought leadership content that establishes expertise and attracts ideal clients.
[Objective] Write a LinkedIn article that demonstrates deep expertise on [topic] while being accessible and actionable for readers.
[Style] Expert but accessible—like a smart friend explaining something complex.
[Tone] [Authoritative / Conversational / Thought-provoking / Practical].
[Audience] [Describe your ideal clients/readers].
[Response] Write an article including: (1) Hook - grab attention with insight or contrarian view, promise value; (2) Problem/Context - why this matters now, what most get wrong; (3) Your Perspective/Framework - unique POV, experience insights, practical approach; (4) Evidence/Examples - real examples anonymized, supporting data/research; (5) Actionable Takeaways - what readers can do differently, clear next steps; (6) Close - memorable final thought, soft CTA (not salesy).
[Article Details] Topic: [What to write about]. Your angle: [Unique perspective]. Key points: [Main messages]. Expertise evidence: [What qualifies you]. Target reader: [Who should read this].
[Constraints] 800-1200 words. Sound human, not AI-generated. Specific insights, not generic advice. Shareable and bookmark-worthy. Avoid being self-promotional.
Podcast/Interview PrepCRISPE
[Capacity] You are a media coach preparing a professional for a podcast or interview appearance.
[Role] Interview preparation specialist for thought leaders.
[Insight] I'm appearing on [podcast name/interview type] to discuss [topic]. I want to come across as an expert, be memorable, and drive business from this appearance.
[Statement] Help me prepare: (1) Key Messages (3-5) - what I must communicate, memorable soundbites for each; (2) Anticipated Questions - 10 likely questions with suggested answers including stories/examples; (3) Bridging Techniques - how to redirect to key messages, pivot from off-topic questions; (4) Stories to Tell - expertise anecdotes, anonymized client successes, personal connection stories; (5) Call to Action - direct listeners to business, free resource to offer, mention without being salesy; (6) Potential Pitfalls - sensitive topics to handle carefully, common mistakes to avoid.
[Personality] Strategic, media-savvy, focused on impact.
[Experiment] Give me one "signature" statement that could become associated with me.
[Interview Context] Show/Publication: [Where appearing]. Audience: [Who listens/reads]. Topic focus: [What we'll discuss]. My expertise: [What qualifies me]. Business goal: [Desired outcome].
[Constraints] Sound natural, not rehearsed. Be quotable. Clear audience value. Avoid self-promotional while building brand.

Client Acquisition & Proposals

Win the clients you want at the rates you deserve.

Proposal WriterCOSTAR
[Context] You are helping a [type of professional] write a compelling proposal that wins business at premium rates.
[Objective] Create a proposal for [engagement type] that demonstrates understanding, builds confidence, and makes it easy to say yes.
[Style] Professional, confident, client-focused—not template-y or generic.
[Tone] Consultative, like a trusted advisor presenting a recommendation.
[Audience] [Decision maker at prospective client].
[Response] Create a proposal including: (1) Executive Summary - their situation/challenge, recommended approach, expected outcomes, investment overview; (2) Understanding Your Needs - demonstrate we listened, reframe challenge with insight, what success looks like; (3) Our Approach - methodology/framework, phased milestones, deliverables, what we need from them; (4) About Us - qualifications (brief), relevant experience, team involved; (5) Investment - pricing with options, what's included, payment terms, value justification; (6) Next Steps - clear path to start, timeline, contact info.
[Engagement Details] Client: [Who is this for]. Challenge: [Problem we're solving]. Scope: [Work to be done]. Pricing: [Fee structure]. Timeline: [Duration]. Our differentiator: [Why us].
[Constraints] Focus on outcomes, not activities. Justify premium pricing with value. Make it easy to say yes. Keep concise (executives skim). Clear next steps.
Discovery Call FrameworkCRISPE
[Capacity] You are a sales coach helping a professional structure discovery calls that qualify prospects and close deals.
[Role] Business development advisor for professional services.
[Insight] I have a discovery call with a potential [type of client]. I want to understand their needs, demonstrate my expertise, and move toward a close—without being salesy.
[Statement] Create a discovery call framework: (1) Opening (2-3 min) - rapport building, agenda setting, permission to ask questions; (2) Discovery Questions (15-20 min) - understand situation, uncover real problems/goals, decision criteria, urgency/timeline, budget qualification; (3) Insight Delivery (5-10 min) - share relevant perspective, demonstrate expertise, plant seeds for solution; (4) Solution Overview (5 min) - how you could help conceptually, relevant experience, engagement overview; (5) Close/Next Steps (5 min) - trial close questions, handle initial objections, clear next step.
[Personality] Consultative, curious, confident—not pushy.
[Experiment] Include "red flag" signals that suggest this isn't a good fit.
[Call Context] Service: [What I offer]. Ideal client: [Who I serve best]. Average engagement: [Typical size/scope]. Call duration: [How long]. My sales style: [Consultative, direct, etc.].
[Constraints] Talk less than 50% of the call. Qualify as much as pitch. Be willing to disqualify. Create urgency without pressure. Clear, agreed-upon next step.

Client Delivery & Reporting

Deliver premium experiences that justify premium rates.

Client Report TemplateCOSTAR
[Context] You are helping a [consultant/advisor] create professional client reports that communicate value and insights clearly.
[Objective] Create a [report type: progress report, analysis, recommendations, quarterly review] for [client type].
[Style] Executive-ready—clear, visual-friendly, action-oriented.
[Tone] Professional, confident, focused on outcomes.
[Audience] [Client stakeholders who will read this].
[Response] Structure a report including: (1) Executive Summary - key findings/progress, what it means, recommended actions; (2) Progress/Findings Overview - what we did, what we found/achieved, visual-friendly presentation; (3) Analysis/Insights - key insights from work, what data tells us, implications; (4) Recommendations - clear prioritized recommendations, rationale for each, expected impact; (5) Next Steps - agreed actions, timeline, accountabilities.
[Report Details] Report type: [Progress, analysis, recommendation, etc.]. Time period: [What period this covers]. Key findings: [Main things to communicate]. Recommendations: [What you're recommending]. Data/evidence: [What supports conclusions].
[Constraints] Executives skim—make it scannable. Lead with conclusions, not methodology. Tie everything to business outcomes. Recommendations specific and actionable. Demonstrate value delivered.

Quick Professional Prompts

Follow-Up Email

"Write a follow-up email after my [meeting/call] with [prospect/client]. Key points discussed: [X]. Next steps: [Y]. Keep it professional and move things forward."

Scope Creep Response

"Help me respond to a client who's asking for [work outside our agreement]. I want to be helpful but protect my boundaries. Options: charge more, push back politely, etc."

Testimonial Request

"Write a message asking [happy client] for a testimonial. Make it easy for them by including specific questions or prompts they can respond to."

Rate Increase Script

"Help me communicate a rate increase from [X] to [Y] to my existing clients. I want to maintain relationships while valuing my worth."

Bio/Intro Variations

"Write 3 versions of my professional bio: 1) Formal for publications/conferences, 2) Conversational for podcasts/interviews, 3) Brief for social media."

Meeting Recap

"Write a professional recap email for my meeting with [client]. Covered: [topics]. Decisions: [X]. Action items: [Y]. Next meeting: [Z]."

Professional Service Principles

AI for the Expert
  • Your judgment is the product. AI helps communicate your expertise; it doesn't replace it.
  • Quality over speed. Use AI to work faster, but don't sacrifice the thinking that clients pay for.
  • Confidentiality matters. Never put client-specific information into AI without proper safeguards.
  • Maintain your voice. Your perspective is your brand. AI drafts; you edit for authenticity.
  • Document your methodology. AI can help you systematize your approach—that's leverage.

Your Expertise + AI Leverage = Premium Practice

The best professionals aren't threatened by AI—they use it to amplify their reach and impact. You bring the expertise, judgment, and relationships. AI handles the writing, research, and administrative tasks that used to eat your billable hours.
