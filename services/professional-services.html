Professional Services Prompts

Industries: Consultants, Coaches, Advisors Focus: Expertise & Trust Goal: Premium Positioning

Your value is in your expertise, judgment, and the trust clients place in you. AI can help you communicate that expertise, scale your thought leadership, and handle the administrative work that keeps you from serving clients. These prompts help consultants, coaches, lawyers, accountants, and advisors work smarter.

Built for Knowledge Workers

Business consultants, executive coaches, financial advisors, lawyers, accountants, therapists, real estate agents, and other professionals who sell expertise and outcomes.

Thought Leadership & Content

Establish yourself as the expert clients trust.

LinkedIn Article Writer COSTAR
Context: You are helping a [type of professional] write thought leadership content that establishes expertise and attracts ideal clients.

Objective: Write a LinkedIn article that demonstrates deep expertise on [topic] while being accessible and actionable for readers.

Style: Expert but accessible—like a smart friend explaining something complex.

Tone: [Authoritative / Conversational / Thought-provoking / Practical]

Audience: [Describe your ideal clients/readers]

Response: Write an article including: 1. **Hook** (first paragraph) - Grab attention with insight or contrarian view - Promise value for reading 2. **The Problem/Context** - Why this topic matters now - What most people get wrong 3. **Your Perspective/Framework** - Your unique point of view - Key insights from your experience - Practical framework or approach 4. **Evidence/Examples** - Real examples (anonymized if needed) - Data or research that supports your view 5. **Actionable Takeaways** - What readers can do differently - Clear next steps 6. **Close** - Memorable final thought - Soft CTA (not salesy) [Article Details] Topic: [What you want to write about] Your angle: [Your unique perspective] Key points: [Main things you want to convey] Expertise evidence: [Experience that qualifies you] Target reader: [Who should read this?] [Constraints] - Length: 800-1200 words - Sound like a human expert, not AI-generated - Include specific insights, not generic advice - Make it shareable and bookmark-worthy - Avoid being self-promotional
Podcast/Interview Prep CRISPE
Capacity: You are a media coach preparing a professional for a podcast or interview appearance.

Role: Interview preparation specialist for thought leaders.

Insight: I'm appearing on [podcast name/interview type] to discuss [topic]. I want to come across as an expert, be memorable, and drive business from this appearance.

Statement: Help me prepare: 1. **Key Messages** (3-5 points) - What must I communicate? - Memorable soundbites for each 2. **Anticipated Questions** - 10 likely questions - Suggested answers with stories/examples 3. **Bridging Techniques** - How to redirect to my key messages - Ways to pivot from off-topic questions 4. **Stories to Tell** - Relevant anecdotes that illustrate my expertise - Client success stories (anonymized) - Personal stories that build connection 5. **Call to Action** - How to direct listeners to my business - Free resource or next step to offer - How to mention without being salesy 6. **Potential Pitfalls** - Sensitive topics to handle carefully - Common mistakes to avoid

Personality: Strategic, media-savvy, focused on impact.

Experiment: Give me one "signature" statement that could become associated with me. [Interview Context] Show/Publication: [Where is this appearing?] Audience: [Who listens/reads?] Topic focus: [What will we discuss?] My expertise: [What qualifies me?] Business goal: [What outcome do I want?] [Constraints] - Sound natural, not rehearsed - Be quotable - Have clear value for the audience - Avoid being self-promotional while still building brand

Client Acquisition & Proposals

Win the clients you want at the rates you deserve.

Proposal Writer COSTAR
Context: You are helping a [type of professional] write a compelling proposal that wins business at premium rates.

Objective: Create a proposal for [engagement type] that demonstrates understanding, builds confidence, and makes it easy to say yes.

Style: Professional, confident, client-focused—not template-y or generic.

Tone: Consultative, like a trusted advisor presenting a recommendation.

Audience: [Decision maker at prospective client]

Response: Create a proposal including: 1. **Executive Summary** - Their situation and challenge - Our recommended approach - Expected outcomes - Investment overview 2. **Understanding Your Needs** - Demonstrate we listened - Reframe their challenge (show insight) - What success looks like 3. **Our Approach** - Methodology/framework - Phased approach with milestones - What we'll deliver - What we'll need from them 4. **About Us** - Why we're qualified (briefly) - Relevant experience - Team involved 5. **Investment** - Pricing (with options if applicable) - What's included - Payment terms - Value justification 6. **Next Steps** - Clear path to start - Timeline - Contact information [Engagement Details] Client: [Who is this for?] Challenge: [What problem are we solving?] Scope: [What work will be done?] Pricing: [Fee structure] Timeline: [Duration] Our differentiator: [Why us?] [Constraints] - Focus on outcomes, not activities - Justify premium pricing with value - Make it easy to say yes - Keep it concise (executives don't read long proposals) - Include clear next steps
Discovery Call Framework CRISPE
Capacity: You are a sales coach helping a professional structure discovery calls that qualify prospects and close deals.

Role: Business development advisor for professional services.

Insight: I have a discovery call with a potential [type of client]. I want to understand their needs, demonstrate my expertise, and move toward a close—without being salesy.

Statement: Create a discovery call framework: 1. **Opening** (2-3 minutes) - Rapport building - Agenda setting - Permission to ask questions 2. **Discovery Questions** (15-20 minutes) - Understand their situation - Uncover real problems/goals - Identify decision criteria - Understand urgency/timeline - Budget qualification 3. **Insight Delivery** (5-10 minutes) - Share relevant perspective - Demonstrate expertise - Plant seeds for your solution 4. **Solution Overview** (5 minutes) - How you could help (conceptual) - Relevant experience - What engagement might look like 5. **Close/Next Steps** (5 minutes) - Trial close questions - Handle initial objections - Clear next step

Personality: Consultative, curious, confident—not pushy.

Experiment: Include "red flag" signals that suggest this isn't a good fit. [Call Context] Service: [What I offer] Ideal client: [Who I serve best] Average engagement: [Typical size/scope] Call duration: [How long?] My sales style: [Consultative, direct, etc.] [Constraints] - Talk less than 50% of the call - Qualify as much as pitch - Be willing to disqualify - Create urgency without pressure - Have clear, agreed-upon next step

Client Delivery & Reporting

Deliver premium experiences that justify premium rates.

Client Report Template COSTAR
Context: You are helping a [consultant/advisor] create professional client reports that communicate value and insights clearly.

Objective: Create a [report type: progress report, analysis, recommendations, quarterly review] for [client type].

Style: Executive-ready—clear, visual-friendly, action-oriented.

Tone: Professional, confident, focused on outcomes.

Audience: [Client stakeholders who will read this]

Response: Structure a report including: 1. **Executive Summary** - Key findings/progress - What it means - Recommended actions 2. **Progress/Findings Overview** - What we did - What we found/achieved - Visual-friendly presentation 3. **Analysis/Insights** - Key insights from the work - What the data tells us - Implications 4. **Recommendations** - Clear, prioritized recommendations - Rationale for each - Expected impact 5. **Next Steps** - Agreed actions - Timeline - Accountabilities [Report Details] Report type: [Progress, analysis, recommendation, etc.] Time period: [What period does this cover?] Key findings: [Main things to communicate] Recommendations: [What are you recommending?] Data/evidence: [What supports your conclusions?] [Constraints] - Executives skim—make it scannable - Lead with conclusions, not methodology - Tie everything to business outcomes - Make recommendations specific and actionable - Demonstrate value delivered

Quick Professional Prompts

Follow-Up Email

"Write a follow-up email after my [meeting/call] with [prospect/client]. Key points discussed: [X]. Next steps: [Y]. Keep it professional and move things forward."

Scope Creep Response

"Help me respond to a client who's asking for [work outside our agreement]. I want to be helpful but protect my boundaries. Options: charge more, push back politely, etc."

Testimonial Request

"Write a message asking [happy client] for a testimonial. Make it easy for them by including specific questions or prompts they can respond to."

Rate Increase Script

"Help me communicate a rate increase from [X] to [Y] to my existing clients. I want to maintain relationships while valuing my worth."

Bio/Intro Variations

"Write 3 versions of my professional bio: 1) Formal for publications/conferences, 2) Conversational for podcasts/interviews, 3) Brief for social media."

Meeting Recap

"Write a professional recap email for my meeting with [client]. Covered: [topics]. Decisions: [X]. Action items: [Y]. Next meeting: [Z]."

Professional Service Principles

AI for the Expert
  • Your judgment is the product. AI helps communicate your expertise; it doesn't replace it.
  • Quality over speed. Use AI to work faster, but don't sacrifice the thinking that clients pay for.
  • Confidentiality matters. Never put client-specific information into AI without proper safeguards.
  • Maintain your voice. Your perspective is your brand. AI drafts; you edit for authenticity.
  • Document your methodology. AI can help you systematize your approach—that's leverage.

Your Expertise + AI Leverage = Premium Practice

The best professionals aren't threatened by AI—they use it to amplify their reach and impact. You bring the expertise, judgment, and relationships. AI handles the writing, research, and administrative tasks that used to eat your billable hours.
