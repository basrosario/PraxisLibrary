My family are among some of the silent sufferers, my mother-in-law, sister-in-law, wife, and daughter all have a connective tissue disorder, called Ehlers-Danlos Syndrome. At its core, EDS is a connective tissue disorder. Think of connective tissue as the “glue” or the scaffolding of your body. It’s made of proteins like collagen that give structure and strength to your skin, joints, blood vessels, and organs.

In someone with EDS, the “recipe” for that glue is a bit off. Because the glue is weak or too stretchy, the body doesn’t hold itself together quite as firmly as it should. Because connective tissue is found everywhere in the body, EDS isn’t just about “bendy thumbs.” It can affect digestion, heart rate, and energy levels. It’s often called an “invisible illness” because someone might look perfectly fine on the outside while their joints are causing significant chronic pain on the inside.

When systems work well, lives improve. When they don’t, the cost is carried quietly by families who look to persevere anyway.....

The Work Behind the Purpose

I am always building where complexity is unavoidable and clarity must be earned

I’ve spent my career and still contribute to leading initiatives of all sizes, where the margin for error is small and the impact is significant. These efforts often span large populations, distributed teams, and mission-critical systems.

I am comfortable operating where complexity is unavoidable and clarity must be built.

Praxis Library is the culmination of that approach. What you see here began as an idea: that AI literacy should be accessible to everyone, not gatekept by technical jargon or paywalled resources. From concept to blueprint to launch, we designed the architecture, wrote the curriculum, built the interactive tools, and iterated through countless revisions. Each page, each methodology breakdown, each quiz question was critiqued, balanced, and refined. Working alongside Claude Code as an AI-assisted development partner, we applied the same systems thinking and disciplined execution that define my professional work. The result is a resource built with intention, tested through use, and offered freely to anyone ready to learn.

A Promise to My Daughter

I will rise before you wake,

To meet the morning sun.

The challenges that we will face,

These battles can be won.

Temptation will surround us,

The pressured days creep too.

But, as I watch you sleeping,

I know we will get through.

I know I will get weary,

As day gives way to night.

Still, while I watch you playing,

My soul finds the will to fight.

Remembering the times,

Before we brought you home.

It's hard to see in hindsight,

How much that we have grown.

So, as I rise before you wake,

And greet the new day's sun.

I know just how much it will take,

This battle has just begun.

Please, know when you are sleeping,

I'll always be there too.

Because the best parts of me,

Are also part of you.
