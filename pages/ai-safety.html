AI Safety & Ethics

Using AI responsibly means understanding both its capabilities and limitations.

Core Principles

Guidelines for responsible AI use

Verify, Don't Trust

AI can generate plausible-sounding but incorrect information. Always verify important facts, especially for decisions with real consequences. AI is a starting point, not the final word.

Understand the Limits

AI doesn't "know" things the way humans do. It predicts likely text patterns based on training data. It can be confidently wrong, outdated, or biased without realizing it.

Protect Privacy

Don't share sensitive personal information, proprietary data, or confidential business information with AI systems. Assume anything you type could be stored or used for training.

Maintain Human Oversight

AI should assist human decision-making, not replace it. Keep humans in the loop for important decisions, especially those affecting people's lives, health, or rights.

Common Risks to Understand

Hallucinations

AI can generate convincing but entirely fictional information, including fake citations, made-up statistics, and fabricated facts. This is especially common for specific details like dates, numbers, and names.

Practice spotting hallucinations

Bias & Fairness

AI systems can reflect and amplify biases present in their training data. Be aware that outputs may contain cultural, gender, racial, or other biases that require human review and correction.

Over-Reliance

It's easy to become dependent on AI for tasks you could do yourself. This can erode your own skills and critical thinking. Use AI to augment your abilities, not replace them.

Privacy Exposure

Information shared with AI systems may be stored, analyzed, or used to train future models. Never share passwords, personal identifiers, health information, or confidential business data.

Best Practices

01

Cross-Reference Important Information

For any fact that matters, verify it through reliable primary sources. If AI cites a study or statistic, look it up. If you can't find corroboration, be skeptical.

02

Review AI Outputs Before Sharing

Never publish or share AI-generated content without careful review. Check for accuracy, tone, appropriateness, and potential issues that could reflect poorly on you.

03

Be Transparent About AI Use

When appropriate, disclose that AI assisted in creating content. This is especially important in academic, professional, and journalistic contexts.

04

Keep Developing Your Own Skills

Use AI to learn and enhance your abilities, not to avoid learning. The goal is augmentation, not replacement. Your judgment and expertise remain essential.

05

Stay Updated on AI Capabilities

AI technology evolves rapidly. What was impossible yesterday may be possible today. Stay informed about new capabilities, limitations, and best practices.

When to Be Extra Careful

Medical Decisions

AI is not a doctor. Never make health decisions based solely on AI advice. Always consult qualified healthcare professionals.

Legal Matters

AI cannot provide legal advice. For legal questions, consult a licensed attorney who understands your jurisdiction and circumstances.

Financial Decisions

AI shouldn't make investment or major financial decisions for you. Consult qualified financial advisors for personalized guidance.

High-Stakes Situations

For any decision that could significantly impact people's lives, safety, or wellbeing, human judgment and expertise must remain central.
