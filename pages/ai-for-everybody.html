Our Philosophy

Democratizing AI knowledge for everyone

No Prerequisites Required

You don't need a computer science degree or technical background. The U.S. Department of Education emphasizes that AI literacy should be accessible to all learners, not just those in technical fields[3]. Praxis starts from first principles.

Free and Open

All content is completely free. No paywalls, no premium tiers, no email gates. UNESCO recommends that AI education resources be openly accessible to reduce digital divides[2].

Multiple Learning Paths

Visual learners explore interactive cards. Readers dive into explanations. Practical learners use hands-on tools. This aligns with Universal Design for Learning (UDL) principles from CAST.org[4].

Real-World Focus

Every concept is grounded in practical application. Whether you're a student, professional, creative, or curious explorer—you'll find relevant examples that connect to your world.

Breaking Down Barriers

Addressing common obstacles to AI adoption

Pew Research found that 52% of Americans are more concerned than excited about AI in daily life[5]. Many people feel excluded from AI—they think it's "not for them" or that they're "not technical enough." We designed Praxis specifically to demolish these barriers.

"I'm Not Technical"

Reality: You don't need to be. AI tools like ChatGPT and Claude understand natural language—the same language you use every day. If you can explain what you want to a colleague, you can explain it to AI.

"It's Too Complicated"

Reality: We break everything into digestible pieces. The CRISP method has just 5 components. You can learn effective prompting in the time it takes to watch a TV episode.

"I'm Too Old"

Reality: Age is irrelevant. AI tools require wisdom and life experience to use well. Your decades of knowledge are an asset, not a limitation. Clear thinking beats technical skills.

"I'll Break Something"

Reality: You can't break AI by prompting it incorrectly. The worst that happens is an unhelpful response. Experiment freely—that's how learning happens.

"I Can't Afford It"

Reality: Praxis is 100% free. Many AI tools have free tiers. You can start learning and practicing without spending anything.

"I Don't Have Time"

Reality: Our tools are designed for busy people. The Framework Matcher takes 30 seconds. The Prompt Analyzer gives instant feedback. Learn in moments, not hours.

Our Learning Principles

How we make complex concepts accessible

Plain Language First

No jargon without explanation. Every technical term is defined in context. The glossary provides instant lookups for any unfamiliar concept.

Show, Don't Just Tell

Every concept includes concrete examples. See bad prompts transformed into good ones. Watch methodologies applied to real scenarios.

Progressive Complexity

Start simple, add depth gradually. Prompt Basics → CRISP → CRISPE → COSTAR → Advanced Techniques. Each level builds on the last.

Practice Over Theory

Interactive tools let you apply concepts immediately. Analyze your own prompts, build new ones, match methods to your tasks.

Learn by Doing

Research from the National Academies shows that active learning strategies improve retention and understanding[6]. Use our tools to experiment, make mistakes, and improve. There's no penalty for trying—every attempt teaches you something.

Accessibility Commitment

Built for all abilities and preferences

Making AI accessible means making Praxis itself accessible. We've implemented comprehensive accommodations to ensure everyone can use this resource effectively.

Visual Accessibility

High contrast mode, adjustable text sizes (1x, 2x, 3x), screen dimming controls. WCAG AA compliant color contrast throughout.

Keyboard Navigation

Every feature accessible via keyboard. Skip links, logical tab order, visible focus indicators. No mouse required.

Screen Reader Support

Semantic HTML, ARIA labels, proper heading hierarchy. Tested with common screen readers to ensure full content access.

User Preferences

Settings persist in localStorage on your device. Your accommodations stay active across sessions without accounts or tracking.

Accessibility Panel

Click the accessibility icon (person in circle) in the bottom-right corner to access text scaling, high contrast mode, and screen dimming controls. Your preferences are saved automatically.
