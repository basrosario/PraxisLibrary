A

Accuracy

A metric measuring how often a model's predictions are correct. Calculated as the ratio of correct predictions to total predictions. While intuitive, it can be misleading for imbalanced datasets.

Metrics Evaluation

Activation Function

A mathematical function applied to neurons in neural networks that introduces non-linearity, enabling the network to learn complex patterns. Common examples include ReLU, sigmoid, and tanh.

Neural Networks Technical

Adversarial Attack

Deliberate attempts to deceive AI systems by providing specially crafted inputs. These can cause models to make incorrect predictions or generate harmful outputs.

Learn about AI safety →
Security Safety

Agent (AI Agent)

An AI system that can perceive its environment, make decisions, and take actions to achieve goals. Modern AI agents can use tools, browse the web, execute code, and interact with external systems.

Architecture Advanced

AI (Artificial Intelligence)

Computer systems designed to perform tasks that typically require human intelligence, such as understanding language, recognizing patterns, making decisions, and generating content.

AI Ethics

The study of moral principles and values that should guide the development and use of AI systems. Covers fairness, transparency, privacy, accountability, and societal impact.

Learn about AI safety →
Ethics Society

AI Readiness

The skills, knowledge, and mindset needed to use AI tools effectively and responsibly. Includes understanding both capabilities and limitations.

Explore AI Readiness tools →

Alignment

The challenge of ensuring AI systems behave in ways that match human values and intentions. A key concern in AI safety research, involving both technical and philosophical considerations.

Safety Research

Algorithm

A step-by-step procedure or set of rules for solving a problem or accomplishing a task. In AI, algorithms define how models learn from data and make predictions.

Fundamentals Computer Science

Annotation

The process of labeling data to create training datasets for supervised learning. Human annotators add labels, categories, or descriptions to raw data like text, images, or audio.

Data Training

Anthropic

An AI safety company founded in 2021 by former OpenAI researchers. Creator of the Claude family of AI assistants, focused on developing safe and beneficial AI systems.

Company LLM Provider

API (Application Programming Interface)

A set of protocols that allows different software applications to communicate. AI APIs enable developers to integrate AI capabilities into their applications without building models from scratch.

Technical Integration

Attention Mechanism

A technique that allows models to focus on relevant parts of the input when producing output. The foundation of transformer architecture, enabling models to capture long-range dependencies in text.

Architecture Transformers

AutoGen

Microsoft's framework for building multi-agent AI applications. Enables conversations between multiple AI agents that can collaborate, debate, and solve complex problems together.

Framework Application

Autoencoder

A neural network that learns to compress data into a smaller representation and then reconstruct it. Used for dimensionality reduction, denoising, and learning efficient data representations.

Architecture Unsupervised

Autoregressive Model

A model that generates output one element at a time, using previous outputs to predict the next. Most LLMs are autoregressive, generating text token by token from left to right.

Architecture LLM

Ablation Study

A research technique that removes components of a model to understand their contribution. Helps researchers understand which parts of a system are responsible for its capabilities.

Research Methodology

Activation Function

A mathematical function applied to neuron outputs that introduces non-linearity. Common activations include ReLU, GELU, and sigmoid. Essential for learning complex patterns.

Architecture Neural Networks

Active Learning

A training approach where the model identifies which unlabeled examples would be most valuable to learn from. Reduces labeling costs by focusing human effort where it matters most.

Training Technique

Adam Optimizer

A popular optimization algorithm combining momentum with adaptive learning rates. The default choice for training many neural networks due to good performance across tasks.

Training Algorithm

Agentic AI

AI systems that can autonomously plan, reason, and take actions to accomplish goals. Includes tool use, multi-step planning, and self-correction capabilities.

Architecture Advanced

AGI (Artificial General Intelligence)

Hypothetical AI that can perform any intellectual task a human can. Unlike today's narrow AI, AGI would generalize across all domains. A long-term goal and safety concern.

Learn about AI safety →
Concept Future

AI Safety

The field focused on ensuring AI systems behave safely and beneficially. Includes technical research on alignment, governance, and preventing misuse or unintended harms.

Learn about AI safety →
Field Safety

AI Winter

Periods of reduced funding and interest in AI research following failed expectations. Notable winters occurred in the 1970s and late 1980s. The current era is considered an AI boom.

Historical Industry

Alpaca

An early instruction-tuned version of Llama created by Stanford researchers. Demonstrated that instruction-following could be achieved with synthetic data at low cost.

Model Historical

Amazon Bedrock

AWS's managed service for accessing foundation models from multiple providers. Offers Claude, Llama, and other models through a unified API with enterprise features.

Platform Cloud

Anchor (Prompting)

A reference point or example in a prompt that guides the AI's response style or format. Anchors help establish expectations for output quality and structure.

Prompting Technique

Anomaly Detection

Identifying unusual patterns or outliers in data that don't conform to expected behavior. Used in fraud detection, system monitoring, and quality control.

ML Task Application

Answer Engineering

Designing prompts to elicit specific response formats or structured outputs. Complements prompt engineering by focusing on how answers should be structured.

Prompting Technique

Architecture Search (NAS)

Automated methods for discovering optimal neural network architectures. Can find better designs than human-created networks but requires significant computational resources.

Research Optimization

Artificial Neuron

The basic computational unit in neural networks, loosely inspired by biological neurons. Computes a weighted sum of inputs, applies an activation function, and outputs the result.

Architecture Fundamentals

Assistant Message

In chat APIs, the AI's response in a conversation. Combined with system and user messages to form the complete conversation context for generating the next response.

API Technical

Asynchronous Generation

Running multiple AI inference requests in parallel rather than waiting for each to complete. Improves throughput for applications handling many concurrent users.

Technical Production

Audio Generation

AI that creates speech, music, or sound effects from text or other inputs. Includes text-to-speech (TTS), music generation, and sound design applications.

Application Generative

Augmentation

Expanding training data by creating modified versions of existing examples. In text: paraphrasing, back-translation. In images: rotation, cropping, color changes.

Training Data

Auto-Complete

AI feature that predicts and suggests text as you type. Powers writing assistants, code completion, and search suggestions. Based on language model predictions.

Application Feature

AutoML

Automated machine learning tools that handle model selection, hyperparameter tuning, and feature engineering. Makes ML accessible to non-experts and speeds up development.

Tools Automation

Auxiliary Loss

Additional loss terms added during training to help learning. Can improve training stability, add regularization, or encourage specific behaviors in the model.

Training Advanced

Average Pooling

A technique that reduces data dimensionality by computing the average of regions. Used in CNNs and for creating fixed-size representations from variable-length sequences.

Architecture Technique

AWS SageMaker

Amazon's ML platform for building, training, and deploying models. Provides infrastructure, tools, and pre-built algorithms for the complete ML lifecycle.

Platform Cloud

Azure OpenAI Service

Microsoft's enterprise offering of OpenAI models through Azure cloud. Provides GPT-4, ChatGPT, and DALL-E with enterprise security, compliance, and regional availability.

Platform Cloud

B

Backpropagation

The fundamental algorithm for training neural networks. It calculates how much each weight contributed to the error and adjusts weights accordingly, propagating the error signal backward through the network.

Training Neural Networks

Batch

A subset of training data processed together in one iteration. Batch processing improves training efficiency and stability compared to processing one example at a time.

Training Technical

Batch Size

The number of training examples processed together before updating model weights. Larger batches provide more stable gradients but require more memory; smaller batches train faster but with more noise.

Hyperparameter Training

Beam Search

A search algorithm used in text generation that maintains multiple candidate sequences at each step, selecting the most promising ones. Balances quality and computational cost compared to exhaustive search.

Generation Algorithm

Benchmark

A standardized test or dataset used to evaluate and compare AI model performance. Common LLM benchmarks include MMLU, HellaSwag, and HumanEval for measuring different capabilities.

Evaluation Research

BERT (Bidirectional Encoder Representations from Transformers)

A influential language model from Google (2018) that processes text bidirectionally, understanding context from both left and right. Revolutionized NLP and inspired many subsequent models.

Model Architecture

Bias

Systematic errors or unfair preferences in AI outputs that reflect biases in training data or system design. Can affect fairness across different groups or perspectives.

Learn about AI safety →
Ethics Fairness

Black Box

A system whose internal workings are not visible or understandable to users. Many AI models are considered black boxes because their decision-making processes are difficult to interpret.

Interpretability Concept

BPE (Byte Pair Encoding)

A tokenization algorithm that breaks text into subword units. Starts with individual characters and iteratively merges frequent pairs, balancing vocabulary size with the ability to handle rare words.

Tokenization NLP

Baichuan

A series of Chinese bilingual LLMs known for strong performance in Chinese language tasks. Part of the growing ecosystem of non-Western foundation models.

Model Chinese AI

Base Model

A pre-trained model before fine-tuning for specific tasks. Base models are good at text completion but need instruction tuning to become helpful assistants.

Model Type Training

bfloat16

A 16-bit floating-point format optimized for neural network training. Sacrifices precision for range compared to float16, offering better training stability.

Technical Precision

Bigram / N-gram

Sequences of N consecutive tokens used in language modeling. Bigrams are pairs; trigrams are triples. N-gram models were dominant before neural approaches but remain useful baselines.

NLP Historical

Binary Classification

A classification task with exactly two possible outcomes (yes/no, spam/not spam). The simplest classification problem, often a building block for more complex tasks.

ML Task Classification

BLEU Score

A metric for evaluating machine translation quality by comparing n-gram overlap with reference translations. While imperfect, it remains widely used for automated translation evaluation.

Metrics Evaluation

Boosting

An ensemble technique that trains models sequentially, with each new model focusing on examples the previous ones got wrong. Powers XGBoost and LightGBM, popular for tabular data.

Technique ML

Bag of Words

A simple text representation that counts word occurrences, ignoring order and grammar. Despite its simplicity, still useful for some classification tasks and as a baseline.

NLP Representation

Bagging (Bootstrap Aggregating)

Training multiple models on random subsets of data and averaging their predictions. Reduces variance and overfitting. The basis for Random Forest algorithms.

Technique Ensemble

Bandwidth (AI Context)

The rate at which data can be transferred, crucial for AI infrastructure. Memory bandwidth often limits GPU performance; network bandwidth affects distributed training.

Infrastructure Performance

Bard

Google's conversational AI product, later renamed to Gemini. Competed with ChatGPT using Google's LLM technology and integration with Google services.

Product Historical

Baseline

A simple model or approach used as a reference point for comparison. New methods should outperform baselines to demonstrate value. Common baselines include random guessing or simple rules.

Evaluation Research

Bayesian Methods

Statistical approaches that incorporate prior knowledge and update beliefs based on evidence. Used for uncertainty quantification, hyperparameter optimization, and probabilistic modeling.

Statistics Theory

Behavior Cloning

Learning to imitate expert behavior from demonstrations. The model learns to map observations to actions by copying what experts do in similar situations.

Training Imitation

Bidirectional

Processing sequences in both directions (left-to-right and right-to-left). BERT processes bidirectionally for understanding; GPT processes unidirectionally for generation.

Architecture Processing

Bing Chat / Copilot

Microsoft's AI-powered search assistant, integrating GPT-4 with web search. Can answer questions with citations, create content, and access current information.

Product Microsoft

Bit Precision

The number of bits used to represent model weights and activations. Lower precision (8-bit, 4-bit) reduces memory and increases speed but may affect accuracy.

Technical Optimization

BLOOM

A large multilingual open-source language model created by BigScience, trained on 46 languages. Demonstrated the viability of collaborative, open AI development.

Model Open Source

Boolean Retrieval

Search using AND, OR, NOT operators to combine terms. Simple but limited compared to semantic search. Still used in specialized databases and advanced search interfaces.

Search Traditional

Bottleneck

A narrow layer in a neural network that forces compression of information. Used in autoencoders and some architectures to learn efficient representations.

Architecture Design

Brain-Computer Interface (BCI)

Technology connecting brain signals directly to computers. AI helps interpret neural signals for prosthetics, communication devices, and research applications.

Application Neuroscience

Browse Mode

AI capability to access and retrieve current web information during conversations. Addresses knowledge cutoff limitations by fetching real-time data.

Feature Capability

Buffer (Memory)

Temporary storage for data being processed. In AI agents, conversation buffers store recent exchanges. In training, data buffers optimize GPU utilization.

Technical Architecture

Burst (API)

Short periods of high API usage that may exceed normal rate limits. Many providers allow bursting with gradual throttling rather than hard cutoffs.

API Usage

C

Catastrophic Forgetting

The tendency of neural networks to forget previously learned information when trained on new data. A significant challenge in continual learning and fine-tuning scenarios.

Training Challenge

Chain-of-Thought (CoT)

A prompting technique that encourages AI to show its reasoning process step-by-step, leading to more accurate and transparent responses for complex problems.

An API endpoint type where the model generates responses in a conversational format. Takes a list of messages (system, user, assistant) and returns the next assistant message.

API Technical

ChatGPT

OpenAI's conversational AI product launched in November 2022. Built on GPT models fine-tuned for dialogue, it popularized conversational AI and sparked widespread public interest in LLMs.

An AI assistant created by Anthropic, designed to be helpful, harmless, and honest. Known for nuanced reasoning, long context handling, and strong performance on complex tasks.

Product Anthropic

Clustering

An unsupervised learning technique that groups similar data points together without predefined labels. Used for customer segmentation, document organization, and pattern discovery.

ML Task Unsupervised

CNN (Convolutional Neural Network)

A neural network architecture designed for processing grid-like data such as images. Uses convolutional layers to automatically learn spatial hierarchies of features.

Architecture Computer Vision

Code Generation

The ability of AI models to write programming code from natural language descriptions. Powers tools like GitHub Copilot, Cursor, and code-focused features in general LLMs.

Application Development

Cohere

An enterprise AI company providing LLMs for text generation, embeddings, and search. Known for Command models and focus on enterprise use cases with strong RAG capabilities.

Computer Vision

The field of AI that enables machines to interpret and understand visual information from images and videos. Applications include object detection, facial recognition, and medical imaging.

Field Images

Constitutional AI

Anthropic's approach to AI alignment where models are trained to follow a set of principles ("constitution") that guide their behavior. Reduces reliance on human feedback for safety training.

Context

Background information provided to AI that helps it understand your situation and needs. Essential for getting relevant, accurate responses.

Microsoft's AI assistant integrated into their products. Originally focused on code completion (GitHub Copilot), now extended to general assistance across Microsoft 365.

Cross-Attention

An attention mechanism where queries come from one sequence and keys/values from another. Essential in encoder-decoder models and multimodal systems that combine different types of input.

An AI-powered code editor built on VS Code, designed for AI-first development. Features include AI chat, code generation, and understanding of entire codebases.

Confidence Score

A numerical value indicating how certain a model is about its prediction or output. Higher scores suggest the model is more sure, though confidence doesn't always correlate with accuracy.

Cross-Validation

A technique for evaluating model performance by splitting data into multiple subsets, training on some and testing on others. Provides more reliable estimates than single train-test splits.

CUDA

NVIDIA's parallel computing platform that enables GPUs to accelerate AI training and inference. Essential infrastructure for deep learning, allowing models to train on thousands of cores simultaneously.

Cutoff Date (Knowledge Cutoff)

The date after which an AI model has no training data. Information after this date is unknown to the model unless provided in the prompt or accessed via tools.

Callback

A function called at specific points during training or inference. Used for logging, checkpointing, early stopping, and custom behaviors in ML pipelines.

Capability (AI)

A specific skill or function an AI system can perform. Capabilities range from basic (text generation) to advanced (multi-step reasoning, tool use). Understanding capabilities helps set realistic expectations.

Concept Assessment

Captioning

Generating text descriptions of images or videos. A multimodal task requiring visual understanding and language generation. Used for accessibility and content organization.

Task Multimodal

Causal Language Model

A model that predicts the next token based only on previous tokens (left-to-right). GPT and most text generation models are causal. Contrast with bidirectional models like BERT.

Architecture LLM

Ceiling Effect

When a benchmark becomes too easy to distinguish between models, all scoring near the maximum. Prompts creation of harder benchmarks to continue measuring progress.

Evaluation Benchmark

Character-Level Model

Models that process text character by character rather than using tokens. More flexible with novel words but typically slower and requiring more parameters for the same capability.

Architecture Alternative

Checkpoint

A saved snapshot of model weights during training. Enables resuming training after interruption, comparing different training stages, and selecting the best performing version.

Training Technical

Chinchilla

A DeepMind model and scaling study showing optimal training requires more data than previously thought. Influenced subsequent model development toward larger datasets.

Research Scaling

Chunking

Splitting long documents into smaller pieces for processing. Essential for RAG and embedding systems where input length exceeds model limits or affects retrieval quality.

Technique Processing

Using clear, unambiguous language in prompts to reduce misinterpretation. Specific instructions and explicit requirements improve response quality.

When training data has unequal representation across categories. Can cause models to favor majority classes. Addressed through sampling, weighting, or specialized techniques.

Cognitive Load (Prompting)

The mental effort required to process complex prompts. Simpler, well-organized prompts often yield better results by reducing the model's processing burden.

Cold Start Problem

Difficulty making predictions for new users or items with no historical data. Common in recommendation systems. Addressed with hybrid approaches combining collaborative and content-based methods.

Challenge Recommendations

Collaborative Filtering

Recommendation technique based on user behavior patterns. "Users who liked X also liked Y." Forms the basis of many recommendation systems at Netflix, Amazon, etc.

A massive open repository of web data used to train many LLMs. Contains petabytes of text crawled from the internet, requiring careful filtering for quality and safety.

Data Training

Commonsense Reasoning

AI's ability to understand everyday knowledge humans take for granted. That water is wet, objects fall down, people need sleep. A challenging area where LLMs have improved dramatically.

Capability Reasoning

Completion

Text generated by an AI to continue a given prompt. The basic operation of language models: given input text, predict what comes next.

Task Fundamentals

Compression (Model)

Reducing model size while maintaining performance. Techniques include quantization, pruning, and distillation. Enables deployment on edge devices and reduces costs.

Optimization Deployment

Compute

Computational resources required for training and running AI models. Measured in FLOPs, GPU-hours, or dollars. A primary constraint and cost driver in AI development.

Infrastructure Resources

Concept Drift

When the relationship between input and output changes over time, causing model performance to degrade. Requires monitoring and retraining to maintain accuracy.

Challenge Production

Conditional Generation

Generating content based on specific conditions or inputs. Image generation conditioned on text, or text generation conditioned on a topic or style.

Technique Generation

Another term for hallucination—when AI generates plausible but false information. The model "fills in gaps" with invented content that sounds convincing.

Confusion Matrix

A table showing correct and incorrect predictions for each class. Reveals where a classification model makes mistakes, enabling targeted improvements.

Limitations or requirements specified in a prompt. "Respond in 50 words or less" or "Use only formal language." Constraints shape and focus AI output.

Continual Learning

Training models incrementally on new data without forgetting previous knowledge. A challenge because neural networks tend to overwrite old information with new.

Training Research

Contrastive Learning

Training by comparing similar and dissimilar examples. The model learns to place similar items close together in embedding space and dissimilar items far apart.

Training Technique

Controllable Generation

Techniques for steering AI output toward desired attributes like sentiment, style, or topic. Enables more precise control over generated content.

Technique Generation

Conversation History

The record of previous messages in a chat session. Provides context for AI responses. Managing history is important as it consumes context window space.

Feature Context

Convolution

A mathematical operation that slides a filter over input to detect patterns. The core of CNNs, effective for images by learning local features like edges and textures.

Operation Architecture

Coreference Resolution

Determining when different expressions refer to the same entity. "John said he was tired" - understanding "he" refers to "John." Essential for text understanding.

NLP Task Understanding

Cost Function

Another name for loss function - the metric being minimized during training. Different tasks use different cost functions: cross-entropy for classification, MSE for regression.

Training Math

Counterfactual

"What if" reasoning about alternative scenarios. Used in explainability ("the prediction would change if...") and for evaluating causal relationships in data.

Concept Reasoning

CPU Inference

Running AI models on CPUs rather than GPUs. Slower but more accessible. Quantized models can run efficiently on CPUs for edge deployment.

Deployment Hardware

Crowdsourcing

Gathering data labels or human feedback from many workers. Platforms like Amazon MTurk provide annotations for training data and RLHF preference collection.

Data Process

Curriculum Learning

Training models on progressively harder examples, mimicking human education. Can improve learning efficiency and final performance compared to random ordering.

Training Technique

Custom Instructions

Persistent preferences that shape all AI responses in ChatGPT and similar products. Set once and applied automatically to every conversation.

D

DALL-E

OpenAI's image generation model that creates images from text descriptions. Named as a portmanteau of "Dalí" (the artist) and "WALL-E" (the robot), it pioneered text-to-image AI.

Model Image Generation

Data Augmentation

Techniques to artificially expand training datasets by creating modified versions of existing data. For images: rotation, flipping, cropping. For text: paraphrasing, back-translation.

Training Data

Dataset

A collection of data used for training, validating, or testing AI models. Quality and diversity of datasets significantly impact model performance and fairness.

Data Fundamentals

Decoder

The component of a neural network that generates output from encoded representations. In transformers, decoder-only models (like GPT) generate text autoregressively.

Architecture Transformers

Deep Learning

A subset of machine learning using neural networks with many layers ("deep" networks). Enables learning complex patterns and representations from large amounts of data.

Field Neural Networks

DeepMind

Google's AI research lab known for breakthroughs like AlphaGo, AlphaFold, and Gemini. Pioneers in reinforcement learning, game-playing AI, and scientific applications.

Company Research

DeepSeek

A Chinese AI company known for efficient, high-performing open models. Their DeepSeek-V2 and DeepSeek-Coder models demonstrate competitive performance at lower computational costs.

Company Model

Deepfake

AI-generated synthetic media where a person's likeness is replaced or manipulated. Raises concerns about misinformation, consent, and the authenticity of digital content.

Learn about AI safety →
Risk Ethics

Delimiter

Characters or symbols used in prompts to clearly separate different sections or types of content. Examples include triple backticks (```), XML tags, or custom markers like ###.

Prompting Technique

Diffusion Model

A generative AI architecture that creates content by gradually removing noise from random data. Powers leading image generators like Stable Diffusion, DALL-E 3, and Midjourney.

Architecture Generative

Dimensionality Reduction

Techniques to reduce the number of features in data while preserving important information. Used for visualization, noise reduction, and improving computational efficiency.

Technique Data Processing

Distillation (Knowledge Distillation)

A technique to transfer knowledge from a large "teacher" model to a smaller "student" model. Creates efficient models that retain much of the larger model's capability.

Training Optimization

DPO (Direct Preference Optimization)

A simpler alternative to RLHF for aligning language models. Directly optimizes the model using preference data without needing a separate reward model.

Training Alignment

Dropout

A regularization technique that randomly deactivates neurons during training. Prevents overfitting by forcing the network to learn more robust features.

Training Regularization

Data Privacy (AI)

Concerns and practices around protecting personal information when using AI systems. Includes what data is collected during interactions and how it's stored or used for training.

Learn about AI safety →
Ethics Safety

Data Leakage

When information from outside the training set improperly influences the model, leading to overly optimistic performance estimates. A common mistake in ML pipelines that causes models to fail in production.

Training Pitfall

Decision Boundary

The line or surface that separates different classes in a classification model. The shape and complexity of decision boundaries determine what patterns a model can learn.

ML Concept Classification

Deterministic vs Stochastic

Deterministic systems produce the same output for the same input every time. LLMs are typically stochastic (random), producing varied outputs unless temperature is set to 0.

Concept LLM

Dialogue System

An AI system designed to converse with humans in natural language. Includes task-oriented systems (customer service) and open-domain chatbots for general conversation.

Application NLP

Distribution Shift

When the data a model encounters in production differs from its training data. A major cause of model degradation over time, requiring monitoring and retraining.

Challenge Production

Document Q&A

Using AI to answer questions about specific documents or text. Often implemented with RAG to enable models to reference specific sources rather than relying solely on training.

Application RAG

Domain Adaptation

Techniques for adapting a model trained on one domain (e.g., general text) to perform well on another domain (e.g., medical or legal text) with limited target domain data.

Training Transfer Learning

E

Embedding

A dense vector representation of data (words, sentences, images) in a continuous space. Similar items have similar embeddings, enabling semantic search and comparison.

Representation NLP

Emergent Abilities

Capabilities that appear in large AI models that weren't present in smaller versions. Examples include complex reasoning, code generation, and following nuanced instructions.

Phenomenon Scaling

Encoder

A neural network component that transforms input into a compressed representation. In transformers, encoder models (like BERT) process the entire input at once for understanding tasks.

Architecture Transformers

Endpoint

A specific URL where an API can be accessed. AI services expose endpoints for different functions like chat completions, embeddings, and image generation.

API Technical

Epoch

One complete pass through the entire training dataset. Models typically train for multiple epochs, with each pass allowing weights to be refined based on all available data.

Training Technical

Evaluation

The process of measuring model performance using metrics, benchmarks, and human assessment. Critical for comparing models and ensuring they meet quality standards.

System Prompt

Prompting Configuration

Safety Filter

Systems that detect and block harmful content in AI inputs or outputs. Part of the safety stack protecting users from inappropriate, dangerous, or illegal content.

Scale AI

A company specializing in data labeling and annotation services for AI training. Provides human feedback at scale, crucial for training and evaluating foundation models.

Company Data

Scikit-learn

A popular Python library for traditional machine learning algorithms. Provides simple APIs for classification, regression, clustering, and preprocessing. The go-to for non-deep-learning ML.

Framework ML

Sentence Transformer

Models that encode entire sentences into single vectors, capturing semantic meaning. Popular for semantic search, similarity matching, and clustering text at the sentence level.

Model Type Embeddings

Speculative Decoding

A technique to speed up LLM inference by using a smaller model to draft tokens that the larger model verifies in parallel. Maintains output quality while reducing latency.

Optimization Inference

Stop Sequence

Text patterns that signal when AI should stop generating. Useful for controlling output length and format, preventing the model from continuing beyond the intended response.

Parameter Generation

Structured Output

AI responses formatted as data structures (JSON, XML) rather than prose. Enables reliable parsing for applications and integrations. Many APIs support structured output modes.

Feature Integration

T

Temperature

A parameter controlling randomness in AI outputs. Temperature 0 gives deterministic responses; higher values (0.7-1.0) increase creativity and variety; very high values may produce incoherence.

Parameter Generation

Text Generation

The AI task of producing human-like text from prompts. Encompasses creative writing, code generation, summarization, and conversational responses.

Task Application

Throughput

The rate at which a system processes requests, often measured in tokens per second. A key performance metric for AI serving infrastructure.

Performance Metrics

Token

The basic unit AI uses to process text. Roughly 4 characters or 0.75 words in English. Context windows, pricing, and rate limits are measured in tokens.

Core Concept Fundamentals

Tokenization

The process of breaking text into tokens for model processing. Different tokenizers (BPE, SentencePiece) produce different token sequences from the same text.

Process NLP

Top-K Sampling

A generation strategy that limits token selection to the K most probable options. Higher K allows more variety; lower K produces more focused output.

Parameter Generation

Top-P (Nucleus Sampling)

A generation strategy that considers tokens until their cumulative probability reaches P. Dynamically adjusts the candidate pool based on the probability distribution.

Parameter Generation

Training

The process of teaching a model by exposing it to data and adjusting its parameters to minimize prediction errors. Requires significant computational resources for large models.

Process Fundamentals

Training Data

The content used to teach AI models. Quality, diversity, and scope of training data significantly affect model capabilities, knowledge, and potential biases.

Data Fundamentals

Transfer Learning

Using knowledge learned from one task to improve performance on another. Foundation models are trained generally, then transfer their capabilities to specific tasks through fine-tuning.

Technique Training

Transformer

The revolutionary neural network architecture (2017) powering modern AI. Uses self-attention to process sequences in parallel, enabling training on massive datasets.

Architecture Foundational

Tree-of-Thought

A prompting technique that explores multiple reasoning paths simultaneously, evaluating and selecting the most promising branches. Extends chain-of-thought with deliberate exploration.

TensorFlow

Google's open-source deep learning framework, widely used for production ML systems. Known for deployment tools and TPU support, though PyTorch has gained research share.

Framework Deep Learning

Test Set

Data held back from training to evaluate final model performance. Unlike validation sets used during training, test sets should only be used once to avoid data leakage.

Data Evaluation

Text-to-Speech (TTS)

AI that converts written text into natural-sounding speech. Modern TTS models like ElevenLabs produce highly realistic voices with emotion and intonation.

Application Audio

Tool Use

AI's ability to call external functions, search the web, run code, or access APIs. Enables AI agents to take actions beyond text generation, expanding capabilities significantly.

Capability Agents

TPU (Tensor Processing Unit)

Google's custom AI accelerator chips designed specifically for neural network computations. Used to train many of Google's largest models including Gemini.

Hardware Infrastructure

Truncation

Cutting off input that exceeds a model's context window. Can occur at the start (losing context) or end (losing instructions). Requires careful prompt design for long inputs.

Limitation Technical

Turing Test

A test proposed by Alan Turing where a human judge tries to distinguish between human and AI responses. While historically important, modern LLMs have made it less useful as a capability measure.

Historical Evaluation

U

Underfitting

When a model is too simple to capture patterns in the data, performing poorly on both training and test data. Addressed by increasing model capacity or training longer.

Problem Training

Unsupervised Learning

Machine learning from unlabeled data, discovering patterns without explicit guidance. Includes clustering, dimensionality reduction, and self-supervised pre-training of LLMs.

Learning Type Fundamentals

User Prompt

The input provided by the user in a conversation, as opposed to the system prompt set by developers. Together with system prompts, they form the complete context for AI responses.

Prompting Concept

Upsampling

Increasing resolution or quantity of data. In image AI, upscaling low-res images. In training, duplicating underrepresented examples to balance datasets.

Technique Data

Use Case

A specific application or scenario where AI provides value. Understanding your use case helps choose the right model, prompting strategy, and safety measures.

Concept Application

Utility Function

A mathematical function that measures how "good" an outcome is. In AI alignment, designing utility functions that capture human values is a fundamental challenge.

Concept Alignment

V

Validation

Testing model performance on data not used in training to assess generalization. Validation sets help tune hyperparameters; test sets provide final performance estimates.

Process Evaluation

Vector

An ordered list of numbers representing data in a mathematical space. Embeddings are vectors; vector similarity measures (cosine similarity) enable semantic search and comparison.

Math Representation

Vector Database

A database optimized for storing and searching high-dimensional vectors (embeddings). Essential for RAG systems, semantic search, and recommendation engines. Examples: Pinecone, Weaviate, Chroma.

Infrastructure Search

Vision-Language Model (VLM)

AI models that can process and reason about both images and text. Examples include GPT-4V, Claude with vision, and Gemini. Enable image understanding, captioning, and visual question answering.

Model Type Multimodal

VAE (Variational Autoencoder)

A generative model that learns a latent space representation of data. Used in image generation and as components of larger systems like some diffusion models.

Architecture Generative

Verification (AI Outputs)

Checking AI outputs for accuracy and appropriateness before use. Essential for high-stakes applications. Can be done by humans, other AI systems, or automated checks.

Vocabulary (Model)

The set of tokens a model can recognize and generate. Determined during tokenizer training. Larger vocabularies handle more languages but increase model size.

Technical Tokenization

Voice Cloning

AI that replicates a specific person's voice from audio samples. Raises significant ethical concerns about consent and misuse for fraud or misinformation.

W

Weight

The numerical parameters in neural networks that are learned during training. Weights determine how inputs are transformed into outputs; large models have billions of weights.

Core Concept Neural Networks

Whisper

OpenAI's speech recognition model that transcribes audio to text with high accuracy across many languages. Open-sourced, enabling widespread use in transcription applications.

Model Speech

Word Embedding

Dense vector representations of words where similar words have similar vectors. Classic examples include Word2Vec and GloVe; modern LLMs use contextual embeddings that vary by context.

Representation NLP

Warmup (Learning Rate)

Gradually increasing learning rate at the start of training before decay. Helps stabilize early training when gradients might be unreliable with random weights.

Training Technique

Watermark (AI)

Hidden patterns in AI-generated content that allow detection of synthetic origins. Proposed for identifying AI text, images, and audio to combat misinformation.

Safety Detection

Weak Supervision

Training with noisy, imprecise, or automatically generated labels instead of perfect human annotations. Can dramatically reduce labeling costs while achieving good results.

Training Technique

Window Attention

A variant of attention that only looks at nearby tokens rather than the full context. Reduces computational cost for long sequences, used in models like Longformer.

Architecture Efficiency

World Model

An AI's internal representation of how the world works. Used to simulate outcomes and plan actions. A key concept in AI safety discussions about model capabilities.

X

XAI (Explainable AI)

The field focused on making AI decisions understandable to humans. Includes techniques for visualizing attention, attributing outputs to inputs, and generating explanations.

Field Transparency

XML Tags (in Prompting)

Using XML-style tags like <context> or <example> to structure prompts and clearly delineate sections. Helps models parse complex prompts more reliably.
